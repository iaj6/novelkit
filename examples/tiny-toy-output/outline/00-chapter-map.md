## The Message (Design Document — Not Prose)

The message has three parts, each predicting a specific, mundane, physical event. Its content is parceled to the reader as events confirm each line. The full text is assembled only when Maren writes it in Chapter 3.

- **Line 1 (confirmed Ch 2):** The *Morven* comes on Tuesday — one day ahead of its Wednesday schedule. The forward crate has a cracked demijohn.
- **Line 2 (confirmed Ch 2, late):** The drum slips at two in the morning. Maren resets it in twelve minutes.
- **Line 3 / trigger (confirmed Ch 3):** The barometer drops three-tenths in under an hour. When it does, she will sit down and write this.
- **Closing instruction (implicit):** The message is folded, placed in the bottle, sealed, and wedged in the basalt crack above the high-tide line at the foot of the iron stair.

The message's specific wording and the question of who "initiated" the loop are never addressed.

## Continuity Anchors

- Story set in **November 1953**.
- Lamp: third-order Fresnel lens on mercury float; clockwork drum wound every four hours.
- Bottle found the morning after a southwest gale, wedged in basalt above the high-tide line at the foot of the iron stair.
- Cal Dresh / *Morven*: fortnightly supply, calls on a Wednesday schedule; in Ch 2 he arrives a day early on Tuesday due to a weather window.
- Maren writes nothing about the bottle in the keeper's log at any point.
- No supernatural mechanism is offered or gestured toward.
