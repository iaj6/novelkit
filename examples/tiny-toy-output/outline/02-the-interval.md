# Chapter 2 — The Interval

**One-line summary:** The *Morven* arrives a day early with a cracked demijohn exactly as written; that night the drum slips at two in the morning and Maren resets it in twelve minutes; one line of the message remains unconfirmed.

---

## Logistics

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| POV | Maren Voss |
|Tense | Simple past |
| Setting | Keeper's cottage, jetty, lamp room; Harrow Point |
| Time | Tuesday through Wednesday of the following week — approximately six days after the finding |
| Target words | ~1,000 |

---

## Goal

Maren's surface goal is the same as always: keep the lamp, maintain the log, receive the supplies. Her covert goal is to watch — to see whether the first prediction arrives.

---

## Obstacle

She cannot tell whether she is watching or whether she is waiting in a way that makes the watching unnecessary. The distinction has no practical consequence: the *Morven* will come or it won't.

---

## Action Beats

### The Days Between (brief, compressed — 150–200 words)

1. **Daily routine, rendered selectively.** Three lamp-windings named but not described at length. Barometer noted: steady, then a slight rise. Maren re-folds a log entry that has no error in it. She opens the drawer, does not remove the bottle, closes the drawer.

2. **One moment of near-action:** She switches on the radio to receive the 18:00 weather bulletin. She sits with her hand near the transmit key for a moment after the bulletin ends. She does not transmit. She notes the forecast in the log.

### Cal's Arrival — Tuesday (the bulk of the scene, ~400 words)

3. **She hears the *Morven* before she sees it.** The engine note, recognizable, coming from the south on a Tuesday morning. She checks the clock: not Wednesday. She puts her mug down.

4. **Cal at the jetty.** He brings the *Morven* alongside without ceremony, ties up, begins handing crates over the rail. His first words are about the weather window: *Channel was clear, no point in waiting*. She nods. They unload together.

5. **The cracked demijohn.** The forward crate: when Maren lifts it, water seeps through the boards. She sets it down, opens the lid. One of the two fresh-water demijohns has a crack along the shoulder — the glass is intact but has been under stress, and at some point the cork has let in air and the water inside smells of the hold. She tips it. She does not say anything unusual about it.

6. **Cal's dialogue — functional and unloaded.** He talks: the crossing, a squall north of the outer reef that missed them, a boat at the mainland yard being re-keeled. He does not ask about the bottle. He does not know about the bottle. He accepts a mug of tea from Maren's pot. She gives less than she receives: *How was the passage? / Fair enough. Wind backed before we were halfway. / Aye.*

7. **What Maren does not say.** She does not mention the bottle. She does not look at the drawer. She stacks the tinned goods in the storeroom in the order she will use them. She signs the delivery receipt Cal needs for the Board.

8. **Cal goes.** *All right then.* He unties. The *Morven*'s engine turns over. She watches it until it rounds the reef and is gone.

9. **After Cal leaves.** Maren goes to the logbook. She records the supply delivery: coal (quantity), oil (quantity), tinned goods (list), fresh water — two demijohns, one cracked and discarded. She records Cal's name, the date, the time of departure. She does not record the bottle.

10. **She opens the drawer.** She takes out the bottle and sets it on the table. She does not unroll the message. She already knows what the first line says. She puts the bottle back.

### The Drum Slip — 02:00 Wednesday (closing scene, ~250 words)

11. **She wakes to the wrong sound.** Not an alarm — the absence of the right sound, and then an arrhythmic clicking from overhead. She is dressed and moving before she has fully processed it.

12. **She goes up to the lamp room.** The beam is still rotating but has slowed — the drum has lost tension, a tooth has slipped on the winding gear. The lamp has not gone out. She checks: no vessels visible in any quadrant.

13. **She resets the drum.** Locates the fault by touch and lamplight; re-seats the slipped tooth; winds the drum back to tension. She checks the rotation speed. It is correct. She notes the time: 02:14.

14. **Twelve minutes.** She does not count them consciously. She counts them now, standing in the lamp room with the beam turning steadily overhead. Finding the fault: approximately four minutes. Re-seating and re-winding: approximately six. Checking the rotation: two. Twelve minutes. The message had said twelve.

15. **She comes down.** She writes in the log: *02:14 — drum slipped, tooth re-seated, lamp on station throughout. Duration of repair approx. 12 min.* She writes the twelve. She does not write anything else.

16. **She stands at the table.** She does not open the drawer. The second prediction has arrived. The third has not.

---

## Outcome / Turn

Two of three predictions have been confirmed, precisely. The message's third line — the trigger — is still ahead. Maren now understands that the loop will close; the only remaining variable is when.

---

## New Question / Pressure Raised

What is the third line — the one that will make her sit down and write? She knows it. The reader has not yet been shown it in full. The pressure is the anticipation of the final confirmation, the knowledge that when the barometer moves, she will have no choice but to act.

---

## Continuity Notes Introduced in This Chapter

- The *Morven* arrives on a Tuesday — one day ahead of its regular Wednesday schedule — because Cal found a weather window. This matches the message's first prediction.
- The cracked demijohn is in the forward crate, water seeping through the boards. Maren discards it. Recorded in the log without comment.
- Cal's visit: supplies include coal, lamp oil, tinned goods, two demijohns of fresh water (one cracked and discarded). Delivery receipt signed.
- The drum slip occurs at approximately 02:02 Wednesday; Maren's log entry reads *02:14*, repair duration approximately 12 minutes. This matches the message's second prediction.
- Maren records the drum slip in the log but does not record the bottle or the coincidence.
- After the drum slip, only one prediction remains: the barometer drop that will trigger the writing.
