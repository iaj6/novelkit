# Glossary

**backing** — Wind direction shifting counterclockwise (e.g., from southwest to south to southeast). Maren notes this in the log as a storm indicator.

**Board of Lights** — The administrative authority that certifies lighthouse keepers, collects fortnightly logbook copies, and issues operational notices. Based on the mainland.

**Cal Dresh** — Mate and de facto captain of the supply tender *Morven*; Maren's fortnightly contact with the mainland. Age 58.

**clockwork drum** — The spring-driven mechanism Maren winds by hand every four hours to rotate the Fresnel lens assembly. Winding takes approximately three minutes. Missing a winding is a recordable event.

**Einar Voss** — Maren's late husband; lighthouse keeper at Harrow Point from 1938 until his drowning in October 1942.

**Fresnel lens** — The third-order prismatic glass lens that concentrates lamp light into a rotating beam. Seated on the mercury float.

**Harrow Point** — The small basalt island, approximately 14 nautical miles offshore, where the story is set. Treeless, exposed, approximately 2 miles long.

**Harrow Point Lighthouse** — Stone tower, built 1891, 28 meters tall, on the eastern cliff of Harrow Point.

**iron stair** — The narrow iron staircase bolted to the eastern cliff face, descending from the lighthouse grounds to the boulder shelf at the base. Where Maren descends after storms to check the cliff drain and mooring ring. Where the bottle is found.

**keeper's log** — The bound ledger in black ink Maren maintains: every lamp-winding, weather observation, and material event, dated and timed. Collected by the *Morven* and forwarded to the Board.

**Maren Voss** — Lighthouse keeper at Harrow Point. Age 46. POV character. Widowed 1942.

**mercury float** — The trough of liquid mercury in which the Fresnel lens assembly sits and rotates, reducing friction to near-zero.

**Morven, the** — The supply tender that delivers coal, lamp oil, tinned goods, fresh water, and mail to Harrow Point every two weeks.

**the bottle** — A sealed glass bottle, stoppered and wax-sealed, found by Maren in the tide-rocks at the foot of the iron stair. Contains the message. A real, physical object.

**the message** — The folded paper inside the bottle, written in Maren's own hand, describing events she has not yet lived. Its full text is not revealed until Maren writes it in Chapter 3.

**Very pistol** — Signal flare pistol kept in the cottage for maritime emergency signaling. Noted as part of the island's emergency equipment.

**cliff gate** — The iron gate at the top of the iron stair on the eastern cliff, which Maren latches when not in use. Noted in Chapter 1 and Chapter 3 outlines.

**Board correspondence paper** — Unlined writing paper Maren keeps for formal communications with the Board of Lights. The paper on which she writes the message in Chapter 3.

**sealing wax stub** — A small stick of yellowish sealing wax kept in the supply tin in the cottage. Maren melts it over the oil lamp to re-seal the bottle in Chapter 3.
