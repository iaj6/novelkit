# Glossary

Alphabetical. Expand as later phases introduce new terms.

---

**Agustín** — The Peruvian guide-interpreter employed by Riedel's party. His role mirrors Isobel's in the Holroyd expedition. His presence is a parallel that Isobel notices.

**Ahnenerbe** — The *Ahnenerbe-Forschungs- und Lehrgemeinschaft* (Ancestral Heritage Research and Teaching Society). An SS-affiliated institute founded in 1935 to sponsor research supporting the mythology of Aryan cultural origins. Funded the Riedel expedition. Real historical institution; its expeditions are characterized by ideologically pre-determined conclusions.

**Arce, Friar** — Jesuit missionary active in Peru ca. 1568–1579. Author of one of the three surviving accounts of Rititambo. Reached the outer approach to the city but not the city itself. His account contains the tree-line landmark that Riedel correctly interprets as identifying a northern approach route.

**Arriero** — A mule driver and specialist handler; a person whose professional expertise involves moving pack animals safely over mountain terrain. Mariano Quispe's trade.

**Atadito** — The standard 80-pound load unit used for Andean mule transport. A bundle wrapped in canvas or reed matting, balanced one per side on the animal. Above 14,000 feet, loads must be lightened or the mules fail.

**Aymara** — Indigenous Andean language spoken primarily around Lake Titicaca and the southern altiplano. Isobel speaks it at working competence.

**Carrow, Reverend Thomas** (deceased) — Isobel's father. Methodist missionary; based in Pisac for thirty-one years. Died of fever six weeks before the expedition departed. Believed in the authenticity of the Rititambo missionary accounts. His belief is part of Isobel's inheritance and part of her reason for being on this expedition.

**Cleto** — One of two additional arrieros hired by Mariano from the Yanatile valley for the lower approach. Turns back at the snowline with the pack mules.

**Cordillera Vilcabamba** — A mountain range in southeastern Peru, running northwest to southeast east of Cusco, draining eastward toward the Amazon basin. Contains some of the least-surveyed terrain in the eastern Andes. The setting of the expedition's upper stages.

**Fischer** — One of the two German mountaineers in Riedel's party. Secondary character.

**Finch, Dr. Aubrey** — Secretary of the Royal Geographical Society (London). Holroyd's off-the-books employer. Holroyd was fourth on his list of four candidates. Finch is not in the field; he is waiting in London for a cable.

**Holroyd, James** — See *Characters*.

**Ichu** — Tussock grass (*Stipa ichu*), the dominant plant of the high Andean puna above approximately 12,000 feet. Wind-dried and tough; grows in dense clumps. Mules will eat it when better fodder is exhausted.

**Indian Survey** — The Survey of India; the British colonial mapping agency. Holroyd served it from 1918 to 1931, when he was blacklisted. His published work from this period is considered excellent.

**Kern** — One of the two German mountaineers in Riedel's party. Secondary character.

**Leica IIIa** — Holroyd's camera. 35mm rangefinder camera, c. 1935, fitted with a 50mm Elmar f/3.5 lens. Carried against his body inside his outer jacket to prevent the mechanism from freezing. His documentation of Rititambo — if he chooses to use it — will be in 35mm black-and-white.

**Lee face / East face** — The eastern face of SM-7, sheltered from the prevailing westerly Andean weather. Faces the Amazon basin. Accumulates wind-deposited snow rather than ablating; almost never thaws at elevation. Location of the bowl containing Rititambo.

**Manila hemp** — The rope material used by both expeditions. Derived from *Abacá* (Musa textilis) fiber. Strong relative to weight; vulnerable to abrasion and moisture absorption over time. Standard mountaineering and load-bearing rope in 1938. Nylon rope does not yet exist.

**Nanga Parbat** — referenced in passing as the type of Himalayan mountaineering Riedel has background in. His specific expedition was a 1934 Karakoram attempt (fictional).

**Pisac** — A town in the Sacred Valley of the Incas (Urubamba River valley), Peru, at approximately 9,700 feet. Reverend Thomas Carrow's posting; where he died. Isobel spent much of her childhood here.

**Primus stove** — Pressurized paraffin (kerosene) camping stove. The standard expedition cooking and heating device in the 1930s. Requires hand-pumping to pressurize; at altitude, burns reluctant and takes longer to heat water, which never reaches a full rolling boil.

**Puna** — High Andean plateau grassland, approximately 12,000–16,000 feet elevation. Wind-exposed, frost-prone year-round, sparsely inhabited by herders. Above the cloud forest; below the permanent snowline.

**Quispe, Mariano** — See *Characters*.

**Quechua** — The primary indigenous language of the Andean highlands; the administrative language of the Inca empire. Isobel speaks it fluently. Mariano speaks it as his first language. The name *Rititambo* is Quechua.

**Riedel, Klaus** — See *Characters*.

**Rititambo** — The expedition's objective. A pre-Inca city on the east face of SM-7, in a snow-filled natural bowl below the summit ice. Named in three independent Spanish missionary accounts of the 1570s; in no surviving document since. The name is a Quechua transcription recorded by friar Arce: approximately "snow waystation" (*riti* = snow; *tambo* = waystation or lodge). Older than the Inca; ignored or avoided by the Inca. Pre-Inca ancestors of Mariano Quispe's valley community are said, in oral tradition, to have withdrawn to it. Stone construction, preserved by cold and altitude. No supernatural properties.

**Royal Geographical Society (RGS)** — British learned society and expedition sponsor; founded 1830. Holroyd's engagement with the RGS is off-the-books. Contact and signatory: Dr. Aubrey Finch, Secretary.

**Rufino** — One of two additional arrieros hired by Mariano from the Yanatile valley. Turns back at the snowline with the pack mules.

**Selva alta** — The high jungle or cloud forest zone on the eastern Andean slopes. Dense vegetation, high humidity, difficult trail conditions; gives way to puna above approximately 12,000 feet.

**SM-7** — *Survey Mark 7.* Holroyd's field designation for the unnamed peak containing Rititambo, assigned during his 1934 Peruvian government contract surveys of the eastern Cordillera. Approximately 19,000 feet. No official name on any published map.

**Tricouni nails** — Serrated steel clinch nails fitted to the welt of leather mountaineering boots for traction. Standard equipment in the 1930s. Grip on wet rock; less reliable on hard ice; destroy the feet on extended talus sections.

**Yanatile valley** — The valley from which the expedition approaches the eastern face of SM-7. Mariano Quispe's home valley. His community's oral tradition holds knowledge of Rititambo.

**Yagua** — An Amazonian indigenous language spoken in the Loreto region of Peru. Isobel speaks broken Yagua — enough to establish contact in the far east, not enough for precision.

**Chavin de Huántar** — A major pre-Inca archaeological site in the Peruvian highlands, approximately 900 BCE–200 BCE, predating the Inca by more than a millennium. Its architecture uses trapezoidal doorways and a scale larger than Inca convention. Isobel cites it as a comparison for Rititambo's architecture during the confrontation with Riedel.

**Tiahuanaco (Tiwanaku)** — A pre-Inca archaeological site near Lake Titicaca (modern Bolivia), flourishing approximately 500–1000 CE. Major Andean civilization predating the Inca. Isobel cites it alongside Chavin de Huántar as evidence for Rititambo's indigenous Andean origin.

**Rolleiflex** — A twin-lens reflex medium-format camera used by Riedel during the expedition. Larger and heavier than Holroyd's Leica IIIa; produces larger-format negatives. Riedel uses it to document Rititambo during the confrontation chapter.

**Kodak Super-X panchromatic** — 35mm black-and-white film stock used by Holroyd in his Leica IIIa. Loaded in 36-exposure cassettes. Kept against Holroyd's body inside his jacket to prevent the mechanism and film from freezing at altitude.

**Muña** — A high-altitude Andean herb (Minthostachys mollis) used to make a thin mint tea. The herder woman on the puna offers it to the party. Common in Quechua-speaking highland communities.

**Pampa Chica junction** — A trail junction in the Yanatile valley where Holroyd's original southeast approach to SM-7 branched. Mariano's corrected north-northeast route does not use this junction. Cleto is given instructions for a route from this area in case the main pass is closed on the return descent.

**Verglas** — A thin coating of ice formed by refreezing on rock faces. Present on the rock step below the bowl passage. Requires crampons and face-in technique for safe descent. The face that turned Mariano back alone in 1933.

**Witness (in Mariano's tradition)** — In the oral tradition of Mariano Quispe's valley community, a witness is not a reporter or documentarian but someone who stands in a fact and comes back changed — and the change is the testimony. The act does not produce a document; it produces a person who has been somewhere and looked, and who can say what was there to the people who need to know it. Mariano's role on the expedition is to be this witness for Rititambo on behalf of his community.
