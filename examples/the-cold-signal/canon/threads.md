# Threads

*Five threads for* The Corrections*. Three to seven is the range; five is the right number for this book. Each thread is a question the reader tracks across multiple chapters — distinct from the spine question ("what will Elena do?") and from the thematic concerns in* `canon/themes.md`*. The weave check at the bottom confirms no chapter goes unadvanced.*

---

## Thread 1: The Daily Schedule

- **Question**: Will Elena's sustained omissions in the daily radio calls with Varga fracture their eighteen-year friendship before she can correct it?
- **Enters**: Ch 01 — The signal arrives after the 1800 window; by not calling at 0800 Day 2, Elena makes her first choice not to disclose. The opportunity is already deferred before she has formed the decision consciously.
- **Advanced in**:
  - Ch 02 — First active omission: "no unusual events" at 0800 (technically true; the least complete truth she can offer).
  - Ch 03 — Second omission: "overnight run was clean" (also true; the screening is becoming habitual).
  - Ch 04 — Two calls, both deflected. "No traffic pending" at 1800 — true; also a screen.
  - Ch 05 — Varga casually mentions a Parkes preprint citing her methodology; her voice does something small in response. The conversation becomes the first one where something in the channel changes, however slightly.
  - Ch 06 — After the 1800 call Elena registers, briefly and without examining it, the loneliness of having news she cannot share with the one person who would understand it.
  - Ch 07 — Two drafts in the desk drawer; she says "nominal"; not transmitting is now an active, recorded choice and Varga cannot hear it.
  - Ch 08 — The fan repair is disclosed to Varga as the truth — a hardware fault, resolved — which functions simultaneously as the most specific thing she has shared with him and as a screen.
  - Ch 10 — "Working or worrying?" Varga's question at 0800 is the first crack in protocol's surface; Elena says: "Thinking." He says: "That's the same thing for you." The exchange is held by both of them.
  - Ch 11 — **Pivot**: "You've sounded, for the past few days, like someone who's working through something. Not the instruments." Elena says: "There is nothing unusual to report." Varga pauses exactly four seconds. He says: "All right." He offers her space. She takes it without entering it.
  - Ch 12 — Warm, ordinary calls. She says: "It's been a good schedule." He says: "It has, Hartmann." A quiet farewell already forming.
- **Resolves in**: Ch 14 — The final scheduled call. Varga says: "Always," in response to her thanks. The friendship has absorbed the silence, the offered space, the long pause in Ch 11. It has not broken. She will tell him from McMurdo, after the apparatus has done what it will do — and both of them understand this without naming it.
- **What it's about underneath**: Elena's omissions with Varga are technically true in ways her lie to Carol is not. Yet they accumulate into something equally dishonest, and by Ch 11 both of them know it. This thread asks what it costs to keep a secret from the person best positioned to understand it — and whether a friendship that can hold that weight is stronger or stranger for having done so. Varga's patience is the thread's structural pressure: he gives her every opportunity; she takes none of them; the relationship survives. Whether that is a tribute to the friendship or an indictment of Elena's habit of solitude is not resolved.

## Thread 2: Destroy

- **Question**: Is destroying the data — the third option, leaving no trace — a real choice Elena is actively holding, or has it already been made for her by who she is?
- **Enters**: Ch 03 — Verification complete. The three options organize themselves in Elena's mind: Transmit. Preserve. Destroy. She does not write them yet, but she registers that she has not immediately reached for the radio. Destroy is one of three live possibilities.
- **Advanced in**:
  - Ch 04 — She writes all three options in her field log, numbered, labeled. She stares at "Destroy" for a full minute. She does not cross it out. She adds a fourth blank line beneath.
  - Ch 05 — Destroy persists unspoken. She is doing science with the data, which is not what someone preparing to destroy it does — but she has not named this to herself.
  - Ch 06 — She checks the fireproof box during station rounds, verifies the tape is seated correctly, latches it closed. The option is not acted upon; it also has not been foreclosed.
  - Ch 07 — Two paper drafts now exist in the desk drawer. Their existence is evidence of what she considered. The option lingers in the background of a chapter that is otherwise about the other options.
- **Resolves in**: Ch 08 — The MicroVAX fan fails. In forty minutes of urgent repair work, Elena discovers she does not want the data to disappear. Not because it complicates her options. Not because it would be wrong. She does not want it to disappear because she does not want it to disappear. She draws a line through "Destroy" in her log and writes: *Not an option. Has not been. This was decided on night one.* The resolution is retrospective: the option eliminated itself when she made three copies in the first hours. The thread reveals character by showing what Elena had been pretending was still a possibility.
- **What it's about underneath**: The thread pressurizes the question of whether Elena's scientific conscience is integrity or a different form of self-interest. She cannot destroy the signal not because it would be wrong — it would also be wrong to suppress it for personal credit, and she is considering that — but because she does not want to. The distinction between moral reasoning and desire matters, and Elena is honest enough to see it. The option's elimination is not a triumph; it is a moment of self-knowledge. She was never going to do it. She needed the fan to fail to know that she had already decided.

## Thread 3: The Corrections

- **Question**: Has Elena's celebrated skepticism — the thirty years of cataloguing what the universe is not doing — been a form of self-protection against the risk of caring, and can she face what the signal reveals about who she became?
- **Enters**: Ch 01 — Elena's professional identity is established in her habits and her field log before the signal arrives: she is a person who finds what is not there, who has heard the institutional joke (if Hartmann calls it, it's real) until it no longer lands. The signal arrives against all of this.
- **Advanced in**:
  - Ch 02 — The Hartmann Protocol is working as designed: it is cornering her. Rigor is working against her preferred outcome, which was to find an error.
  - Ch 03 — Verification is complete. She writes the conclusion and initials it: *E.H.* It is hers to own. She has been here before — but never here.
  - Ch 04 — She writes the draft notification and reads it back. It is exactly right. The reason for holding back is not in the text. She has to face that.
  - Ch 05 — She writes in her field log: *The tools for finding it were not designed for having found it.* This is the first sentence in which she admits the epistemological problem: her career built the instrument but not the procedure for what comes after.
  - Ch 06 — In the emergency cache hut she catches herself doing orbital geometry on the signal's sky position — and realizes she has been doing this for two days. She has been behaving like a believer. This is new information about herself.
  - Ch 07 — She reads the draft aloud in the receiver hut. Every sentence is correct. The conclusion sounds the same spoken as it does in her head. She does not transmit. The obstruction is not in the science.
  - Ch 08 — She does not want the data to disappear. This is information about herself she did not have before the fan failed.
  - Ch 09 — Carol says: "I trust you." Elena says yes to the sleep question. These two things are in the same phone call.
  - Ch 10 — **Pivot**: Elena does the career accounting — the full thirty years — and for the first time names what her skepticism has been: *a kind of shelter.* She stopped believing contact was possible sometime in her forties, without announcement, without deciding. The signal has forced her to see the shelter for what it was. She separates the question of what she wants from the question of what the data requires, and separated, she sees both more clearly.
  - Ch 11 — She examines whether she is protecting Varga from the weight of the knowledge or protecting herself from what it would mean to say it out loud. She does not answer.
  - Ch 12 — She runs the protocol for the second and final time — not from doubt but as ritual completion. The repetition has changed its quality. She has come through the reckoning.
  - Ch 13 — She writes the finding into the official station log. Writing it there is irreversible in a way writing it in her field log was not. Afterward, she sleeps — the first unbroken night since the signal arrived.
  - Ch 14 — She reads the eleven-day log from the beginning. Her record holds up. It is a coherent document. She was who she was.
- **Resolves in**: Deliberately unresolved. Whether Elena's thirty years of skepticism — the carefully maintained distance, the stopped belief, the career built on corrections — cost her something or cost her nothing: the story does not adjudicate. Brandt says "then we have work to do" and this is not a verdict. Elena says "yes" and starts the kettle. The signal confirms that the possibility she stopped believing in was real. Whether she was right to stop believing, whether the shelter was worth what it cost, whether a different Elena would have been better or worse at any of this — these questions are raised and left open. The reader arrives at them without being directed.
- **What it's about underneath**: This is the story's deepest thread. Elena built a professional self out of a discipline that turned out to be, simultaneously, a genuine rigorous methodology and a managed relationship with the risk of discovery. The signal does not vindicate or rebuke this; it simply arrives, non-negotiable. The thread's refusal to resolve is not laziness — it is the only honest answer to the question it's asking. To adjudicate would be to know something about a life that no one, including Elena, is positioned to know.

## Thread 4: The Blank Line

- **Question**: Is there a fourth path — neither immediate institutional transmission, nor delayed personal publication, nor destruction — by which Elena can honor the signal's scientific weight and her own conscience, and what exactly does it look like?
- **Enters**: Ch 01 — Elena makes three copies and tells no one. This is already a gesture toward Option 4: she is not transmitting immediately; she is not destroying; she is doing something that the protocol has no name for yet. The blank line does not yet exist on paper, but the behavior that will fill it is already in motion.
- **Advanced in**:
  - Ch 04 — She writes "4. Something else." in her log and leaves it blank. The fourth option has a formal address on the page for the first time.
  - Ch 05 — She has been doing science with the data, buying time she hasn't consciously named. The option is taking shape through her behavior before it has taken shape in language.
  - Ch 06 — She catches herself doing informal orbital geometry on the signal's sky position — work she has no authorization to do yet, work that assumes she will have time to do it. She has been acting on Option 4 without calling it anything.
  - Ch 07 — Two drafts in the drawer; "the question of timing is not the only question." She owns the delay now; it is an active choice.
  - Ch 08 — Option 3 is crossed out. The remaining unknown is Option 4.
  - Ch 09 — Elena cannot share the weight of the finding with Carol; she lies rather than say nothing. The fourth option requires holding the knowledge in a specific way, and she is still finding the way.
  - Ch 10 — She separates the question of her career legacy from the question of what the data requires, and sees the path: rigorous documentation followed by handoff to a person she trusts in person, before the apparatus takes over. The blank line is almost full.
  - Ch 11 — She thinks about "when it is no longer only mine to hold." She can name the shape of the option now.
- **Resolves in**: Ch 12 (named), Ch 13 (enacted), Ch 15 (completed). In Ch 12 she writes in the log: *Option 4: complete the record. Hand it over in person. The rest is not mine to decide.* In Ch 13 she writes the full record into the official station log, labels the tape HS-910603, files the annotated printout. In Ch 15, she says to Brandt: *There's something in the log from the first night. Read from page forty-seven.*
- **What it's about underneath**: Option 4 is the discovery of a form of ethical action the protocol was not designed to anticipate. It is neither surrender to the apparatus nor personal suppression nor erasure — it is one more act of care before the world gets the finding. The blank line is the space where Elena's thirty years of rigor and her professional conscience converge: she does not transmit immediately because the protocol covers detection, not disclosure. She does not destroy because she is the custodian of something larger than her options. She does not hold for credit because she cannot live with herself holding it that way. What she does instead — complete the record perfectly and hand it to a trusted human being — is not in any protocol she has read. It is what a scientist does when the tools for finding it were not designed for having found it.

## Thread 5: I Trust You

- **Question**: What does Elena's one outright lie reveal about the cost of carrying singular knowledge alone — and does the trust her daughter extends survive, unexamined, past the end of the story?
- **Enters**: Ch 09 — Elena calls Carol in Boulder on the civilian slot. Carol asks if she's sleeping all right. Elena says yes. This is the only lie she tells anyone during the eleven days. Carol says: "I trust you." These two sentences are in the same phone call.
- **Advanced in**:
  - Ch 10 — Elena cannot sleep. She lies in her bunk at 0248 thinking about the signal, as she has been doing every night since it arrived. She thinks, later, about the word *trust* and what it costs and what it is worth. She does not write about the call in her field log. The Day 9 entry reads: *Instruments nominal. No unusual events.* — the same screening language she has used with Varga.
  - Ch 11 — The lie is sustained. She examines whether she is protecting Varga from the knowledge or protecting herself, and she does not answer. The same question applies to Carol, unstated.
  - Ch 14 — She decides how she will tell Brandt: after the team is inside, after coffee, pointing to page forty-seven. She does not decide anything about Carol. Carol is not part of the handoff.
- **Resolves in**: Deliberately unresolved. Carol does not appear in Ch 15. The lie stands. Carol's "I trust you" is never redeemed within the story's frame. Elena will tell Carol eventually — after McMurdo, after the apparatus has done what it will do, after the paper is submitted or suppressed or claimed — but that conversation is not part of this book. The lie remains on the record: *Yes, I'm sleeping all right.* Elena's Day 9 log entry, with its *no unusual events*, is as dishonest a document as anything she has written to Varga, and she knows it, and she does not revise it.
- **What it's about underneath**: The Carol thread is not about plot. It is about what singular knowledge does to the ordinary human transactions of love. Elena is a good mother in the ways she can be; this is the one moment in the story when she is not. That it is also the most understandable moment — she cannot tell Carol, cannot say nothing, so she lies cleanly and moves on — does not resolve it. The thread refuses resolution because the story refuses to let Elena's carrying of the signal be cost-free. She lies to the person who trusts her most simply, and the lie is both forgivable and unexamined. Carol's trust is the casualty of Elena's solitude that the story does not fix.

**Notes on the weave:**

- **Ch 02** advances only two threads — T1 and T3. This is the tightest chapter in the weave. It is not a dead chapter: both threads are genuinely advanced (first active omission; protocol cornering her). The relative thinness is appropriate — it is the chapter in which Elena is most focused on the technical work and least focused on anything else.

- **Ch 13** advances only two threads — T3 and T4. Again not dead. Ch 13 is the night of the documentation; the Varga calls have already occurred in the Ch 12 window; the Carol beat has passed. Two threads are enough for a chapter that is the story's moment of maximum formal completion.

- **Thread 2 (Destroy)** runs Chs 03–08 and does not appear in the weave after Ch 08. This is by design: it resolves at the midpoint and is not revisited. Its absence from Chs 09–15 is not a gap; it is the silence of a settled question.

- **Thread 5 (I Trust You)** does not appear in Chs 01–08 because Carol has not yet appeared in the story. Its absence from those chapters is not a gap; the thread begins when it begins. The reader will not miss it in Chs 01–08 because T1 and T3 are both doing intensive work in that span.
