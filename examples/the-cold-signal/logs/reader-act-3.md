# Developmental Editor — Act 3 Assessment
## Chapters 11–15: Something in Your Voice / The Protocol, Final Pass / HS-910603 / The Last Morning / Read from Page Forty-Seven

---

### What the Act Is Doing

Act 3 is a deceleration that has earned its slowness. The deliberative pressure that drove Acts 1 and 2 has been released by the end of Chapter 10, and these five chapters exist to enact — not reconsider — the decision already made. The structural sequence is correct: Ch. 11 closes the last moment of real tension (the Varga pivot); Ch. 12 converts the protocol from inquiry to ritual; Ch. 13 executes the documentation; Ch. 14 is an arrival; Ch. 15 is a handoff. The draft understands what each chapter is *for*, and this understanding is the act's greatest strength.

---

### What Is Working

**The Varga farewell arc across Chs. 11, 12, and 14 is the act's sustained achievement.** The Ch. 11 radio exchange is the best-written scene in the draft: "There is nothing unusual to report" lands with full weight precisely because we hear it from Elena's side as she hears it from herself — not a deflection, but a door latched from the inside. Varga's four-second pause and "All right" are perfectly calibrated. The Ch. 12 evening call ("It's been a good schedule" / "It has, Hartmann") completes the farewell register with total economy. And in Ch. 14, "Thank you for the schedule" / "Always" arrives as the correct two-word resolution to eleven chapters of omission. This arc fulfills Thread 1 without a single false note.

**Category 7 in Ch. 12 is the finest character moment in the act.** "She had always found mirages more interesting than hallucinations." Delivered in the middle of a technical review, this sentence contains Elena's entire scientific personality in thirteen words. It is the style functioning at its peak: technical vocabulary in service of character, never explaining itself to the reader.

**Chapter 14's procedural precision is exemplary.** The good butter finally used, the bunks squared, the calibration run, the radome circuit that deliberately refuses transcendence ("She had never had an occasion to regard it as other than a structure housing a piece of equipment. She did not mark it now"), the Parkes preprint interrupted at page thirteen by the sound of engines — these are the texture the brief calls for. The closing line — "She walked out to meet it" — is the best sentence in the act and possibly the manuscript.

**Chapter 13's tape-labeling sequence is exactly right.** The formal label pressed beside the original handwritten one; both labels initialed; the tape replaced in the box. This is the enactment of Option 4, rendered as small irreversible physical action, without announcement.

**The restraint of Ch. 15 is earned.** Elena not watching Brandt read; the translated attention ("the way that happens when a sentence reorganizes everything that came before it"); the room's texture during Brandt's silence — these are formal achievements. Brandt's "Then we have work to do" lands correctly: not excitement, the natural next step.

---

### What Isn't Working

**Chapter 11's receiver-hut meditation section outstays its welcome.** After the superb radio exchange, Elena sits in the receiver hut and works through an extended analysis of what it would have meant to tell Varga on Day 1 (starting at "What would it have meant to tell him on Day 1"). This is largely a reprise of the Ch. 10 accounting in different clothing. The Amsterdam memory earns its space — it is the friendship's founding document and belongs here — but the paragraphs of hypothetical reasoning ("she might have transmitted immediately / might have handed the decision to the protocol") cover ground the reader has already surveyed. This section could lose 200–250 words without any loss of substance or resonance.

**Chapter 13 over-explains at the radio.** The passage beginning "There had been eleven days of this — eleven days in which transmission had been possible" retreads the pattern of non-transmission that the reader has tracked for twelve chapters. The first sentence of this passage is sufficient: "The reason was that the work was done." Everything that follows slightly diminishes the restraint that made the act's deceleration credible.

**A voice slip in Ch. 15 breaks the POV register.** During the crate-carrying scene: "the way you are aware of something that is waiting at the exact point where the next set of actions converge." The second-person "you" appears nowhere else in the narration. The manuscript maintains strict third-person limited throughout; this is an inadvertent lapse that should be closed in revision.

**The final image of Ch. 15 is a degree too comfortable.** "I'll start the kettle again" doubles the kettle gesture from earlier in the chapter and lands slightly softer than the manuscript's commitment to restraint has earned. The closing is not wrong — returning to ordinary station routine is thematically appropriate — but it is less precise than "She walked out to meet it" in Ch. 14, which is the act's truer final note.

---

### Voice Assessment

The register holds throughout the act with three specific exceptions: Ch. 11's "something uncomfortably close to relief" (slightly too labeled, by canon standard, for a narration that should let action carry feeling); the Ch. 15 "you" pronoun noted above; and one line in Ch. 12 — "She had been moving toward it since Day 1, or close to it" — that edges toward the retrospective editorial voice the narration usually avoids by a fraction. None of these are structural; all are second-pass tightenings.

---

### Thread Development in This Act

All active threads advance correctly. Thread 1 pivots in Ch. 11, farewells in Ch. 12, and resolves cleanly in Ch. 14 ("Always"). Thread 2 is correctly absent — it was settled in Ch. 8 and its silence here is appropriate. Thread 3 is deliberately unresolved in Ch. 15, as the canon design requires. Thread 4 resolves across Chs. 12–13–15 in the correct three-part sequence (named / enacted / completed). Thread 5 is unresolved by design — Carol is absent from Ch. 15 and the lie stands — and this absence is handled correctly: there is nothing to flag. The weave is clean.

---

### In-Act Pacing

The act's deceleration is deliberate and largely successful. The risk — that Ch. 13 reads as too quiet between the formal closes of Chs. 12 and 14 — is a real one, and readers who have been tracking the deliberative tension may find it under-energized. But the chapter's quietness is thematically correct: the documentation is the story's moment of maximum formal completion, and the prose serves it well. The Chapter 11 meditation is the one pacing problem that actually slows the act unnecessarily, because it reprises thinking rather than enacting it.

---

### Strongest Chapter in the Act

Chapter 14. Formally complete, tonally controlled, three of the act's best images, the best closing sentence.

### Chapter Most in Need of Revision

Chapter 11. The core is excellent; the middle needs trimming.
