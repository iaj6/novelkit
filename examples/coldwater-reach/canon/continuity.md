# Continuity

Hard facts that must not break in later drafting. These are binding.

1. The story runs from **October 1943 to spring 1945**, ending just before VE Day (May 8, 1945).
2. **Eira Bowman** is 52 in October 1943. She has been harbormaster of Coldwater Reach since 1936, following her husband Owen's death in the November gale of **November 1935**. Owen Bowman was the previous harbormaster.
3. **Hannah Pfeiffer** is 17 in October 1943. Her family farm is three miles inland from Coldwater Reach. Her brother **Will Pfeiffer** is serving in the Pacific. His last V-Mail arrived in August 1943.
4. **Dr. Marcus Klein** is 47 in October 1943. He is Viennese, Jewish, and was interned at **Camp Sherbrooke**, Quebec, from **June to December 1940**. He arrived in Coldwater in **March 1942**. He boards with Henry and Lillian Crow.
5. **Henry Crow** and **Lillian Crow** are Black. They came north from Norfolk, Virginia, in **1924**. Henry is the chief custodian of Coldwater Reach Consolidated School. He has a son, **Elias Crow**, currently serving in the Navy.
6. The body washes onto the **Coldwater breakwater** after a three-day blow, arriving on or around **October 14, 1943**. The man carries no identification. The Coast Guard takes the body. The body's identity is never definitively confirmed within the story — the reader assembles an answer across thirty chapters, but the underlying fact stays partly in shadow.
7. On the night of **October 11, 1943**, Eira saw an unidentified man on the north side of the wharf at approximately 2 a.m. She did not hail him. She did not report this to the Coast Guard.
8. The **German POW labor detail** on Pfeiffer farm consists of four prisoners, all captured in North Africa in 1943. The youngest is **Karl Reinmar**, 19, from Kirchheim, Bavaria — approximately 50 kilometers from Albrecht Pfeiffer's hometown of **Amberg, Bavaria**.
9. **Lonzo Reed**, 60, is the Coastal Patrol private assigned to escort the Pfeiffer farm POW detail. He is a Coldwater native and a veteran of the First World War.
10. The three main POV characters — Eira, Hannah, and Marcus — **never meet as a group of three**. Their threads cross in pairs, by accident or design, but there is no scene in which all three are present together.
11. There is **no spy plot, no Nazi agent, no coded communication, no submarine landing**. The body on the breakwater is a mystery of human circumstance, not espionage.
12. **Albrecht Pfeiffer** emigrated from Amberg, Bavaria, to Maine in **1922**. He has not returned to Germany.
13. The story has **30 chapters**, rotating POV across the three main characters (approximately ten chapters each). There may be 2–3 exceptional short chapters from a fourth perspective (Henry Crow, Lonzo Reed, or Karl Reinmar), but these are structural exceptions, not regular POV slots.
14. **At least one narrative thread must remain deliberately unresolved**. Each of the three characters resolves their *relation* to the breakwater question, but the underlying fact of who the dead man was may or may not be answered in full.
15. The POW labor program detail is historically accurate: German POWs were housed and used as farm labor throughout Maine in 1943–1945, under the auspices of the Geneva Convention, paid in camp scrip, and guarded by aged or deferred service personnel.
16. Marcus Klein's German and Latin teaching position at the school was created by the enlistment of the previous instructor, a local man named **Braddock**. The school board's employment of Marcus was pragmatic and remains grudging.
17. **Coldwater Reach** is a fictional town. Real Maine place names (Portland, Bangor, Houlton, Sherbrooke, Cambridge) may appear. Real people's names must not appear.
