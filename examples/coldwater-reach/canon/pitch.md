# Pitch

**The Breakwater Season** is a literary novel set in the fictional coastal town of Coldwater Reach, Maine, from autumn 1943 through spring 1945. Three people — Eira Bowman, a fifty-two-year-old woman who holds the harbormaster post no man wanted after Pearl Harbor; Hannah Pfeiffer, a seventeen-year-old daughter of Bavarian immigrants whose dairy farm now houses four German POW laborers; and Dr. Marcus Klein, a Viennese Jewish scholar teaching high-school German in the town that hired him because it had no other choice — each carry, separately and without coordination, a single unresolved event: an unidentified man's body that washed onto the Coldwater breakwater in October 1943, the day after Eira saw something on the dock she has told no one about. Over eighteen months, each character's investigation of that event is subordinate to an internal reckoning that was already underway before the body arrived: Eira with the cost of holding authority in a body the authority was never designed for; Hannah with the slow accumulation of small choices that will determine who she becomes; Marcus with whether displacement is permanent, and what remains of a person when it ends. The war does not intrude as adventure. It arrives as a radio crackle, a casualty telegram, a ration book running low, four young men working silently in a cold field. The story ends in spring 1945, just before VE Day, with resolutions that are smaller and older than the war's ending — and at least one question the reader assembles but no character fully answers.
