# Chapter 2 — Arrival

**One-line summary:** Four German POWs arrive at Pfeiffer farm by truck from Camp Houlton; Hannah watches from the kitchen window and notes the youngest one last.

**POV:** Hannah Pfeiffer  
**Tense:** Third-person limited past  
**Arc phase:** Setup

---

## Setting / Time

Pfeiffer farm, three miles inland from Coldwater Reach. Mid-October 1943 — a few days after the body's discovery, though Hannah knows almost nothing of it yet (a rumor at school, dismissed). Cold, overcast. The fields are mostly cleared; the barn work is the farm's center of gravity.

---

## Goal

Hannah has no goal in this chapter beyond getting the morning milking done before school. The POWs' arrival is a thing happening to her farm, not something she has prepared for or wanted. Her observational intelligence makes her a witness before she is a participant.

---

## Obstacle

The arrival reorders the farm's social geography in ways Hannah hasn't been asked about. Albrecht and the county man have arranged this; Magda has not left the house. Hannah has to fit the prisoners into a mental picture she hasn't been asked to form.

---

## Action Beats

**Opening — morning milking:**
- Hannah is in the barn at 5:45 a.m. with the cows. The smell, the warmth, the mechanical rhythm of milking. The farm's interior world at its most self-contained.
- Her father is already in the field with the last of the fall work. Her mother is in the house. Silence of the household is established.
- She notes Will's absence — his hook is empty by the barn door where he used to hang his jacket.

**The county man arrives:**
- A truck from the county office comes mid-morning with a man named Aldrich who has papers for Albrecht to sign. Hannah is the one who calls her father in from the field.
- Aldrich explains the arrangement — the prisoners from Camp Houlton, the escort schedule, the payment in scrip, the Geneva Convention provisions. He speaks rapidly and uses words like *detail* and *complement* that Hannah files away.
- Albrecht signs without speaking in English. He asks one question: *Woher kommen sie?* (Where are they from?) Aldrich doesn't understand. Hannah translates. Aldrich says they're from the Afrika Korps. Albrecht says nothing more.

**The icehouse preparation:**
- Hannah goes with Aldrich to inspect the icehouse on the eastern edge of the property. It's been cleared — sawdust swept, four cots set up two-by-two, cast-iron stove, single bulb on a cord. The smell of cold wood and old sawdust and a new stove's first fire.
- She thinks: *it's smaller than she expected.* Four grown men will sleep here.

**The truck arrives:**
- The prisoners come in an Army truck late afternoon, Lonzo Reed's Chevrolet following behind. Four men climb down, in German Army field-grey with POW marked on the back. They're quieter than Hannah expected.
- Hannah watches from the kitchen window, standing at the sink with the dishes.
- Three of the men are older — late twenties to thirties. The fourth, last off the truck, is young. Blond. He looks at the icehouse, then at the barn, then at the tree line. He doesn't look at the farmhouse.
- This is Karl. Hannah doesn't know his name yet.
- Lonzo herds them toward the icehouse. Albrecht stands in the yard watching. He does not speak.

**Evening:**
- Dinner at the Pfeiffer table. Albrecht says grace in German — *Komm, Herr Jesu* — as he does every night. Then silence.
- Hannah listens. She can hear, faintly, the prisoners in the icehouse — not speaking, just the sound of movement. A stove grate being adjusted.
- Her mother Magda has put extra potatoes on the table and then sat down without eating them.
- Hannah thinks about the word *Woher kommen sie* — where do they come from — and realizes she doesn't know the answer, and wants to.

---

## Outcome / Turn

The chapter ends with the POWs installed in the icehouse and Hannah aware of them as an unresolved question. She has not spoken to any of them. She has not been told not to. The farm has a new population and she doesn't know what that means.

---

## New Question or Pressure Raised

- Who are these four men? Where specifically are they from?
- What does Albrecht's question — *Woher kommen sie?* — mean? What does it cost him to have German soldiers on his farm?
- What is Magda's silence about, and what does it cost the family?
- Where is Will — and why are his V-Mails short?

---

## Continuity Notes

- Establishes: The four prisoners on the Pfeiffer farm. Karl is the youngest; he is last off the truck.
- Establishes: Lonzo Reed's Chevrolet (he drives himself, not official Army transport).
- Establishes: The icehouse layout — four cots, two-by-two, cast-iron stove, single electric bulb, sawdust smell.
- Establishes: Albrecht's behavior — he signs the paperwork, asks one German-language question, watches from the yard, does not speak at dinner.
- Establishes: Magda's withdrawal — present but absent from family interaction.
- Establishes: Will's hook by the barn door — an absence marker that should recur.
- Hannah does not yet know Karl's name. She identifies him only as the youngest one.

---

## Notes for Drafter

- The farm's physical world should be dense and specific: barn smells, the weight of pails, the sound of cows, the quality of mid-October cold. This is Hannah's world and she is good at it.
- Karl should appear briefly and without drama — last off the truck, looking at things, not at people. Hannah notices; she doesn't assign meaning yet.
- Albrecht's question (*Woher kommen sie?*) should feel weighted — the reader should understand it might be personal curiosity, or anxiety, or something older — but the prose should not explain which.
- The evening scene should carry the sound of the prisoners in the icehouse without making it symbolic. It is just sound through walls.
- German phrases in Hannah's chapters are italicized, never prose-translated. Context carries meaning.
