# Chapter 02 — The Icehouse

**One-line summary**: The POWs arrive at the Pfeiffer farm; Hannah watches through the barn boards and notices the youngest one.

**POV**: Hannah Pfeiffer  
**Tense**: Third-person limited, past  
**Setting**: The Pfeiffer dairy farm: the barn, the farmhouse doorway, the icehouse — three miles inland from Coldwater Reach  
**Time**: Mid-afternoon, Thursday, approximately October 14–16, 1943

---

## Goal
Hannah wants to understand what the Army truck has brought onto her family's property, and what it means that these men are now twenty yards from the barn.

## Obstacle
The barn boards give her cover but not clarity. Her father stands in the doorway and says nothing. Her mother doesn't come out at all. The language barrier is total: the men speak only German to each other; Hannah understands most of it but catches only fragments across the distance.

---

## Action Beats

- Chapter opens during the afternoon milking — Hannah with her hands already on the work, the familiar cold of the stall, the smell of hide and manure and the first October frost in the concrete floor — when the sound of a truck engine cuts through the routine. Not a farm truck; too heavy.
- She goes to the gap between the boards she's known about since childhood. The Army truck: two soldiers up front, one in the truck bed with the four men. The four wear Army-issue denim work clothes, identical, not quite fitting any of them correctly. They step down from the truck without being told to — they have done this before.
- She catalogs them: three older men (forties, maybe — one with a gray beard; one very large; one who moves like his knees hurt). The fourth is young, and she notices him the way you notice something that doesn't fit its category. He looks no older than Rolf did when Rolf left.
- Lonzo Reed steps down from the passenger cab: slow, deliberate, using the door as a brace for his bad knee. He sets his rifle across the crook of his arm like someone who has thought about where to put it and this is where he decided. He produces a folding canvas camp chair from the truck bed and opens it beside the icehouse door.
- Hannah's father stands in the farmhouse doorway. He watches. He does not speak to the soldiers or to the prisoners. When the soldiers address him — a name, a clipboard, a signature line — he signs without a word.
- The four men are walked through the icehouse door. It closes. The soldiers depart in the truck. Lonzo Reed sits down in his chair.
- Hannah stays in the barn until the milking is done and the light has changed.
- At dinner: her father speaks German — not as a choice but as a condition, like breathing. He says something about *die Arbeit* (the work) the boys will do, something about the fence on the north pasture. Hannah's mother moves from stove to table without contributing a sound.
- Hannah translates the German in her head, filling in the words she doesn't quite catch. She thinks about the young one's face. He was looking at the icehouse wall when they walked him in. Not frightened, exactly. Something else.
- She goes to bed in Rolf's old room — now her room, though she still thinks of it as Rolf's. Rolf's coat hangs on the hook by the door. She falls asleep thinking the German word *fremd* — foreign, strange, not from here.

---

## Outcome / Turn
The POWs are installed. The situation has begun. Hannah has registered Karl's existence as distinct from the others without yet knowing his name.

## New Question or Pressure Raised
Who is the young one, where is he from, and what does it mean that he is now living in the icehouse her family owns?
