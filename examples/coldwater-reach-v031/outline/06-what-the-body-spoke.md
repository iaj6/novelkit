# Chapter 06 — What the Body Spoke

**One-line summary**: Marcus is asked by the school principal to consult on a German-speaking rumor; he visits Eira at the harbor office and makes an offer she files without accepting.

**POV**: Marcus Klein  
**Tense**: Third-person limited, past  
**Setting**: Coldwater Reach High School (principal's office, Marcus's classroom); the harbor office; the street between them  
**Time**: November 1943

---

## Goal
Marcus wants to understand what the body inquiry has to do with him — specifically whether being consulted is an invitation or a use.

## Obstacle
The rumor is thin (a fisherman heard something "European, maybe German"), and going to the harbor office means inserting himself into a situation where he is not quite welcome and where the act of offering help may be read as claiming too much.

---

## Action Beats

- Principal Aldous Fenn calls Marcus in on a Tuesday morning (the older suit). The Coast Guard, Fenn explains, made a general inquiry: did anyone in town hear the man on the dock speak before the storm? A local fisherman — Ed Larrabee, who was on the dock patching a trap buoy that afternoon — reported that he heard the man say something that sounded "European, maybe German." The Coast Guard passed this to the harbormaster's office, which mentioned it to the selectmen, which mentioned it to Fenn.
- Fenn asks Marcus if he'd be willing to speak with the harbormaster — not as an investigator, but as a civic resource, in case the harbormaster has any materials (a phrase or written word) that need identification. He phrases this carefully, not wanting Marcus to feel used, which means Marcus feels the care and reads it correctly.
- Marcus says he'd be glad to help. He means it and is also aware that saying anything else would look like avoidance.
- He walks to the harbor office after his afternoon class. He has been in it once before, for a vessel documentation check when he was planning a classroom exercise on maritime regulation that never materialized. He knows where it is.
- Eira is at her desk. She looks up when he comes in. She is not unfriendly, but she is not expecting him and the harbor office is her professional space; she does not yield ground in it.
- He explains what Fenn asked him to do. He says he's been told that a fisherman heard the man speak something that might be German. He offers: if any written item should surface — a document, a phrase — he's willing to assist with identification.
- Eira says: "The Coast Guard has what I know." She says it without inflection, which is the way she says most things, but it contains a great deal.
- He says: "Of course. I won't take up your time." He starts to leave. Then stops. He says, more carefully: "If it would be useful — not to the Coast Guard necessarily, but to you — I know something about identification documents from that period. Varieties of German. I'm at the school."
- Eira looks at him for a moment. She says: "I'll note it."
- He walks back. It is nearly dark; the harbor is a sound more than a sight. He thinks about the phrase *a man without papers* — which in German would be *ein Mensch ohne Papiere*, and which in German carries a weight that the English phrase only approximates. To be without papers in Europe in the last decade was to be without proof of your right to exist. He wonders whether the man knew what he was without.
- He tells Henry about the visit that evening, briefly. Henry says: "She'll use you when she needs you. Not before."

---

## Outcome / Turn
Marcus has offered his expertise; Eira has filed it. Nothing has advanced except the existence of a connection between them that can be activated later. The rumor about the German-speaking man is now in Marcus's head as an open question.

## New Question or Pressure Raised
Is the body connected to German identity in some way that will touch Marcus's specific expertise? If so, when?

---

## Continuity Notes
- Ed Larrabee: local fisherman, heard the man say something "European, maybe German" on the dock in the afternoon of October 9, before the storm. This is the basis of the rumor that comes to Marcus. Not a major character.
- Aldous Fenn, principal of Coldwater Reach High School. He means well; his request is reasonable; Marcus reads the subtext accurately.
- Marcus's offer to Eira: identification documents, varieties of German, general linguistic expertise. This is foreshadowing for Chapter 19 (when Marcus independently finds August Schell's name) and Chapter 29 (when Eira finally closes the file).
- Eira's response — "The Coast Guard has what I know" — is precise and true and also a way of declining to open any of the doors she has not yet decided to open.
- Henry's assessment: "She'll use you when she needs you. Not before." Henry reads social dynamics accurately.
- The chapter should not feel like a plot-delivery mechanism. Marcus's walk to the harbor office, his observation of the town at near-dark, his thinking about *ein Mensch ohne Papiere* — these carry as much weight as the exchange with Eira.
- Marcus is wearing the older suit (Tuesday). This detail can anchor the chapter's opening without comment.
