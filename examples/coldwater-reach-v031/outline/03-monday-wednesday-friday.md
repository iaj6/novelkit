# Chapter 03 — Monday, Wednesday, Friday

**One-line summary**: Marcus's daily life in Coldwater Reach is established; he hears about the body at school, and meets Eira briefly at a civil defense warden meeting.

**POV**: Marcus Klein  
**Tense**: Third-person limited, past  
**Setting**: Coldwater Reach High School (converted mill building); Henry and Lillian Crow's house; the school basement (civil defense warden meeting)  
**Time**: Late October – early November 1943

---

## Goal
No single goal — this chapter establishes Marcus's world in Coldwater Reach through the accumulation of his daily routine: the two suits, the school, Henry and Lillian, the town's ambient suspicion, and his first real encounter with Eira.

## Obstacle
He is a man whom Coldwater Reach tolerates rather than understands. The chapter's obstacle is the ordinary friction of being legible to people in the wrong terms — foreign, German-seeming, inexplicable in this particular village in this particular war.

---

## Action Beats

- Monday morning: Marcus puts on the good suit (Monday, Wednesday, Friday). He leaves Henry and Lillian's house at twenty past seven, walks to school. He catalogs the walk — the smell of the harbor before he reaches the ridge, the lobster boat engines starting in the dark, the Congregational church that is never unlocked when he passes but whose bell he can hear setting the town's clock.
- German class: six students, including Hannah Pfeiffer — whom he notices, as he notices all his students, with the particular professional attention of a teacher assessing capability. She already knows more German than she lets on; she answers correctly, slightly after the pause that signals she's decided how much to reveal.
- The teacher's lounge at noon: the body comes up. The principal, Aldous Fenn, mentions it — a man found on the breakwater, Coast Guard involved, probably nothing. The other teachers look at Marcus for half a second, a reflex, then look at the table. Marcus drinks his coffee.
- He thinks, walking back to his classroom: what the reflex means. He has been calculating reflexes like this since at least 1936. He is not offended; he is accurate.
- Evening: Henry and Lillian's kitchen. Lillian serves the meal without ceremony; Henry asks what Marcus heard. Marcus tells him about the body. Henry says: "They find out who he was?" Marcus: "Not yet." Henry: "They will or they won't. Doesn't change anything for us."
- The civil defense warden meeting, Thursday evening, in the school basement: a ring of men around a table — the warden, a selectman, the fire chief, two Coast Guardsmen, and half a dozen others. Marcus is there as a building representative for the school. He notices Eira Bowman across the table — the only woman, not performing her authority, simply having it. He has seen her before at the harbor, at a distance.
- The meeting is procedural: blackout regulations, fire watch schedule. Marcus is given the Tuesday rotation, which he already knew.
- Walking out: he and Eira reach the door at the same time. He holds it. She steps through and says, over her shoulder: "You're the German teacher." Not a question. He says: "Austrian, in fact." She says: "Same road home," meaning the direction. They walk fifty yards together and then her road forks and she goes left without a further word.
- At home, in his room, he opens the grade book. His annotations are in Kurrent. He stares at a sentence he wrote in the margin of an exam — a student's error — and thinks about the word *unheimlich*. Literally: un-home-like. The uncanny. But also: the sensation of being in a place that should feel familiar and doesn't.

---

## Outcome / Turn
Marcus is established in his world: the school, the house, the town's tolerance at its current temperature. He has met Eira — not meaningfully yet, just enough to know she exists.

## New Question or Pressure Raised
What will the school board do? What is the body, and does it have anything to do with Marcus's expertise?
