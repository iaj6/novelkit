## 29-april

### Fix applied

- **"a Tuesday" → "a Saturday" (March 24, 1945).**
  - **Original text:** "she had noted the breakout date in the harbor log on March 24, a Tuesday, the ice going out in a northwest blow"
  - **Conflict:** March 24, 1945 was a Saturday. Calculation: January 1, 1945 = Monday (1944 leap year, 366 days, 366 mod 7 = 2; Saturday + 2 = Monday). January → February 1 = Thursday (+31). February → March 1 = Thursday (+28, non-leap). March 1 + 23 days = Thursday + 2 = Saturday. "A Tuesday" is incorrect by four days.
  - **Fix:** Changed "a Tuesday" → "a Saturday." No other prose altered.

### Flagged — systemic issue, not fixable in this chapter alone

- **Issue:** "Lighthouse Service" notices and documents referenced as active in April 1945.
- **Conflict:** The US Lighthouse Service was abolished July 1, 1939 and merged into the US Coast Guard. Official buoy-position notices and cross-reference forms issued in 1944–1945 would be Coast Guard documents, not Lighthouse Service documents. Ch29 references three such items as current: "the Lighthouse Service notice on the spring buoy positions" (twice), "the single Lighthouse Service cross-reference form she had completed in August at the district's request," and the tender's "Lighthouse Service ensign." The 1938 filing cabinet described as "Lighthouse Service standardized" is historically plausible as physical equipment predating the merger.
- **Status:** Cannot fix in ch29 alone without creating inconsistency with earlier chapters that likely use the same terminology. Prior continuity passes did not flag this, suggesting "Lighthouse Service" is established book-wide usage. If the author wishes to correct for historical accuracy, all instances across the manuscript would need to be updated to "Coast Guard" or the appropriate post-1939 designations. Flag for author's attention in final pass.
