# Story Arc

One tight line per drafted chapter, in chronological order.

- **01-the-breakwater** — A drowned stranger washes up on Coldwater Reach's breakwater; Eira Bowman examines the body, hands it to the Coast Guard, files her report, and marks one field with a dash she cannot explain away.
- **02-the-icehouse** — Four German POWs arrive at the Pfeiffer farm; Hannah watches through barn boards, catalogs the men, notices the youngest one, and goes to bed thinking the word *fremd*.
- **03-monday-wednesday-friday** — Marcus Klein's daily world is established — the good suit, the German class, the Crow house — and he meets Eira at a civil defense meeting, then returns to his room thinking the word *unheimlich*.
- **04-the-coast-guard** — Coast Guard Lieutenant Pruitt questions Eira about the night before the storm; she answers the exact question asked, omits the man on the pier, and discovers a departure log entry in her own hand she cannot remember making.
- **05-feeding-time** — Hannah establishes her barn-and-yard morning routine around the POWs; she and Karl exchange their first six words in German — *Ich auch* — across the farm gate.
- **06-what-the-body-spoke** — Fenn asks Marcus to consult the harbormaster about a German-speaking rumor; Marcus offers his expertise, Eira files it without accepting, and he walks home thinking about what a man without papers means in German.
- **07-the-log** — Alone in the harbor office on a December evening, Eira re-examines the Macomber departure entry in her own handwriting, works through three unresolved possibilities, and writes her first note in her personal log: *Inquiry?*
- **08-vater** — At the December dinner table Albrecht names Karl's proximity for the first time in direct response to Hannah's question — *Regensburg ist nicht weit* — and Hannah understands her father knows, and always has.
- **09-the-board** — Edna Locke warns Marcus of a possible board review; Henry counsels him to wait and do nothing; Marcus files an informational letter to Cambridge and names *Duldung* as his working position.
- **10-february-ice** — While freeing a grounded coaster from the inner-anchorage ice, Eira learns Birch Macomber held a Portland Navy district contract since fall 1943 and "had papers," adding the lead to her personal log.
- **11-the-word-for-harbor** — Hannah exchanges word-cards with Karl across the fence; he tells her he has never seen the sea, and she does not yet say she could show him.
- **12-two-suits** — Marcus answers Cambridge with no, commits to staying in Coldwater Reach for the year, and learns the name "A. Schell" from Ada Gile at the post office.
- **13-ada-giles-window** — Eira visits Ada Gile and learns the general-delivery mail was a postcard from Portland — a date and a time — and sits alone with a theory she cannot put in the official log.
- **14-mud-season** — Hannah turns 18; Marcus's lesson on *mündig* plants the word for what she's been practicing; Karl says *Glückwunsch* at the fence; Rolf's V-Mail arrives from Italy and the family holds it in silence.
- **15-the-sixth-of-june** — D-Day, June 6, 1944: Marcus holds his private knowledge of Paris and the Marais while running his classroom; that evening he reads the Emergency Committee's first letter and puts it back.
- **16-the-inspector** — Naval district inspector Whitmore names August Schell—German-born Swedish national, crew of the Ångermanälven—as the probable body; Eira tells the minimum truth and writes the name in her private log.
- **17-off-duty** — Karl's Sunday off-duty movement brings him and Hannah to the north fence together; their first real cross-lingual conversation covers the sea, the river, his uncertain father, and Rolf in Italy.
- **18-what-henry-notices** — Henry finds the anti-Klein petition in the teacher's lounge wastebasket and tells Marcus enough — three names, one on the committee — without saying how he knows.
- **19-august** — Marcus takes the bus to Bangor, finds August Schell's name and biography in the Bangor Daily News seamen's bureau notice, and rides home holding the knowledge alone.
- **20-addendum** — Eira writes a complete account of the night of October 9, 1943 on official stationery, folds it, and files it unforwarded in Owen's pending drawer; she drinks the coffee.
- **21-v-mail** — Hannah writes Rolf a V-Mail that omits everything real — Martha's death, Karl's name, the word *mündig* — and walking home observes, without self-pity, that everyone in town is keeping something.
- **22-reeds-chair** — Lonzo Reed finds Karl absent at the 9 p.m. headcount, writes "present," sits in his chair in the October dark until Karl returns at 10:15, then locks up without a word.
- **23-october-night** — Hannah takes Karl to the ridge above Coldwater Reach and shows him the harbor lights; she tells him about the drowned man, hears he already knew, and lies down afterward in the solidity of something she chose.
- **24-the-hearing** — The school board votes 5–2 to retain Marcus; he substitutes presence for belonging when asked about loyalty; Henry confirms the count from the corridor.
- **25-the-selectmen** — Selectman Orcutt implies a returning veteran should have Eira's post; she declines without refusing, then reorganizes Owen's chart system into her own for the first time in eight years.
- **26-the-last-winter** — Karl tells Hannah his father died December 28 in Regensburg; Hannah holds the news alone while Magda, quietly, begins to speak again in small sentences.
- **27-the-landlords-game** — Over a Thursday chess game in February 1945, Marcus tells Henry he thinks he has stopped moving; Henry accepts the statement without ceremony; Marcus thinks the word *Ansässigkeit* for the first time since 1937.
- **28-regensburg** — Karl lies in the icehouse dark thinking of Regensburg's spire, his father's tile floor, the word *bleiben*, and the harbor Hannah showed him — suspended between a home that may not exist and a farm he now knows.
- **29-april** — Commander Whitmore closes the August Schell case officially; Eira writes the name in the harbor log, walks the breakwater, files the addendum in the permanent record, and goes to meet the buoy tender.
- **30-before-the-day** — Late April 1945: Hannah does the farm, the fence, the school, and the road home, then kneels in the kitchen garden beside her mother to plant onion sets — the war two weeks from ending, she does not know that.
