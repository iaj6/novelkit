# Continuity — Hard Facts

These statements must not be broken or contradicted in any draft.

1. The POV character is **Maren Voss**, female, age 46.
2. Maren is the lighthouse keeper at **Harrow Point Lighthouse** and the sole occupant of the island.
3. Her husband **Einar Voss**, the previous keeper, drowned in a storm inspection in **October 1942**. Maren has held the post since **March 1943**.
4. The story is set in **November 1953**.
5. The bottle is a real, physical glass bottle, sealed with wax, found wedged in the basalt tide-rocks at the foot of the iron stair on the eastern cliff, **above the high-tide line**, on a morning after a southwest gale.
6. The message inside the bottle is written in **Maren's own handwriting**. She recognizes it immediately and without doubt.
7. **Maren tells no one** — not Cal Dresh, not the radio, not the log — about the bottle or the message.
8. The message describes specific events that occur during the story's timeframe. Those events occur as described.
9. The story's final action is **Maren writing the message** that will go into the bottle, closing the causal loop.
10. **No supernatural mechanism** is offered, sought, or implied by any character or by the narrative voice. The impossibility of the object is noted by no one aloud.
11. The supply tender is the ***Morven***; its operator is **Cal Dresh**, age 58, who calls every two weeks.
12. The lamp uses a **third-order Fresnel lens on a mercury float**, driven by a **clockwork drum Maren winds every four hours**.
13. The story comprises **three chapters**, each approximately **1,000 words**.
14. The story is told in **third-person limited past tense**, tightly bound to Maren.
