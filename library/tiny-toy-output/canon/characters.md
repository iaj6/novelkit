# Characters

## Maren Voss — POV Character
**Role:** Lighthouse keeper, Harrow Point. Sole island occupant.
**Age:** 46
**Appearance:** Broad-shouldered, weather-browned, hands that look older than her face. Short gray-brown hair kept under a wool watch cap outdoors. Burns in summer (irrelevant in November, but she remembers it).

**Background:** Maren's husband Einar Voss held the Harrow Point post from 1938. In October 1942, during a gale inspection of the eastern mooring, he went off the iron stair and into the sea. His body came in two days later on the shingle. Maren, then 35, was the only person on the island. She completed the log entry, kept the lamp, and sent a radio message to the Board. When the Board sent a temporary replacement she did not leave. She sat the keeper's examination and received her own certificate in March 1943. She has held the post for eleven years.

**Inner Drives:** Maren does not want to be surprised. She has built a life organized around known quantities — weather, fuel, the four-hour cycle — because the surprise of Einar's death was sufficient. What she has not examined, and the bottle will force her to examine, is the degree to which she stayed on the island not for duty but because leaving would require deciding what to want next. Her deeper drive: to find evidence that her life has a shape she could not see from inside it — that staying was not surrender but direction.

**Voice and Manner:** Terse. Functional declarative sentences. She does not volunteer information and does not encourage questions. Emotion surfaces as action: when unsettled she cleans, re-checks, records. She trusts the barometer over her joints and instruments over intuition. She reads the log the way other people read diaries.

**Relationship to the Bottle:** She recognizes her handwriting immediately and without doubt — the particular way she writes the letter *r*, the hard stop at the end of each sentence. The recognition is worse than if the handwriting had been a stranger's. She does not speak the word *impossible* aloud. She puts the bottle on the logbook table and looks at it while the kettle boils.

## Cal Dresh
**Role:** Mate and de facto captain of the supply tender *Morven*. Maren's fortnightly human contact.
**Age:** 58
**Appearance:** Thick neck, a short-billed cap worn in all weathers, hands so calloused they look carved.

**Background:** Has run the Harrow Point supply route for nine years, through the previous Board contractor. Knows Maren's stores and requirements well enough to load the *Morven* without a list.

**Inner Drives:** Practical, incurious by temperament. He is not without feeling — he noticed when Maren's stores of tea rose sharply in the winter of 1949 and said nothing, which is itself a form of care. He does not pry because he does not believe he has the right to.

**Voice and Manner:** Comfortable silence. He talks about weather, the engine, other islands, a boat he is repairing. He does not ask personal questions. He would say "all right then" rather than "goodbye." He accepts a mug of tea when it is offered and does not linger.

**Relationship to Maren:** The closest thing she has to a human tether. She would not say they are friends. He is the person she would call on the radio if something went genuinely wrong, and she has not yet needed to.

**Function in the Story:** Cal arrives in Chapter 2 with the fortnight's supplies. Maren does not tell him about the bottle. The scene establishes what she chose not to say.

## Einar Voss (Absent — Referenced)
**Role:** Maren's late husband. Former lighthouse keeper.
**Died:** October 1942, drowned in a storm inspection.
**Function:** Einar's absence is the stone the lighthouse is built on. He is not mourned in the story in any explicit way — Maren does not think about him directly — but his presence is in the post she holds, the log entries she keeps in the same format he used, and the iron stair down the cliff face.
