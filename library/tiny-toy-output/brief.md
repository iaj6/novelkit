# Brief

## Premise

A lighthouse keeper on a remote rocky island finds a sealed glass bottle washed up on the rocks one morning. Inside is a folded message — but the handwriting is her own, written in a future she has not yet lived. She tells no one. She watches the days, the weather, and the rotation of the lamp, and the future the message describes begins to press back through the present.

## Length and shape

- Target length: ~3,000 words
- Number of chapters: 3

## Voice

Third-person limited past. Restrained, slightly melancholic. The keeper's interior life unfolds through weather, light, and ritual rather than introspection.

## Constraints

- No supernatural mechanism is explained.
- The bottle and the message are real, physical objects — not dreams.
- The story ends with the keeper writing the message that appears in the bottle.
