# Chapter 1 — The Finding

**One-line summary:** The morning after a southwest gale, Maren finds a sealed bottle in the tide-rocks, reads a message written in her own hand, and tells no one.

---

## Logistics

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| POV | Maren Voss |
| Tense | Simple past |
| Setting | Harrow Point island: iron stair, boulder shelf, keeper's cottage |
| Time | Pre-dawn to mid-morning, a Tuesday in early November 1953 (the day after the gale) |
| Target words | ~1,000 |

---

## Goal

Maren's task is her ordinary post-storm check: descend the iron stair, inspect the cliff drain and mooring ring, return and log the result. She wants nothing except to confirm the island is intact.

---

## Obstacle

The bottle exists. It is not supposed to be there — not in the sense of the supernatural, but in the practical sense that it is an object requiring a decision: open it, or don't.

---

## Action Beats

1. **04:00 lamp-winding.** Maren winds the clockwork drum in the dark, three minutes, notes the backing wind in the log: *SW gale overnight, backing W by 03:00, now light NW, sea running, clear*. No comment on the storm itself.

2. **First light descent.** She pulls on her oilskin, takes the torch, unlatches the cliff gate, and descends the iron stair. The stair is slick. She holds the rail with both hands. Swell still grinding on the boulder shelf below.

3. **Cliff drain and mooring ring checked.** Both clear. She notes this without writing anything yet; she will record it upstairs. She turns to go back.

4. **She sees the bottle.** Wedged in a crack in the basalt, two meters from the stair foot, above the tide line — a green glass bottle, stoppered and sealed with wax the color of old candle tallow. She picks it up. It is heavier than she expected. The wax is intact.

5. **She brings it up.** She carries the bottle inside her oilskin rather than her hand, not out of sentiment but because the stair rail needs both hands. She sets it on the logbook table. She takes off her oilskin. She fills the kettle.

6. **She opens it.** She cuts the wax seal with her pocketknife — steady, careful, the way she would unseal a Board notice. She pries the cork. Inside: a tightly rolled paper, dry. She unrolls it on the table.

7. **She reads the message.** Three short lines and a closing instruction. She recognizes the handwriting before she has read a word — the particular form of the letter *r*, the hard full-stops, the slight leftward lean of the capital T. The words are hers. She reads to the end. She reads it again.

8. **She does not speak.** The kettle comes to a boil. She does not move toward it for a moment. Then she makes the tea.

9. **The message's lines are not reproduced verbatim here.** The reader perceives: (a) three specific future predictions, (b) a closing instruction to write and seal this message and place the bottle in the crack. The first line mentions the *Morven* arriving on a Tuesday with a damaged crate. Maren knows the *Morven* is due Wednesday.

10. **Decision by inaction.** She does not write in the log. She does not switch on the radio. She rolls the message back into the bottle and puts the bottle in the drawer under the logbook — the drawer where she keeps spare lamp mantles.

11. **08:00 lamp-winding.** She winds the drum. She notes the time. The lamp turns. Outside, the sea is gray and flat in the aftermath of the gale. She stands in the lamp room for a moment longer than the winding requires.

---

## Outcome / Turn

The bottle has been found, read, and hidden. Maren has not acted on it and has not rejected it. By keeping it she has already begun to comply. The future the message describes is now something she will be watching for.

---

## New Question / Pressure Raised

Does the *Morven* come on Tuesday? If it does, what does that mean for the other two lines?

---

## Continuity Notes Introduced in This Chapter

- The bottle is a real physical object, green glass, cork-and-wax sealed, dry interior.
- The message is dry — it was not water-damaged, suggesting it did not arrive by sea in any recent storm.
- Maren stores the bottle in the drawer under the logbook, alongside spare lamp mantles.
- Maren does not record the bottle or the message in the keeper's log.
- The message contains three predictions and a closing instruction.
- First prediction: *The Morven* arrives on a Tuesday with a cracked demijohn in the forward crate.
- Wax seal color: old candle tallow — yellowish, not red.
- The chapter takes place the morning after a southwest gale in early November 1953.
