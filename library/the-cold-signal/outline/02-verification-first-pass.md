# Chapter 2 — Verification, First Pass

**One-line summary:** Elena works through the Hartmann Protocol systematically across Day 2, eliminating eleven of twelve false-positive categories; the first Varga check-in passes without disclosure.

**POV:** Elena Hartmann  
**Tense:** Third-person limited past  
**Setting:** Receiver hut (primary); main hut (briefly, for meals and radio); the path between. Full day, Day 2.  
**Target words:** ~2,600

---

## Goal
Elena wants to eliminate every explainable cause she can identify before she allows herself to reach a conclusion. This is her methodology — the Hartmann Protocol — and she will follow it whether she wants to or not. Her aim today is to be disciplined.

## Obstacle
The data resists every category of elimination she applies. Each test she runs comes back clean in the wrong direction: the signal is more organized than any alternative can account for. Rigor is working against her preferred outcome, which was to find an error.

---

## Action Beats

- **She wakes after four hours.** Late morning (no sun). The main hut feels different — she registers why: she knows something the hut does not. Coffee, standing at the galley window looking at darkness. She does not eat properly. She goes to the receiver hut.

- **The Protocol in formal operation.** Elena works through the Hartmann Protocol category by category, in the order it is written. She has used this protocol for fifteen years; some of the categories she could rule out in her sleep. She rules them out anyway. Each elimination is logged with full methodology: the test performed, the instrument readings, the comparison data. It is slow. It is meant to be slow.

- **Categories eliminated in sequence.** The chapter tracks this work at Elena's pace — not rushed, not compressed. The reader sees enough of the technical work to understand what she is doing and why each elimination matters. Categories: local RF interference (antenna coupling), satellite pass artifacts, aircraft transponder, equipment oscillation/resonance, ionospheric reflection of terrestrial broadcast, solar/stellar natural emission, pulsar signature, magnetospheric event. Each one: run the test, check the result, log the elimination. Eleven categories by end of day. One remaining: she needs the next day's extended comparison data to run it out fully.

- **The 0800 Varga call.** She is in the middle of category three when 0800 arrives. She goes to the main hut. The Codan, the call sign exchange. Varga's voice is warm, unhurried — he reports weather at South Pole, asks after her instrumentation (routine question, he asks it most days). She says: instruments nominal, weather passable, no unusual events. She says this without hesitation. She notes in her thought: *true, technically*. She asks after his health. He pauses one beat longer than usual before answering, which is just Varga's way. The call closes in under four minutes.

- **The middle of the day — discipline and avoidance.** A passage tracking how Elena manages the hours between the technical work: she eats because she knows she has to, not because she is hungry. She makes a second pot of coffee. She reads a paper she brought from McMurdo — an unrelated piece on magnetospheric atmospheric coupling — reads three pages and realizes she has not registered a word. She goes back to the receiver hut.

- **She replays the signal.** In the afternoon, between protocol categories, she allows herself one clean playback of the original recording, headphones on, eyes closed. The narration does not describe what she hears. It describes what she does afterward: she removes the headphones, sets them on the desk, and sits for sixty seconds without moving. Then she opens her log to the next blank page and continues the protocol.

- **The 1800 Varga call.** Brief — weather exchange, system status. She says nothing. Varga says the aurora was visible at South Pole last night, asks if she saw it at her latitude. She says she was inside most of the night. He says: *Hartmann, you should look up sometimes.* A gentle joke; she allows a small sound that is not quite a laugh. The call ends. She stands in the radio room a moment after the frequency clears.

- **End of day: eleven categories eliminated, one pending.** She logs the day's work. The protocol demands comparison data over a second observation window before she can formally close category twelve. She sets the instruments to record overnight. She goes to the main hut. She does not read. She lies in her bunk with the lights off and thinks about the structure of the signal without letting herself draw conclusions — the way you can look directly at something without naming it.

---

## Outcome / Turn
Eleven categories eliminated. One remaining. Elena has followed the protocol exactly and it is doing its job, which means it is cornering her. The 0800 call has passed cleanly — she said nothing false; she said nothing complete. The silence in that call is now a pattern, even if only she knows it.

## New Question Raised
What is left to check? And if category twelve closes the same way the first eleven did — what then?
