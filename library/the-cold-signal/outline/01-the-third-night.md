# Chapter 1 — The Third Night

**One-line summary:** Elena receives the forty-seven-second signal, runs initial verification through the night, and makes three copies before dawn — holding the knowledge alone for the first time.

**POV:** Elena Hartmann  
**Tense:** Third-person limited past  
**Setting:** Receiver hut, Halvorsen Station; then the path between huts; then the main hut briefly. Night, approximately 2100–0500. Day 1 of the story.  
**Target words:** ~2,800

---

## Goal
Elena is doing routine overnight monitoring — a thing she has done hundreds of times — when the signal arrives. Her goal at chapter's start is simply to complete the observation window without incident.

## Obstacle
The signal is not an incident she knows how to handle by existing procedure. Every protocol she has was built for the moment *before* — for discrimination and verification. Nothing in the protocol covers what to do when verification succeeds.

---

## Action Beats

- **Open on routine.** Elena in the receiver hut, 2100. The particular quality of the receiver hut at night: the chart recorder's stylus, the MicroVAX's drive hum, the warm-electronics smell. She is writing in her field log. Everything is normal. Establish the sensory world of this space — the one lamp, the green instrumentation glow, the specific panel that vibrates at this hour.

- **The signal arrives.** 47 seconds beginning at 21:14:07 local. The chart recorder stylus deviates. She sees it before the instrumentation registers the anomaly formally. She watches it happen. The narration does not race ahead of her — she watches, she notes the time, she does not yet move.

- **First response: methodological.** She does not react. She leans forward and checks the front-end electronics manually — connector seats, IF chain, gain settings. All nominal. She checks the chart recorder feed. The trace is on the paper. She replays the digital recording from the MicroVAX buffer. It is there. She plays it again. She writes: *21:14:07. 47s. Source bearing logged. Anomalous. See tape.* She does not write more yet.

- **She makes the copies.** She writes a 9-track backup from the MicroVAX — labels the tape with the date and time in her own hand, sets it in the fireproof box. She prints a second paper chart from the buffer. She notes in her log: three copies extant. She knows the number and location of all three.

- **The verification begins, first pass.** She runs through the most obvious terrestrial sources from memory: satellite pass, aircraft transponder, local equipment oscillation. All eliminable within the first ninety minutes on instrumental grounds. None of them have this duration, this structure, this bearing. She writes each eliminated source in the log with its grounds for elimination.

- **She walks to the main hut.** At 0130 she goes to the main hut for coffee. The walk: 60 meters along the orange guide rope. The cold, the stars, the Milky Way, the breath cloud in the headlamp beam. She does not hurry. She makes instant coffee. She stands in the galley and holds the mug. She does not call Varga. She does not turn on the fax. She goes back to the receiver hut.

- **She stays up through the night.** Second and third review of the recording. She writes careful notes about the signal's internal structure — duration, frequency parameters, what makes its organization anomalous relative to any known source — without allowing herself the word she is circling. By 0400 she has filled four pages of her field log. She is precise about what the data shows. She does not yet write down what the data means.

- **Dawn (no sun — instrument dawn).** At 0500 the overnight observation window ends. She shuts down non-essential equipment per routine. She does not shut down the MicroVAX. She logs the end of the window. She looks at the fireproof box where the tape is stored. She goes to sleep in the main hut with her field log on the desk beside her bunk.

---

## Outcome / Turn
Elena ends the chapter having received an unambiguous signal, made three copies, begun verification, and told no one. She has not yet formed the decision into language — but she has already chosen not to call Varga, which is itself a choice. The signal exists. She is the only person who knows.

## New Question Raised
What does verification look like when you already suspect you know the answer? Can she trust the protocol when the stakes are this large?
