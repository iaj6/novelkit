# Chapter 3 — Elimination Complete

**One-line summary:** Elena eliminates the final alternative and reaches certainty; the three options name themselves in her mind and she transmits nothing.

**POV:** Elena Hartmann  
**Tense:** Third-person limited past  
**Setting:** Receiver hut (morning and early afternoon); main hut (evening). Day 3.  
**Target words:** ~2,600

---

## Goal
Elena wants to close the twelfth category and have a clean result — ideally an elimination, a false positive, a known source. She is not hoping for confirmation. She is hoping for an error she missed.

## Obstacle
The overnight comparison data closes category twelve the wrong way. There is no error. The signal is exactly what it cannot be.

---

## Action Beats

- **She collects the overnight data at 0600.** The chart recorder has been running. She pulls the overnight log from the MicroVAX. She is careful. She checks calibration first before she reads the data, because she trained herself to check calibration first and the training holds. Calibration is clean. She reads the data.

- **Category twelve: elimination fails.** The final alternative — a class of naturally occurring, imperfectly understood deep-space radio phenomena that can in rare cases produce brief ordered sequences — would require a specific spectral signature that is absent from the signal. Elena runs the comparison. It is absent. Category twelve does not account for what she recorded. She logs: *Cat. 12 eliminiated. No known natural or terrestrial source accounts for received signal. Verification complete.* She writes the date and time. She writes her initials: E.H.

- **She sits with the printout.** She takes the paper chart from the fireproof box and lays it on the desk. The trace is 47 seconds long. She looks at it without a magnifier, then with one. The narration does not describe what the trace shows. It describes Elena looking at it the way a person looks at a thing they already knew but needed to see in their hands.

- **The 0800 call.** She is at the radio on time. Varga's voice, the weather report, the system status exchange. She says: instruments nominal. He asks if she had a good night. She says the overnight run was clean. This is true. It is the most precise truth she can offer and the least complete. The call is four minutes. She hangs up and does not move for a long moment.

- **The three options come into view.** Not as a dramatic realization — as a simple accounting. Back in the receiver hut, Elena sits at the desk and in her mind the options organize themselves the way a calculation organizes: *Transmit. Preserve. Destroy.* She does not write them down yet. She thinks about each one the way she would think about a hypothesis: what does it require, what does it produce, what does it cost. She gives each one its full weight. She does not reach a conclusion. She registers that she has not immediately reached for the radio, which is itself a datum.

- **She makes coffee and eats.** A beat of physical ordinariness in the middle of the largest thing she has ever experienced. She makes two pieces of toast in the main hut. The toaster is too slow at this altitude and the bread comes out warm rather than toasted. She eats it anyway. The radiator pipes tick. The generator hum is steady.

- **She checks the fireproof box.** Back in the receiver hut: she verifies the tape is seated correctly, the label is legible, the box is latched. She goes to the MicroVAX terminal and confirms the file is there, the backup flag is set. She checks the chart printout is filed in the instrument log binder. Three copies. All present. She writes in her log: *Three copies confirmed present. MicroVAX disk. 9-track tape (FB-1, receiver hut). Chart printout (log binder, p. 44–46). E.H.*

- **The 1800 call.** Short. Weather. Status. She says: all nominal. Varga says the South Pole instrument array had a ghost reading this afternoon, turned out to be a loose cable at the back-end. He laughs about it — a loose cable, after all that. Elena says: it's always a loose cable. Another almost-laugh between them. The call ends. She turns off the radio room light and sits in the dark for a minute before going to her bunk.

---

## Outcome / Turn
Certainty is now formal and complete. Elena has followed her own protocol to its end and it has delivered the conclusion. She has not transmitted. She has not destroyed anything. She has gone to bed. The three options are alive in her mind and none of them has won yet.

## New Question Raised
She has eleven days remaining before the LC-130. What is she waiting for? Or: does she know what she is waiting for?
