# Continuity

Hard facts. These must not be broken in any chapter or revision.

1. Elena Hartmann is sixty-four years old in June 1991.
2. She has spent approximately thirty years in radio astronomy and astrobiology research.
3. This is her second-to-last Antarctic winter season.
4. The station is Halvorsen Station, a USAP-affiliated radio observatory on the East Antarctic plateau at approximately 2,400 meters elevation.
5. The signal arrives on the third night of polar darkness, June 1991. This is Chapter 1.
6. The signal is exactly forty-seven seconds in duration.
7. Elena is alone at Halvorsen Station for eleven days following the signal's receipt, until the LC-130 arrives.
8. The story spans twelve days total across fifteen chapters. Chapter 1 is the night of the signal. Chapter 15 is the day the LC-130 lands with Elena's returning team.
9. The signal's content is never described to the reader. The narration conveys its shape, duration, internal structure, and the grounds for Elena's certainty. The content — whether it constitutes a message, and what that message is — is permanently withheld.
10. No second signal is received. No decoded message. No visions. No subsequent non-terrestrial contact of any kind.
11. Elena has exactly three copies of the signal data: (a) the primary recording on the Halvorsen Station MicroVAX hard disk; (b) a backup copy on 9-track magnetic tape, stored in the receiver hut's fireproof box; (c) a paper chart recorder printout. She knows the number and location of all three copies at all times.
12. Dr. Tomás Varga, radio astronomer at Amundsen-Scott South Pole Station, is Elena's designated safety check-in contact. Scheduled exchanges are daily at 0800 and 1800 local time on SSB HF radio.
13. Dr. Annelie Brandt is the team leader who arrives on the LC-130. She does not appear physically until Chapter 15.
14. Carol Hartmann, Elena's daughter, receives one radio call from Elena during the eleven days. Elena tells her she is sleeping all right. This is the only lie Elena tells anyone during the story.
15. The USSR exists in June 1991 and its Antarctic stations are staffed and operational. (The USSR dissolves in December 1991, six months after the story ends.)
16. Communications at Halvorsen Station are limited to SSB HF radio and scheduled thermal-fax. No email. No satellite internet. No satellite phone. No capability exists for real-time unscheduled contact with the outside world except via the HF radio during the scheduled windows.
17. The story is realistic fiction. No magic realism. No speculative technology. All physics, biology, and Antarctic logistics are consistent with what was real and possible in June 1991.
18. Elena is the only character physically present at Halvorsen Station from Chapter 1 through Chapter 14 inclusive.
19. Elena's PhD is from Caltech, awarded 1964. She is German-born, emigrated 1963.
20. David Hartmann (Elena's ex-husband) and Elena divorced in 1982 and are not in contact during the story.
