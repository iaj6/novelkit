# Story Arc

One tight line per drafted chapter, in chronological order.

- **01-the-third-night** — Elena receives a forty-seven-second structured signal at 21:14:07, makes three copies through the night, eliminates terrestrial sources in a first verification pass, and tells no one.
- **02-verification-first-pass** — Elena works the Hartmann Protocol across Day 2, eliminating eleven of twelve false-positive categories; the 0800 call passes with "no unusual events" — her first act of omission.
- **03-elimination-complete** — Category twelve is eliminated at 06:04; verification complete, initialed E.H.; the three options crystallize in Elena's mind and none of them wins.
- **04-the-three-options** — Elena writes the three options formally in her log, drafts a perfect transmission notification, puts it in the drawer unsent, and adds a fourth blank line: "Something else."
- **05-the-0800-schedule** — Day 5: Varga casually cites Elena's methodology paper; her voice does something small; she decides he noticed nothing, and is less certain of this by evening.
- **06-station-rounds** — Elena completes solo station rounds across all five structures; in the emergency cache hut she discovers she has been running orbital geometry on the signal for two days — already doing science, already decided something.
- **07-the-draft** — Elena writes a twelve-page formal analysis, reads its conclusion aloud in the receiver hut, folds both drafts together in the desk drawer unsent, and records in her log that the question of timing is not the only question.
- **08-the-fan** — The MicroVAX fan seizes; Elena repairs it under urgency, discovers she was never going to destroy the data, and draws a line through Option 3 in her field log.
- **09-the-civilian-slot** — Elena calls Carol on the civilian slot, listens well, lies cleanly when asked about sleep, and hears "I trust you" — the only outright falsehood of the eleven days, written nowhere in the log.
- **10-the-record** — Elena names her skepticism as shelter, separates career legacy from the signal's requirements, writes four pages in the log, and answers Varga's "working or worrying?" with the most honest word she's said in nine days.
- **11-something-in-your-voice** — Varga says Elena sounds like someone working through something; she says there is nothing unusual to report; his four-second pause and offered space settle the friendship into a shared, unspoken understanding.
- **12-the-protocol-final-pass** — Elena runs the Hartmann Protocol a second and final time as ritual completion, writes Option 4 explicitly in her field log, and says a quiet farewell to Varga over the evening channel.
- **13-hs-910603** — Elena writes the full signal record into the official station log (p. 47), formally labels the tape HS-910603, annotates the printout, does not transmit, and sleeps through to 0600 — her first unbroken night since Day 1.
- **14-the-last-morning** — Elena cleans and readies the station, says farewell to Varga on the final scheduled call, decides exactly how the handoff to Brandt will happen, and hears the LC-130 engines on page thirteen of the Parkes preprint.
- **15-read-from-page-forty-seven** — The LC-130 lands, the team arrives, and Elena hands the record to Brandt with seven words; Brandt reads it and says: then we have work to do.
