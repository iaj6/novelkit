## Ending Summary

The story does not end with the discovery of the city (that is Chapter 6). It ends with three incompatible choices:

- **Holroyd** cables the RGS with coordinates and a defensible account; he carries the Leica and its twelve exposures but has not decided whether to deliver them. He leaves behind the sensational framing — the sealed chamber, unopened; the photographs, withheld for now.  
- **Isobel** goes to Pisac before Cusco, to her father's house. She carries his annotated Arce account and six pages of her own notes — the beginning of a document that will eventually frame the city in its Quechua and pre-Inca context. She leaves behind the need to win the argument with him.  
- **Mariano** goes home to the Yanatile valley without stopping in Cusco. He carries only the witness he came to carry — the completed act of standing in the fact on behalf of his community. He tells the woman who keeps that knowledge what it looks like. He leaves no document.

These choices are each defensible and incompatible with one another.
