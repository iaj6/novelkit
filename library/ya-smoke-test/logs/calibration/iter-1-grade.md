## What the sample does well

- **Voice distinction is immediate and audible.** Mira is minimum-words/maximum-precision ("I know." / "Yes."), Emric is wordy and slightly defensively energetic ("I know. I said I know."), Cass states alarming facts in neutral bureaucratic tones ("The patrol route is ten minutes. He has seven left."). The three are distinguishable after two lines each.
- **The Bardugo punchline structure is working.** "he'd brought a candle, both unnecessary and very Emric" is exactly right — the setup is the description, the punchline is the appositional noun phrase. Notably, the prose does not add a sentence explaining why this is funny.
- **Chapter-ending beat is clean.** "She didn't believe him." — not a literary fade, not thesis restatement. The decision to go tonight has been made; the ending adds one beat of character-tension without resolving it. Cliffhanger-adjacent without being melodramatic.
- **Technical heist plotting emerges through dialogue.** The ward-sequencing explanation ("Trigger the lock-ward and the trip-wire in sequence — not together, sequence, within eight seconds") is delivered in action, not exposition. The reader learns the plan at the same pace the characters are confirming it.

## What the sample drifts on

- **"as if it knew better" (paragraph 2):** The lamp simile is a mild atmospheric-literary note. Not a register violation at this scale — one metaphor-personification doesn't break YA — but it is the kind of phrasing that belongs to a slightly more literary register than the Bardugo exemplar aims for. If this pattern intensifies in later chapters, it should be flagged.
- **Second paragraph setup:** Five sentences of setting/atmosphere elapse before the first dialogue exchange. The guidance's rule ("the first paragraph must begin with dialogue or concrete physical action") is technically cleared by the Novik-style opener, which is the first paragraph. But the second paragraph is 5 sentences of scene-setting before dialogue. This is minor — the guidance also allows "two sentences of setting" — but it is closer to an atmospheric-delay opening than the drafter guidance would ideally produce.

Neither drift breaks the register. Neither reads as literary adult fiction. Both are low-severity stylistic notes.
