# Calibration Sample — Iter 1
## Chapter 1 opening (~400 words)

---

Mira had accounted for three kinds of failure. Emric being three minutes late was not one of them, which meant she'd already found a fourth.

Classroom 2-F was dark. The staircase lamp at the far end of the corridor threw a pale stripe across the floor and stopped there, as if it knew better. Cass stood against the window sill, hands in her coat pockets, watching the street below. Neither of them had said anything for the last five minutes. They were both counting the same clock.

"He's three minutes late," Cass said.

"I know," Mira said.

"The patrol route is ten minutes. He has seven left."

"I know."

Cass nodded, satisfied. She had a talent for stating the already-obvious; she deployed it like a public service.

The door opened.

"I know," Emric said immediately.

"You're late," Mira said.

"I know. I said I know." He set the candle on the front desk — he'd brought a candle, both unnecessary and very Emric — and produced a spark from his sleeve. The wick caught. His face came into the yellow light. "The hearing's been moved."

Cass turned from the window.

"How far?" Mira asked.

"First bell." He looked at the candle. "Six in the morning."

Nobody said anything. First bell was seven hours away.

"Right," Mira said. "Then we go tonight."

"That's—" Emric started.

"Yes."

He stopped. That was the thing about Mira's yes: there was nothing after it. He dragged a chair from the nearest desk and sat down, which was what he did when he'd decided not to argue.

Mira spread the folded papers on the desk beside the candle. Ward maintenance logs — three terms' worth, copied one page at a time from the monitor files. She'd had access to those since first year. She hadn't told Emric about the copying. She hadn't told either of them.

"The archive has three wards," she said. "Lock-ward, trip-wire, erasure. They don't communicate cleanly. Trigger the lock-ward and the trip-wire in sequence — not together, sequence, within eight seconds. The erasure ward reads it as a double-activation error and discharges early." She tapped the third diagram. "After it fires, there's a gap. Four minutes."

"Four minutes," Emric said.

"Nobody moves without my signal. Not on fifty-nine. Not on sixty." Mira looked at him steadily. "My signal."

"I know," Emric said.

She didn't believe him.
