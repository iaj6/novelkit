# Chapter 1 — The Eleven-Bell

**One-line summary:** Three apprentices meet in a disused classroom to argue over the plan, assign roles, set the rules, and then commit — stepping into the dark corridor together as the echo of the eleven-bell fades.

---

## POV and tense

Third-person limited, past tense. Mira only.

---

## Setting / time

A second-floor classroom that hasn't been used for a full term — Classroom 2-F, east corridor. The lamp in the bracket hasn't been refilled and gives nothing. A cold yellow stub of a candle sits on the front desk, which is the only light source. Emric brought it. The room smells of old chalk and the particular cold-window smell of the North Sea in November. The eleven-bell has just rung. The patrol will pass within ten minutes.

---

## Chapter shape

**Single scene / dialogue-heavy.** Almost entirely dialogue and physical beats. One location, one continuous scene. The chapter contains no interiority passages of more than two or three sentences — the information travels through the conversation, not through Mira's reflection.

---

## Ending mode

**Mid-action / cliffhanger.** The chapter ends the moment they step out of the classroom and into the corridor — the door handle catching behind them is the final beat. They are now committed and moving.

---

## Goal

The three need to agree on the plan in enough detail that it can be executed, assign the three roles, establish the rules of engagement, and leave Classroom 2-F before the eleven-bell patrol reaches this corridor. Secondary goal: Mira needs Emric to agree to the wait-for-signal rule, because she already knows he won't keep it but she needs him to say it out loud.

---

## Obstacle

Two obstacles. First: Emric wants to skip the plan and go immediately. He has been told the hearing was moved to first bell (6 a.m.) — earlier than they expected — and this has made him reckless rather than careful. Second: Cass raises the asymmetric stakes. Her provisional scholarship is one conduct mark away from review. If she gets caught, she doesn't just get expelled — she goes back to wherever she came from. She hasn't said where that is. She is saying this not to back out but to force Mira and Emric to acknowledge what they're asking of her.

---

## Action beats

1. **Cold open in the dark.** Mira is already there when the chapter starts; Cass is already there. They are not speaking. Emric is late by approximately three minutes, which is not long enough to worry but long enough to be annoying. Chapter may open mid-thought: Mira's sardonic voice establishing the situation before Emric arrives.

2. **Emric arrives with new information.** The hearing has been moved. Tomorrow, first bell. Not mid-morning as they planned — six in the morning. This closes the window for any alternative strategy. Tonight is the only night this plan is executable.

3. **The plan is laid out, in order.** Mira explains the ward-interaction gap: the lock-ward and trip-wire ward, when activated in sequence, trick the erasure ward into discharging early. She calculated this from the ward maintenance logs she was given access to in first year as a monitor. The window is approximately four minutes after the discharge. She names the steps, counts out loud: step one, step two, step three, sixty seconds. Nobody moves on forty-nine. Nobody moves on fifty-eight. Sixty. She looks at Emric when she says this.

4. **Cass's role: the padlock.** While Mira holds the ward gap open, Cass works the mechanical lock. She has two modified iron pins in her left coat pocket — she found them two months ago, she says, after she noticed the padlock on the archive door was an old five-lever mechanism, the kind that can be read by feel. She has practiced on a similar lock she found in the maintenance stores. Emric asks how long. Cass says: ninety seconds to two minutes, assuming the levers haven't been oiled recently. They haven't. She knows this because she checked.

5. **Emric's role: retrieval.** The notebook will be in the Student Materials section, Misconduct Evidence, current year. Emric knows the archive's filing logic from the two weeks he spent in the outer reading room at the start of term (trying to understand how the school's wardwork ledgers were organized; he was reading them for pleasure, which is very Emric). He estimates five minutes to locate it, once he's inside.

6. **Cass raises the stakes asymmetry.** She names her specific risk, flat and precise: her provisional scholarship has a conduct clause. A disciplinary finding of any kind — not even expulsion, just a formal finding — triggers automatic review. She is not backing out. She wants them to say, out loud, that they know what they're asking her to risk. Silence for a moment. Emric says her name. Cass says: "I know. I just wanted you to say it." Then she says she's in.

7. **Emric's appeal — the thing that tips it.** He says: the notebook is three months of work. He was not cheating. He was doing original research into arcane resonance layering, something the coursework doesn't even touch, and Magister Aldren took it and called it misconduct, and tomorrow morning he goes in front of a panel without it. He is not asking them to break rules for something trivial. He knows what he's asking. He just — he needs the notebook back. He trails off. Mira looks at the candle. This is the moment she stops calculating and decides.

8. **The rule, restated.** Mira says: nobody moves without her signal. Not when the ward gap fires, not when Cass opens the lock, not when they're inside. Her signal only. She looks at Emric. He says: "I know." She does not believe him, and she does not say so, and both of them know she doesn't believe him.

9. **They go.** Mira blows out the candle. Cass opens the door a precise inch and reads the corridor by the ambient light from the staircase lamp at the far end. She nods. They file out.

10. **Ending beat.** The classroom door swings shut behind them. The handle catches with a small, final click in the silence. Mira counts four seconds listening. Nothing moves. She starts walking. The chapter ends.

---

## Outcome / turn

They are committed and in motion. The hearing is in seven hours. They have approximately forty minutes of usable darkness.

---

## New question or pressure raised

Will Emric actually wait for Mira's signal? (He said he would. He will not.) And: what did Cass mean by "wherever she came from" — the thing she didn't say?

---

## Continuity notes introduced in this chapter

- The hearing has been moved to first bell (6 a.m.) — earlier than the original "tomorrow morning" estimate
- Emric's notebook contains original research into arcane resonance layering, not coursework
- Cass's provisional scholarship has a conduct clause: a formal disciplinary finding triggers automatic review
- Cass found and modified two iron pins approximately two months ago, after noticing the archive padlock type during a building survey
- Cass has practiced on a similar five-lever padlock found in the maintenance stores
- Emric knows the archive's filing logic from time spent in the outer reading room at the start of term
- The classroom (2-F) is identifiable and pre-established as unused; Mira chose it
- Nobody moves without Mira's signal: this rule is established aloud and agreed to

---

## Notes for the drafter

- The chapter is almost entirely dialogue; lean into it. The mechanics of the plan must be clear enough that when they fail in Chapter 2, the failure is legible.
- Cass's stakes-asymmetry beat is emotionally important but should be rendered in flat affect — she is not emotional about it, which makes it land harder.
- The Mira/Emric dynamic: she does not trust him to wait for the signal, but she doesn't say so. The reader should feel this clearly without Mira narrating it.
- The chapter opens in darkness (no working lamp) — the only light is the candle Emric brought. Use this atmospherically but briefly.
- The ending should feel like a door shutting behind them — literally and structurally.
