# Chapter 3 — The Passage

**One-line summary:** Escaping through the service passage while Prefect Hale's patrol converges on the east wing, Mira tells the others what the Registry says, they argue what to do with it, and Mira tucks it inside her coat as the midnight bell rings.

---

## POV and tense

Third-person limited, past tense. Mira only.

---

## Setting / time

Three locations in sequence, treated as one continuous scene: (1) the sub-basement passage behind the tapestry; (2) the east wing staircase and stairwell landing; (3) the service passage (cold, narrow, stone — a drainage-era corridor that runs beneath the ground floor, bypassing the east wing entirely). The chapter ends in the ground-floor corridor at the foot of the dormitory staircase. Time: approximately 11:30–midnight.

---

## Chapter shape

**Action-heavy with interiority woven in.** The physical action is continuous — they are always moving, always listening. The interiority is not reflective pausing; it is Mira's thinking running alongside her body, the way someone solves a problem while running. No chapter breaks or sections. The movement through the three spaces provides natural pacing variation: the sub-basement passage is tense and slow; the staircase is fast; the service passage is the argument scene.

---

## Ending mode

**Unresolved decision.** The chapter ends on the act of Mira's choice — she puts the Registry inside her coat and starts climbing the dormitory stairs — not on its consequences. The midnight bell rings on the third step. The book ends. We do not see whether they escape. We do not see the hearing. We do not see what she does with the Registry. The ending is a door held open, not a door swinging shut.

---

## Goal

Get from the archive to the dormitories before Hale completes her sweep of the east wing. Secondary, unspoken goal: decide what to do about the Registry before they separate — because once they're back in their respective rooms, the decision hardens into inaction.

---

## Obstacle

Three interlocking obstacles:
1. **Hale is in the east wing.** The direct route to the dormitories goes through the east wing upper corridor. They cannot use it.
2. **The service passage adds time.** The safer route (sub-basement → service passage → ground floor) is longer and Mira has to recalculate on the fly. They're not out of time, but the margin is shrinking.
3. **Emric, upon learning what the Registry says, wants to stop moving.** He needs a moment that they cannot afford. The dynamic of the chapter is: Cass keeps moving, Mira keeps moving and talking, Emric has to be pulled forward.

---

## Action beats

### Segment 1: Sub-basement passage (beats 1–3)

1. **Out of the archive and into the passage.** Cass relocks the archive door's padlock — she cannot leave it visibly unlatched; the pause is three seconds, not a debate. The sound from above (Hale's lantern chain) is still methodical, one floor up, but it is getting no further away. They move back through the passage toward the tapestry at the east end.

2. **Mira recalculates.** While they move, Mira is running the timing: they had twenty-six minutes when they left the archive. The direct route — east wing stairs to the second floor, dormitory corridor — is ten minutes. But Hale is in the east wing. If Hale comes down and finds the tapestry disturbed, they are caught; if Hale stays upstairs, the east wing route is still viable but involves passing her. Mira calls it without waiting to be asked: service passage. Slower. Safer. She takes the lead past the tapestry, past the east staircase, into the narrower south passage that runs toward the maintenance stores.

3. **The lantern chain goes quiet.** Just before they reach the service passage entrance (a low iron door, unlocked, that Cass has opened before), the sound from above stops. This is worse than hearing it. Silence means Hale has entered a room, not a corridor. They cannot know which room. They go through the iron door. Cass pulls it shut behind them.

### Segment 2: The staircase (beats 4–5)

4. **Up through the east staircase briefly.** To reach the service passage from the sub-basement, they must ascend one floor on the east staircase — which is the thing Mira was trying to avoid. One flight, fifteen seconds, maximum speed. Emric is already taking the steps two at a time. Mira is behind him. Cass last, counting under her breath. The stairwell is dim; the lamp at the landing is half-down. Nobody is there. They reach the ground-floor landing and turn immediately left, away from the east wing corridor, into the service passage entrance.

5. **Into the service passage.** Cold stone, narrower than the archive passage, ceiling low enough that Emric has to angle his shoulders slightly. The smell of drainage and old mortar. No lamp brackets here — their shielded lantern is the only light. The passage runs approximately sixty feet before reaching the ground-floor linen corridor, which connects to the dormitory staircase. They have approximately eighteen minutes.

### Segment 3: The argument (beats 6–11)

6. **Emric asks what she found.** He has held off for as long as he physically can, which is two minutes and forty-five seconds. "Tell me now. While we walk." Mira tells him: there is a list. A ledger in the back cabinets. Red binding. She explains the Conclave Registry in four sentences — what the heading says, what the names say, what "collection" means in the context of the form, which is nothing definitive but is enough. She and Emric and Cass are all three named in the current year's cohort. Their names were reported to the Conclave before this term ended.

7. **Emric stops walking.** He is in the middle of the service passage. Cass does not stop. She says, quietly, still moving forward: "Keep walking." He does. He is gripping his notebook with both hands. Mira watches him from the side — his jaw, the line of his shoulders. She cannot know what he's feeling, but she can see that his hands aren't steady.

8. **Cass asks the practical question.** About twenty feet into the passage, Cass says: "What are we going to do with it." She means the Registry — the ledger Mira is still carrying. Not a question. A statement shaped like a question. Mira understands she is not asking whether to decide; she is asking Mira to say the thing Mira has already decided.

9. **The disagreement.** Emric: "Burn it. We burn it in the fireplace in the common room and there's no evidence." Mira: "The Registry lists forty years of students." Emric: "That's not our problem right now." Cass, flat: "It was someone else's problem every year for forty years. Now it's ours." A pause. Emric shifts: "If we take it, they know someone broke in. If we burn it, they know someone broke in. At least if we burn it we can't be caught carrying it." Mira: "If we burn it, we are the only people who know what was in it." Another pause. This is the beat neither of them can walk away from: burn it and they erase not just the evidence against the school but the only record of what happened to all those students. Mira says this. Quietly. Not as an argument. As a fact.

10. **Cass does not weigh in on the decision.** She says: "Twelve minutes." That is all she says. She keeps moving. This is the signal that the argument has run out of passage.

11. **Emric's last line.** He says: "Whatever you decide, Mira." He says it without looking at her. It is not permission; it is a transfer of weight. He is giving her the decision to make and they both know it. He holds up his notebook briefly — one-handed, in the lantern light — and then puts it back inside his coat.

### Segment 4: The ground floor and the decision (beats 12–16)

12. **Emergence into the linen corridor.** The service passage ends in a low door that opens into the ground-floor linen corridor — warm compared to the passage, smelling of sizing and lamp oil. The dormitory staircase is visible from here, fifteen feet away. Emric is already at the foot of it. Cass is beside Mira. They have perhaps eight minutes before the midnight bell. They are going to make it.

13. **Mira stops.** She does not realize she has stopped until she notices Cass is no longer moving either — she stopped when Mira stopped, without comment. Emric is at the foot of the staircase, one hand on the banister, looking back. The Registry is in Mira's hands.

14. **The choice, rendered.** Three options, as Mira holds them, each with its shape: (a) Put it down. Set it on the corridor floor. Someone finds it in the morning — maybe the school retrieves it quietly, maybe the Conclave never knows there was a problem. The safest option for all three of them. The one that costs the most. (b) Burn it. The information is gone. So is every name in the previous forty years. So is any proof. (c) Take it. It goes inside her coat. The hearing is in five hours. She doesn't know what she does with it after that — who she tells, whether she tells anyone, whether anyone would believe her, what it means for the next cohort and the one after. She knows two things: it is the only copy, and she is the one holding it.

15. **The decision.** She folds the Registry across its spine once — firmly, precisely, the way she folds anything she means to keep — and puts it inside her coat, against her body, buttoning the top button to hold it flat. She looks at Emric. He holds up the notebook once, a small gesture. She walks to the staircase.

16. **The midnight bell.** She puts her foot on the third step and the midnight bell begins to ring. The sound comes through the walls — the gatehouse tower, one floor up and a hundred feet away, its clapper striking once, twice. The chapter ends on the bell. Not before it resolves. Not after. On it, as it is happening, as Mira is mid-step on the dormitory stairs with the Registry inside her coat and the hearing five hours away.

---

## Outcome / turn

Mira has taken the Registry. She has not destroyed it. She has not left it. The notebook is in Emric's coat. All three are, presumably, about to reach their dormitories before the midnight patrol can reach them. None of this is confirmed. The book does not show the confirmation.

## Continuity notes introduced in this chapter

- The service passage: a sub-basement to ground-floor route, runs approximately sixty feet, exits into the ground-floor linen corridor; unlocked iron door; no lamp brackets; drainage-era construction
- Mira makes the routing call (service passage over east wing) at the tapestry exit without discussion
- Emric is told the contents of the Registry while moving through the service passage; Cass hears simultaneously
- Emric and Mira disagree on whether to burn the Registry; Cass declines to decide, cites time
- Emric transfers the decision to Mira explicitly ("Whatever you decide")
- Mira folds the Registry and places it inside her coat at the foot of the dormitory staircase
- The midnight bell rings while Mira is on the third step of the dormitory staircase: this is the final beat of the story
- Chapter 3 ends on this beat. The hearing, the escape, and the consequences of the Registry are not shown.

## Notes for the drafter

- The argument in the service passage is the chapter's emotional core. It should move fast — they are walking while they talk — and none of the three should be wrong. Emric's "burn it" argument is coherent. Mira's "it's the only record" is also coherent. Cass's non-answer is the most honest of all three. Don't write the argument so that Mira is obviously right.
- The decision beat (beats 13–15) is the chapter's only moment of near-stillness, and it should be brief. Three options, named, then a decision. The folding gesture is physical characterization: Mira does not hold things loosely.
- "Whatever you decide, Mira" from Emric is the most important line in the chapter. It transfers moral weight. It should land and not be explained.
- The midnight bell ending is not atmospheric ornamentation — it is structural. The bell has been the chapter's governing clock throughout. It ending on the bell is the book ending on its own mechanism.
- Do not add a sentence after the bell rings. The chapter ends mid-action, mid-step, mid-bell. The reader closes the book in the silence between the first strike and the second.
- The Registry's physical presence should be consistent: it starts under Mira's arm, ends inside her coat. Readers should feel the weight of it throughout.
- Cass's economy of words reaches its peak here. Her two speaking moments in this chapter are: "Keep walking" (to Emric) and "Twelve minutes" (calling time on the argument). That's all she needs.
