# Brief

## Premise

Three sixteen-year-old apprentices break into the locked archive of their magic school to steal back a notebook one of them was unfairly accused of cheating with. They have one hour between bell rounds. The archive turns out to contain something the school's been hiding — and the youngest of the three figures out what it is before the others, which becomes a problem.

## Length and shape

- Target length: ~7,500 words
- Number of chapters: 3

Three short chapters: planning the break-in, executing it, getting out. Tight episodic structure. Each chapter is one location and roughly one act.

## Voice

Third-person limited past, primary POV is **Mira** (16, the youngest, the protagonist). Sharp, fast, dialogue-forward. Banter between the three apprentices. Concrete sensory detail over abstract reflection.

## Audience and register

- **Target audience age range**: 13–15 / YA
- **Voice pace**: fast
- **Reading-grade target**: 7th–8th grade
- **POV count intent**: one anchor protagonist (Mira); the other two apprentices are vivid but seen through her
- **Chapter-ending convention**: cliffhangers or mid-action; never a literary fade

## Constraints

- **YA-readable.** No graphic content. Stakes are real (expulsion, dangerous knowledge, the school hides something) but the prose stays propulsive.
- **No grimdark register.** The three apprentices are smart, funny, scared, competent. They bicker. They have a plan. The plan goes mostly wrong in interesting ways.
- **Mira is the protagonist.** Even though the other two are vivid, the reader experiences the book through Mira. She's the smartest in the room and she knows it; the friction comes from when "smartest" doesn't equal "right."
- **No fight scenes.** This is a heist. The stakes are stealth, time, what they find, and what they decide to do with it.
- **Chapter 3 ends on Mira making a decision the reader doesn't see resolve.** Open ending — sequel-shaped, not closure-shaped.
