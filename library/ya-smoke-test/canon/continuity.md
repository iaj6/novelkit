# Continuity

Hard facts established in canon. Later drafting must not contradict these.

1. The three protagonists — Mira, Emric, and Cass (Cassia) — are all sixteen years old. Mira is the youngest by four months within their cohort.
2. Emric's notebook was confiscated by Magister Aldren and is being used as evidence in an academic misconduct hearing scheduled for the following morning. Emric was not cheating; the notebook is original arcane research.
3. The heist takes place in a single night, during the window between the eleven-bell and the midnight bell — approximately fifty minutes.
4. The archive is in the sub-basement of Thornwick's east wing, behind a tapestry. It has three wards on the door: a lock-ward, a trip-wire ward, and an erasure ward. The door's mechanical lock (iron padlock, not arcane) is Cass's entry point.
5. The archive contains a ledger with a red cloth binding — the Conclave Registry — listing students identified for "collection" at year's end. The current year's page contains all three protagonists' names.
6. Mira finds and reads the Registry before telling Emric and Cass what she has found. This is the central dramatic fact of Chapter 2 and the engine of Chapter 3.
7. The Conclave is an outside governing body of senior mages. The school's arrangement with the Conclave has been in place for at least forty years. No further details about the Conclave's purpose are resolved within this story.
8. At the close of Chapter 3, Mira makes a decision whose consequences are not shown. The ending is open and sequel-shaped. No resolution of: what happens at the hearing, what the Conclave does, whether the Registry is stolen or left.
9. No fight scenes occur in this story. The stakes are stealth, time, information, and decision-making.
10. Prefect Hale conducts the midnight patrol. She is not corrupt and is not a villain — she would expel all three by the rules she believes in.
11. The school's name is Thornwick (full name: The Thornwick Collegium for Applied Arcane Studies). It sits on a chalk headland above the North Sea.
12. POV is Mira only, third-person limited past tense, for the entire story. No exceptions.
