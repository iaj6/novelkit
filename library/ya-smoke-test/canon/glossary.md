# Glossary

Alphabetical. Later phases should add to this file as new names and terms are introduced.

---

**Aldren, Magister** — The Thornwick faculty member who confiscated Emric's notebook and filed the academic misconduct report. His specific motivations are unresolved within the story.

**The Archive** — The sub-basement repository at Thornwick. Contains administrative ledgers, academic records, and the Conclave Registry. Accessed via a passage behind a tapestry in the east wing. Three wards on the door; mechanical iron padlock.

**Bell rounds** — Thornwick's hourly bell system. A bell rings on the hour from 6 a.m. to midnight. A prefect patrol follows within ten minutes of each bell. The window between the eleven-bell and midnight-bell patrol is the heist's operative window.

**Cass (Cassia)** — One of the three apprentice protagonists. Sixteen, practical, knows Thornwick's physical systems better than anyone. Dry humor, deadpan delivery. Her full name is Cassia; everyone calls her Cass.

**The Conclave** — A governing body of senior mages outside Thornwick. Has maintained an agreement with the school for at least forty years: Thornwick identifies its three most academically exceptional apprentices each year; the Conclave claims them at year's end. Their ultimate purpose is not revealed in this story.

**The Conclave Registry** — A ledger with red cloth binding kept in the second locked cabinet in the archive. Lists every cohort of claimed students going back to the founding of the agreement. The current year's page names Mira, Emric, and Cass.

**Emric** — One of the three apprentice protagonists. Sixteen, the one whose notebook was confiscated. Passionate about the underlying mechanics of arcane systems; his notebook was original research, not cheating material. Energetic, loud, sometimes reckless, fiercely loyal.

**Erasure ward** — The third ward on the archive door. Wipes short-term memory if the trip-wire ward is triggered in isolation. See: ward-interaction gap.

**Hale, Prefect** — Conducts the midnight patrol. Thorough, rule-governed, not cruel. A credible threat, not a villain.

**Lock-ward** — The first ward on the archive door. Keyed to a specific arcane signature. Not the entry point the three use.

**Mira** — Protagonist and POV character. Sixteen (youngest in cohort). The sharpest analyst of the three; knows it; this is both accurate and insufficient. Inner drive: to matter in a way that is real, not just impressive.

**Thornwick** — The Thornwick Collegium for Applied Arcane Studies. A magic school built into a chalk headland above the North Sea. Two hundred and thirty years old. Pre-industrial material technology, arcane infrastructure. Five stories plus a sub-basement.

**Trip-wire ward** — The second ward on the archive door. Sends an alert if someone crosses the threshold without authorization. The three must disable it before entering.

**Ward-interaction gap** — The predictable but exploitable conflict between Thornwick's layered wards. When the lock-ward and trip-wire ward are activated in a specific sequence, the erasure ward fires prematurely, which disables the trip-wire for approximately four minutes. Mira calculated this from the ward maintenance logs.

**Classroom 2-F** — A second-floor classroom in Thornwick's east corridor. Unused for a full term before the heist night. No working lamp (bracket unrefilled). Has east-facing corridor windows. Mira chose it as the planning meeting point. A cold yellow candle stub left by Emric on the front desk served as the only light source during the planning meeting.

**Ward-interaction gap** — The exploitable fault in Thornwick's layered archive door wards. Step 1: trigger the lock-ward (starts 90-second reset cycle). Step 2: trigger the trip-wire ward from outside before the lock-ward resets. With both wards simultaneously active, they cancel each other's alert to the erasure ward, which fires at nothing. The trip-wire ward then requires approximately four minutes to reset, creating the gap window. Mira calculated this from ward maintenance logs accessed as a first-year monitor.

**Five-lever Hessian-pattern padlock** — The mechanical lock on the archive door's iron fitting. Approximately thirty years older than the ward system. A five-lever mechanism that can be read and opened by feel. Cass identified it during the infrastructure practicum building survey roughly two months before the heist.

**Arcane resonance layering** — The subject of Emric's original research: what happens to ward structures when wards are layered at specific intervals on the same object, including interference patterns and amplification effects. Not covered in Thornwick's curriculum and possibly absent from published texts. The notebook's contents were the actual reason Magister Aldren confiscated it.

**Solen, Prefect** — A Thornwick prefect. Overheard by Emric speaking to Aldren's assistant outside the ward records room, confirming that Emric's hearing had been moved to first bell (6 a.m.).

**Outer reading room** — An accessible section of Thornwick's archive area where students may request administrative ledgers. Emric spent two weeks there at the start of the term reading filing ledgers and thereby learned the archive's organizational logic.

**Conduct clause** — A provision in Cass's provisional scholarship agreement. A single formal disciplinary finding — not expulsion, not even suspension — triggers automatic review of her scholarship. Cass named this aloud during the planning meeting, forcing Mira and Emric to acknowledge the asymmetric stakes before agreeing to proceed.

**Lock-ward node** — The activation point of the archive door's lock-ward: a small iron boss set flush at eye height on the door's face, tarnished to near-black. Activated into scanning mode by a deliberate triple-tap.

**Secondary sweep** — A hidden feature of the archive door's trip-wire ward: a thin wash of pale blue light at ankle height, activated only after the primary discharge. Not accounted for in Mira's original ward-gap calculation. Emric's boot crossed it during the Chapter 2 entry sequence, dispatching a query pulse to the patrol system.

**Conclave Registry — Year 231** — The current-year ledger in the Registry series. Heading reads: CONCLAVE REGISTRY — YEAR 231 — IDENTIFIED FOR COLLECTION. Lists Mira, Emric, and Cass by name, with a date three months from the heist. Facing page is a pre-filled printed form bearing both Thornwick's and the Conclave's seals, marked Transmitted: Autumn Term, Year 231. Mira took this ledger from the archive during Chapter 2.

**Identified for Collection** — The phrase used in the Conclave Registry heading to categorize the three named students each year. Meaning within the story: those students are reported to the Conclave and claimed at year's end. The specific nature of Collection is not explained within the Registry text itself.

**Year 231** — Thornwick's internal year-numbering system. The current story year is Year 231 — the number appears on the Registry heading and on the transmitted form.

**Thornwick's seal** — Thornwick's official institutional seal, appearing in the upper left corner of the pre-filled Conclave reporting form inside the Registry.

**Conclave's seal** — The Conclave's official seal, appearing in the upper right corner of the pre-filled reporting form inside the Registry. Mira has seen this seal before on official placement notices from the school.

**Service passage** — A drainage-era corridor running beneath Thornwick's east wing, approximately sixty feet long, connecting the sub-basement level to the ground floor via seven stone steps cut into the passage's eastern wall. Barely shoulder-width; ceiling low enough that Emric must angle both shoulders and head. No lamp brackets. Smells of mortar, drainage, and very old clay. Exits into the ground-floor linen corridor. Accessed from the sub-basement south branch, past the maintenance stores access run, through a low iron door that Cass left unlocked during the building survey three months before the heist.

**Ground-floor linen corridor** — The ground-floor corridor at Thornwick connecting the service passage exit to the dormitory staircase. Approximately fifteen feet from the dormitory staircase foot. Smells of sizing and lamp oil. Has a working wall-bracket lamp (oil in the reservoir). Warmer than the service passage.

**Dormitory staircase** — The staircase in the ground-floor linen corridor leading to the student dormitories. Approximately fifteen feet from the service passage exit. Mira is on the third step when the midnight bell begins at the end of the story.
