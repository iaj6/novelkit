# World

## The School: Thornwick

Full name: The Thornwick Collegium for Applied Arcane Studies. Everyone calls it Thornwick. It has been here for two hundred and thirty years, which the administration mentions at every possible opportunity.

Thornwick is built into and onto a chalk headland above the North Sea — five floors of grey stone, plus a sub-basement that predates the school by at least a century. The cliff face below it is streaked white and brown. In winter the wind comes in sideways and the casement windows rattle no matter how many times they are repaired. The corridors smell of lamp oil, old parchment, and something faintly metallic that nobody ever explains.

The school is pre-industrial in its material technology (oil lamps, iron fittings, hand-copied ledgers) but runs on arcane infrastructure: wards on every door, a communication system of enchanted bell-pulls, and a heating system that malfunctions seasonally in ways that only affect student quarters. Staff quarters are always warm.

## Bell Rounds

Thornwick runs on a strict bell system. A large bell in the gatehouse tower rings on the hour, every hour, from six in the morning to midnight. Following each bell, a prefect patrol covers the main corridors within ten minutes. During the gap between the bell and the patrol's arrival, and again during the twenty minutes after the patrol has passed and before the next bell, the corridors are effectively empty. Between the eleven-bell and the midnight bell, there is approximately fifty minutes of usable darkness.

## The Archive

In the sub-basement, at the end of a passage behind a faded tapestry of a sea-battle in the east wing, is the archive. Three wards on the door: a lock-ward (keyed to a specific arcane signature), a trip-wire ward (alerts if crossed without authorization), and an erasure ward (wipes short-term memory if tripped alone). The wards are old and layered; their interactions are not always predictable. This is relevant.

Inside: iron shelving floor to ceiling, smelling of cedar oil and dust. Ledgers arranged by year on the left, by category on the right. In the back, four locked cabinets of dark wood. The light does not reach there well. The cabinets are locked mechanically — old iron padlocks, not arcane — which is the detail Cass noticed when she mapped the building two months ago and filed away for no reason she could have articulated at the time.

The archive is cold. Colder than the corridors above it. The cold is the first thing that hits when the door opens.

## Thornwick's Secret: The Conclave Registry

The Thornwick Collegium has maintained an agreement with the Conclave — a governing body of senior mages outside the school's walls — for at least forty years. The deal: Thornwick identifies its three most academically exceptional apprentices each year and reports their names to the Conclave. The Conclave "claims" those students at the end of their final year. What this means in practice, the school does not advertise. The students are not told. They receive letters of "distinguished placement" and are expected to be grateful.

The Registry is a ledger with a red cloth binding, kept in the second locked cabinet. It lists every claimed cohort going back to the founding of the agreement. The current year's page has three names.

## Mood

Fast, cold, a little claustrophobic. The school is an institution that believes in its own rules. The rules are mostly functional and occasionally wrong. The three apprentices know this in the abstract; they are about to know it in the specific.

No gothic decay, no horror atmosphere. Thornwick is intimidating the way a bureaucracy is intimidating: not because it is malevolent, but because it is large, confident, and not particularly interested in your objections.
