# Characters

## Mira — Protagonist, POV

**Age:** 16 (recently turned; the youngest in her cohort by four months)  
**Role:** The one who figures things out. The anchor. The problem.

Mira is the sharpest analytical mind in the second-year cohort and she knows it. This is stated as a fact, not a flaw — she is correct, and everyone including her teachers knows she is correct. The friction in her story is not that she's wrong about her intelligence; it's that being the smartest person in the room does not tell you what to *do* with what you find.

**Inner drives:** To matter in a way that is real, not just impressive. She gets top marks and this has always felt like proof of something. She is about to find out what it was actually proof of.

**Voice in dialogue:** Quick, sardonic, precise. She gives the minimum number of words needed to make her point, and then one extra to make it funny. She is not cruel, but she is fast, and fast can sting. She is also the planner — she is the one who noticed the ward-interaction gap, who mapped the timing, who ran the risk calculation.

**Relationship to Emric:** Genuine affection, competitive edge. She would do almost anything for him. She is annoyed that she would do almost anything for him, because it makes her less rigorous.

**Relationship to Cass:** Mutual respect, slight wariness. They are too similar in some ways (both observational, both private) and have reached the détente of people who decided it was easier to be on the same side.

**What the reader sees from her POV:** The other two are vivid, funny, and fully present — but Mira's perception of them shapes everything. If Emric looks reckless to Mira, the reader sees a reckless Emric. The reader never fully knows if Mira's assessment is fair.

## Emric

**Age:** 16  
**Role:** The one whose notebook was taken. The emotional driver. The one who would absolutely do something impulsive if Mira let him.

Emric's notebook is a working journal — not the kind of casual notes the administration suspected, but three months of experimental arcane research he was doing on his own time, not for any class. He was not cheating. He was doing something much more interesting than the coursework, and the professor who confiscated the notebook either didn't understand what it contained or understood it too well.

**Inner drives:** To build things. To figure out how arcane systems work underneath the rules. He cares more about the *how* of magic than the *results*, which puts him in consistent conflict with Thornwick's results-first curriculum. He does not understand yet that this is why his name is in the Registry.

**Voice in dialogue:** Louder than Mira, faster than Cass. He is often wrong at high volume. He is also the one who pushes forward when Mira is calculating and Cass is being technically correct about the risks. Sometimes this is extremely useful. Sometimes it is the opposite.

**Relationship to Mira:** He trusts her the way people trust whoever has the map. He also occasionally ignores her, which is when things get interesting.

**Relationship to Cass:** They bicker. It is purely affectionate. They have been at Thornwick together since the first year.

## Cass (full name: Cassia)

**Age:** 16  
**Role:** The practical one. The one who knows how the building works.

Cass has been at Thornwick longer than either of the others (she arrived a term early on a provisional scholarship) and in that time has developed a comprehensive understanding of the school's physical infrastructure: which wardlocks respond to pressure rather than signature, which corridors have cold spots that indicate drainage passages, and, crucially, which of the archive's door locks are mechanical rather than arcane. She didn't collect this information for any particular purpose. She collects information. It is what she does.

**Inner drives:** To stay. Thornwick is the first place she has been that rewards the thing she is naturally good at. She does not talk about what came before. The heist makes her more anxious than she shows because she has more to lose from expulsion than she wants to admit.

**Voice in dialogue:** Dry, exact, economical. She delivers alarming facts with the affect of someone reading a grocery list. Her humor is entirely deadpan and Emric is the only one who always laughs.

**Relationship to Mira:** Mutual respect. Cass has thought several of the same things Mira is about to say, about four seconds earlier, and has decided not to say them yet.

**Relationship to Emric:** They bicker as a form of fondness. Cass would never say this.

## Background: The Prefects and Staff

**Prefect Hale** — the midnight patrol prefect. Thorough. Not vindictive. Would expel all three without hesitation if she found them, because rules are rules and she believes this. She should feel like a credible threat, not a villain.

**Magister Aldren** — the Magister who confiscated Emric's notebook and filed the misconduct report. His specific motivations are not known within this story; whether he was following orders or acting on his own initiative is part of what Mira's open ending leaves unresolved.
