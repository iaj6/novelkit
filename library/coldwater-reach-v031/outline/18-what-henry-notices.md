# Chapter 18 — What Henry Notices

**One-line summary**: Henry moves through the school after hours, sees the petition in the teacher's lounge waste basket, and tells Marcus enough without telling him everything.

**POV**: Henry Crow  
**Tense**: Third-person limited, past  
**Setting**: Coldwater Reach High School (after hours, evening); Henry and Lillian's house (kitchen)  
**Time**: August 1944

*Exceptional chapter — target 1,200–1,800 words*

---

## Goal
Henry is doing his job: cleaning the building. What he finds is not something he went looking for. His goal, having found it, is to give Marcus what he needs to know without compromising how he came to know it.

## Obstacle
If Henry says too much he implicates himself — his access to rooms and papers is custodial, not investigative, and he has navigated that boundary carefully for nineteen years. If he says too little, Marcus walks into the hearing without knowing the petition exists.

---

## Action Beats

- 6 p.m. The school is empty — or as empty as a building gets; it never fully goes quiet, it just lowers to a murmur of pipes and settling. Henry works the building from the top floor down, the way he always has: it takes him ninety minutes on a weeknight, less if the teachers picked up after themselves (they didn't).
- He goes through the rooms with the particular unseeing efficiency of a man who has worked in this building for nineteen years and knows every room's habits. The music room's piano still open. The history room's map of Europe with a fresh pin in Normandy, and one in Florence (Edna Locke added it last week when the news came). The principal's office dark, door locked, the letter on the desk visible through the glass panel — school board stationery, a name at the top that he recognizes as a board member, first paragraph visible: *the matter of Dr. Klein's continued appointment.*
- He doesn't linger. He goes to the teacher's lounge. Three wastebaskets. The first two: corrected papers, a sandwich wrapper, the usual. The third — the one near the table where the coffee urn sits, where people stand and talk — has a sheet of paper that has been crumpled and then partially smoothed, as if someone thought better of discarding it and then decided to discard it anyway.
- He doesn't open it all the way. He sees: a header (*Petition to the Coldwater Reach School Committee*), and below it three names that he recognizes — parents of students, one of them on the school committee itself. He smooths it enough to read the first sentence: *We, the undersigned, respectfully request a formal review of the employment of Dr. Marcus Klein on the grounds of his foreign national status and the uncertain loyalties attendant thereto.* He puts it back in the basket, crumpled as he found it.
- He finishes the building. He goes home.
- At the kitchen table: Lillian has made supper; Marcus is at the table with his grade book, annotating in Kurrent. Henry sits down. Lillian brings the food without ceremony.
- After supper, when Lillian is in the other room, Henry says: "You have at least one friend on the board. Edna Locke." Marcus looks up. Henry: "And you have organized opposition. Not many people. But they have a draft petition."
- Marcus: "How organized?"
- Henry: "Three names. One of them is on the committee."
- Marcus is quiet for a moment. He says: "Do you want to tell me how you know?"
- Henry: "Not particularly."
- Marcus nods. He closes the grade book. He says: "Thank you, Henry."
- Henry moves a piece of bread around his plate. He says: "You already knew something was coming. Now you know what it looks like."
- Marcus: "Yes."
- Henry: "That's all I've got."
- They do the dishes together without talking.

---

## Outcome / Turn
Marcus knows the petition exists and has at least three names behind it, one of them on the committee. He now knows the shape of the opposition and can brace for it. Henry has done the minimum necessary and not a word more.

## New Question or Pressure Raised
Who are the three names? Will the petition make it to a formal school board vote? What will Marcus do differently now that he knows?
