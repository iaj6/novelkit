# Chapter 07 — The Log

**One-line summary**: Alone in the harbor office on a December evening, Eira finds an irregularity in her October departure log that points to Birch Macomber.

**POV**: Eira Bowman  
**Tense**: Third-person limited, past  
**Setting**: Harbor office; Eira's house (briefly, at the end)  
**Time**: December 1943 — an evening, possibly a Thursday, the town at the Congregational Christmas program

---

## Goal
Eira wants to understand what she is actually carrying — to make the thing she knows into something with a shape she can work with or set down. She goes back to the logs.

## Obstacle
The irregularity she finds is in her own handwriting, which means she cannot extricate herself from it. She made the entry or she didn't. She does not remember which.

---

## Action Beats

- Chapter opens: the harbor office at night, December, everyone else in town at the Congregational Christmas program (she has not gone; she never goes; Owen used to go for both of them). The coal stove in the corner. The tide chart pinned above the chart cabinet. The sound of the harbor is specific in winter — less traffic, the ice forming at the inner anchorage's north end, the particular knock of the mooring lines against the pilings.
- She pulls the October log books. This is a professional act, the kind she performs regularly, but tonight it is not routine. She is looking for something.
- She lists, methodically, every vessel in harbor October 7–12: lobster boats *Thistle* and *Doris V.*, both still at mooring; the Lighthouse Service ice barge *Ploughman*, anchor; coaster *Macomber*, Birch Macomber, Machias, arrived October 8.
- The *Macomber*'s departure entry: October 11, 0430. In her handwriting.
- She goes to the memory. October 11 was the storm's second day. She came in at 0700; the wind was still running at 40 knots from the northeast; the harbor was closed to outbound traffic by standing regulation. She would not have passed a departing vessel — she would have been at the dock to stop it. She does not remember the *Macomber* being there to stop, which means it had already gone before she came in. But the log says 0430.
- Possibility one: she was there at 0430 and doesn't remember. It was the second day of the blow; she had been up most of the previous night. Exhaustion does things.
- Possibility two: she logged it from a note — the harbor has no formal overnight watchman, but the pier shed has a message board. Someone could have left a note, and she could have transferred it to the log without thinking.
- Possibility three: she invented the time as a placeholder, intending to confirm later, and then didn't, because the body arrived the next morning and everything else became secondary.
- She sits with all three possibilities. She does not decide between them. She closes the log.
- What she knows for certain: a coaster does not put out at 0430 on the second day of a nor'easter unless there is a reason. Macomber is a cautious skipper. If he put out then, something made him.
- She adds a note to her personal log (not the official record): *Macomber departure — October 11, 0430. Machias. Reason for departure in bad conditions: unknown. Inquiry?*
- She goes home. Owen's oilskin coat on the hook. She makes tea; she does not drink it. She sits at the kitchen table with her hands around the cup until the tea goes cold, then goes to bed.

---

## Outcome / Turn
The irregularity in the departure log is now a defined question: why did Macomber put out in the storm's second day, and who (if anyone) put that time in the log? This is not yet evidence of anything. It is a shape — a thing that wants explaining.

## New Question or Pressure Raised
Where did Macomber go, and what was he doing in Coldwater Reach in the first place? The body came in on October 13 — two days after the *Macomber* departed.

---

## Continuity Notes
- The October log entries must be consistent with Chapter 04 (where Eira gives the same information to Lieutenant Pruitt). Same vessels, same dates.
- The Macomber departure entry: October 11, 0430. This is the irregularity. The Drafter must not resolve whether Eira made it legitimately or not — it should remain genuinely uncertain. The important thing is that she cannot explain it.
- Eira's personal log is separate from the official harbor log. The personal log is where she notes things she is not ready to make official.
- Birch Macomber: skipper, 50s, spare, cautious with money — reliable, not a risk-taker. The fact that he put out in bad conditions is what makes the departure anomalous for this particular man.
- The Congregational Christmas program: the town is occupied. Eira is alone in the office by choice. The detail is period-accurate and establishes her relation to the town's social fabric — she participates in the work of the community but not in its social rituals.
- Owen's oilskin coat and the tea going cold: both are Eira processing without processing. She does not editorialized about her grief or her worry; she makes tea and lets it go cold. That is the register.
- Drafter note: the harbor office interior should be established more fully here than in earlier chapters. The coal stove, the tide chart, the chart cabinet, the pending drawer, the two cups on the hook — render them as a working environment, not as atmosphere.
