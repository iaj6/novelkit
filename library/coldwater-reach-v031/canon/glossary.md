# Glossary

Alphabetical. Add new entries as the story develops. Entries here are canonical — spelling, definition, and usage are fixed.

---

**Ada Gile** — Postmistress of Coldwater Reach since 1928. Age 63 at story opening. Knows the town's business by trade; holds the position where information and grief about casualties concentrate.

**Albrecht Pfeiffer** — Hannah's father. Bavarian immigrant (from Straubing), arrived Maine 1922. Age 58. Has stopped speaking English at home since the war began. Dairy farmer.

**BC-1206** — Period wartime radio receiver type. Present in homes and public spaces in Coldwater Reach. The source of OWI broadcasts, war news, and the radio voice of the war's presence.

**Burleigh's** — The diner on Reach Road, Coldwater Reach. Closed Tuesdays since 1942 for lack of staff.

**Camp Houlton** — Real historical POW camp, Houlton, Maine, established 1944. The main internment facility from which satellite work details (including the Pfeiffer farm detail) were administered. The Pfeiffer detail predates the formal Houlton establishment under an earlier Bangor provost marshal arrangement.

**Camp N (Sherbrooke)** — Real internment facility in Sherbrooke, Quebec, Canada. Marcus Klein was held here as an enemy alien for approximately nine months before being released and cleared. He does not discuss it.

**Coldwater Reach** — Fictional coastal fishing town on the downeast Maine coast, positioned roughly between Machias and Lubec. Population approximately 900 in 1943. Site of the story's action.

**Eira Bowman** — Harbormaster of Coldwater Reach. Age 52. Widowed 1935. POV character.

**Emergency Committee in Aid of Displaced Foreign Scholars** — Historical organization, Cambridge, Massachusetts. Assisted European academics displaced by fascism. Marcus Klein was on their rolls from autumn 1940.

**Hannah Pfeiffer** — Seventeen-year-old resident of the Pfeiffer dairy farm. First-generation American, functionally bilingual (English/German). POV character.

**Hannah's coat** — Her winter coat, two sizes too large; formerly belonged to her brother Rolf before he enlisted.

**Henry Crow** — Chief custodian, Coldwater Reach High School. Age 56. Born Norfolk, Virginia; came north 1924. Marcus Klein's landlord and closest friend in Coldwater Reach.

**icehouse (Pfeiffer)** — Board-and-batten building on the Pfeiffer farm property, insulated with sawdust packed between walls. Renovated in late 1943 to serve as the POW bunkhouse.

**Karl Reinmar** — Youngest of the four German POWs on the Pfeiffer farm detail. Age 19. From Regensburg, Bavaria. Captured Tunisia, May 1943. Speaks no English at arrival (October 1943).

**Kurrent** — The old Viennese cursive script. Marcus Klein's natural handwriting; he has trained himself to print for the blackboard.

**Lillian Crow** — Henry Crow's wife. Age 53. Born Norfolk, Virginia. Co-landlady to Marcus Klein; treats him as a guest rather than a specimen.

**Lonzo Reed** — Coastal Patrol private assigned to the Pfeiffer POW work detail. Age 61. Born Coldwater Reach. Bad knee from a lobster-trap winch accident in 1937. The permeability in the supervision of the POWs.

**Magda Pfeiffer** — Hannah's mother. Bavarian immigrant, arrived Maine 1922. Age 54. Her youngest brother was killed on the Eastern Front, June 1942; she has been largely silent since.

**Marcus Klein** — German and Latin teacher, Coldwater Reach High School. Age 47. Viennese, Jewish. Arrived Coldwater Reach March 1942. POV character.

**OWI** — Office of War Information. Federal wartime propaganda and information agency. Its broadcasts shape the ambient language of the home front.

**Owen Bowman** — Eira's late husband. Harbormaster of Coldwater Reach 1921–1935. Drowned in the November storm of 1935.

**Pfeiffer farm** — Dairy operation three miles inland from Coldwater Reach. The site of the German POW work detail. Home to Albrecht, Magda, and Hannah Pfeiffer.

**Reach Road** — Main street of Coldwater Reach. The post office, general store (ration-book distribution point), Congregational church, and Burleigh's diner are all located here.

**Regensburg** — City in Bavaria, approximately 50 km from Straubing. Karl Reinmar's hometown.

**Rolf Pfeiffer** — Hannah's brother, not a POV character. Enlisted before the story opens; his current status (alive, wounded, dead) is not established in the canon and is left to the Plotter to determine.

**Sherbrooke** — City in Quebec, Canada. Site of Camp N, where Marcus Klein was interned.

**Straubing** — Town in Bavaria, on the Danube. Albrecht Pfeiffer's hometown. Karl Reinmar is from nearby Regensburg.

**V-Mail** — Victory Mail. Wartime letter system using microfilm to reduce bulk; letters were photographed, shipped on film, printed at destination. The standard medium of correspondence between the home front and soldiers overseas.

**Western Union** — The telegraph company that delivered casualty notifications during WWII. A Western Union messenger at the door was the universal sign of catastrophic news.

**Gervais, Petty Officer L.** — U.S. Coast Guard petty officer, Machias station. Attended the breakwater scene October 13, 1943, took custody of the unidentified body, and advised Eira that the First District would be in contact. Young (approximately 30), manages situations by managing his expression first. Not a major character.

**Macomber (vessel)** — A coaster skippered by Birch Macomber. In Coldwater Reach harbor from at least October 3 through the October storm and beyond. Eira checked its mooring bridle after midnight on October 10 — the occasion on which she saw the unidentified man at the pier end.

**Birch Macomber** — Master of the coaster Macomber. In harbor at Coldwater Reach during and after the October 1943 storm.

**Samoset** — The Lighthouse Service buoy tender that calls at Coldwater Reach approximately twice monthly. Put in around October 4, 1943.

**Roy Blaine** — Lobsterman of Coldwater Reach. One of the two who found the unidentified body on the breakwater at approximately 0530 on October 13, 1943. His smack has a persistent uneven idle.

**Eddie Dunst** — Roy Blaine's nephew (Roy's sister's boy). Was with Roy when the body was found on the breakwater, October 13, 1943.

**harbor office** — Converted pilot house at the near end of the pier shed, Coldwater Reach harbor. Approximately 12 by 14 feet. Eira Bowman's workplace. Contains: chart cabinet (left of desk, not reorganized since Owen's death), VHF set (shelf above south window), Owen's barometer (east wall, at the height where Owen hung it), two coffee-cup hooks below the barometer (left: Eira's, right: Owen's), wall peg for the oilskin, tin percolator.

**the dash** — Eira's notation in the vessel-traffic column for the evening of October 9, 1943. In her log, a dash means 'no anomalies noted.' She made the mark rather than recording the man she saw on the pier — not a written lie, but an omission marked. The structural fulcrum of Thread 1.

**breakwater iron ladder** — The iron ladder bolted into the last course of granite at the seaward end of the Coldwater Reach breakwater. Its lowest rung is eight inches above the waterline at low tide (four hours past ebb). The body was found in the angle between the seaward granite face and this ladder.

**Berta** — One of the Pfeiffer dairy cows. Named. Hannah is milking Berta when the Army truck arrives.

**Else** — One of the Pfeiffer dairy cows. Named. Milked by Hannah in the same session as Berta.

**North pasture fence** — A fence on the Pfeiffer farm's north pasture that Albrecht Pfeiffer identifies as needing repair the night the POWs arrive. He plans to assign the POW work detail to begin with it.

**fremd** — German word meaning foreign, strange, not from here. Hannah thinks this word at the end of ch02, the night the POWs arrive, without applying it to any specific person. One of her father Albrecht's words, used by him for things that arrive from outside.

**Rolf Pfeiffer** — Hannah's older brother, enlisted before the story opens. He was nineteen when he left. His former room is now Hannah's; his coat hangs on the hook by its door. His deployment location as of October 1943 is not yet confirmed in Hannah's POV (Italy by April 1944 per outline).

**Aldous Fenn** — Principal of Coldwater Reach High School. Age 55 at story opening. Has held the principal's post for twenty-two years (since approximately 1921). Runs the school with brisk administrative fluency. Treats Marcus Klein with the same efficient correctness he applies to all operational matters — not hostile, not interested. First appears in ch03 when he mentions the breakwater body in the teacher's lounge.

**Thibodeau** — Mathematics teacher at Coldwater Reach High School. Present in the teacher's lounge when Fenn mentions the body on the breakwater (late October 1943); one of those whose gaze briefly redirects toward Marcus Klein before returning to the table. No first name established.

**Calloway (Miss)** — Home economics teacher at Coldwater Reach High School. Has been at the school approximately twenty-three years — one year longer than Principal Fenn. The school's institutional memory. Present in the teacher's lounge reflex scene of ch03.

**Haverstock** — Civil defense warden of Coldwater Reach. Former hardware store owner before the county emergency acquisition program changed the nature of his business. Runs the monthly civil defense warden meetings from the school basement with organizational precision. First appears ch03.

**Gould** — Fire chief of Coldwater Reach. A quiet man who attends civil defense warden meetings with the patience of someone for whom the agenda holds no surprises. First appears ch03.

**Curtis Aiken** — Student in Marcus Klein's German class at Coldwater Reach High School. Treats German grammar as a solvable puzzle; methodical and single-minded in his approach. First appears ch03.

**good suit** — Marcus Klein's better suit. Medium gray worsted, purchased in London (Cheapside) in autumn 1938 from a tailor who measured him in silence. Worn Monday, Wednesday, and Friday — the same days as the German class. As of autumn 1943 the elbows are beginning to go. The older suit goes on Tuesday and Thursday.

**unheimlich** — German word meaning uncanny, un-homely. In Marcus Klein's usage: the sensation of being exposed in a place because one has not grown familiar enough to disappear into it — the reversal of heimlich's double meaning (familiar/belonging-to-the-house; also: hidden/secret). Marcus thinks this word at the end of ch03 to describe his condition in Coldwater Reach. Introduced ch03.

**heimisch** — German adjective: at home in a place in a way that requires no effort, no calculation, no attention. To be heimisch is to be absorbed into a place so that it no longer shows you from the outside. Marcus Klein has not been heimisch anywhere since 1937. Distinguished from merely residing somewhere or being tolerated (geduldet). Introduced ch03.

**Duldung** — German noun: toleration; being-permitted-to-remain. Passive grammar — one is geduldet by someone who decides to permit. Marcus Klein uses this word to describe his position in most of Coldwater Reach, contrasting it with his unclassified position at the Crow household table. Introduced ch03.

**teacher's lounge** — Small room adjacent to Principal Fenn's office at Coldwater Reach High School. Site of the noon reflex scene in ch03 when Fenn mentions the breakwater body and Thibodeau and Calloway briefly redirect their gaze toward Marcus Klein.

**school basement (civil defense)** — The basement of Coldwater Reach High School, formerly the mill's boiler pit, made habitable by Henry Crow over nineteen years. Low ceiling, old stone walls, voice-absorbing acoustics. Two long tables pushed together serve as the civil defense warden meeting space. First appears ch03.

**Pruitt, Lieutenant** — U.S. Coast Guard officer, First District (Portland). Approximately mid-twenties, fair-faced, deliberate manner. Conducted the follow-up inquiry at the Coldwater Reach harbor office in early November 1943, asking Eira Bowman about vessel traffic and activity on the night of October 9–10. Not a recurring major character; named for consistency across the investigation thread.

**Thistle** — Lobster smack based in Coldwater Reach. Master: Roy Blaine. At mooring in the inner anchorage during the October 1943 storm and logged in the harbor's vessel-traffic record for the night of October 9–10.

**Doris V.** — Lobster smack based in Coldwater Reach. Master: Delmar Stannard. At mooring in the inner anchorage during the October 1943 storm. Logged in Eira's harbor record for the night of October 9–10.

**Delmar Stannard** — Coldwater Reach lobsterman, master of the smack Doris V. One of the town's working fishermen. Sometimes leaves notes on the hook beside the pier shed door when his boat arrives at odd hours.

**Ploughman** — Lighthouse Service ice barge. At anchor in the inner anchorage of Coldwater Reach during the night of October 9–10, 1943. Distinct vessel from the Lighthouse Service buoy tender Samoset.

**bolted drawer** — The bottom-left drawer of the chart cabinet in the harbor office, locked separately from the cabinet body. Contains vessel registration files in paper envelopes, filed alphabetically. The key is a small brass key on Eira's ring — the only copy, duplicated in Machias in 1932 at Owen Bowman's request. Distinct from the pending drawer (upper section, where Owen's things remain).

**Hilde** — A named dairy cow on the Pfeiffer farm. Placed next to Else in the barn's milking-alley order. First named in ch05.

**south gate (Pfeiffer farm)** — The equipment gate in the south fence of the Pfeiffer farmyard. Wide enough for the tractor. Separates the barnyard from the working field to the south. Hannah uses it daily to carry the grain bucket to the calf pen. The site of the first exchange between Hannah and Karl in ch05.

**calf pen (Pfeiffer farm)** — A pen approximately thirty feet from the south gate on the working-field side of the south fence. Hannah carries the grain bucket there each morning as part of her feeding routine.

**south barn gap** — A gap between the third and fourth boards of the south wall of the Pfeiffer dairy barn, at shoulder height when standing. Looks out onto the south working yard, the south gate, and the near edge of the icehouse. Hannah uses it to observe Karl at work without being observed. Distinct from the east barn wall gap (boards 4–5, waist height, facing the farm lane and icehouse front).

**auch** — German conjunction: also, too, as well. Used alone ('Ich auch') to mean 'me too' or 'I as well' — the humblest possible grammar for placing oneself in the same category as someone else. Karl Reinmar's first substantive response to Hannah Pfeiffer; the word she turns over in her thinking for the rest of the morning in ch05. Recurs structurally in Thread 4 (The Fence), culminating in ch17.

**Da, bitte** — German phrase: 'There you are' or 'Here it is' (literally: there, please). Karl Reinmar's first words to Hannah Pfeiffer, spoken while placing her grain bucket on the gate rail in November 1943.

**Ed Larrabee** — Fisherman at Coldwater Reach, works out of the commercial float. In the afternoon of October 9, 1943, he was patching a trap buoy at the float and heard the drowned man say something 'European, maybe German.' His testimony is the basis of the linguistic inquiry that reaches Marcus Klein in November 1943 via Principal Fenn. Minor character; not a recurring presence.

**Mrs. Farwell** — Secretary to Principal Aldous Fenn at Coldwater Reach High School. Writes Fenn's inter-office notes on school letterhead. First named in ch06.

**ein Mensch ohne Papiere** — German: 'a person without papers.' Marcus Klein's conceptual frame for the unidentified man on the breakwater, introduced in ch06. Carries the weight of papers-as-legal-personhood in the Nazi-era context, where removal of documents meant removal of standing as a legal person. Distinguished by Marcus from the English 'a man without papers,' which carries only administrative inconvenience.

**staatenlos** — German: stateless; without a state that claims you; without a legal floor of existence. Marcus Klein thinks this word in relation to the unidentified man on the breakwater in ch06. Carries both the administrative condition and a physical image of being unsupported.

**Lettie Morse** — Derelict smack from which Eira salvaged a length of copper pipe in January 1936 (the month after Owen drowned). The pipe was bent into the two hooks on the east wall of the harbor office, below Owen's barometer.

**Eira's personal log** — A plain brown notebook kept in the second left-hand drawer of Eira's harbor-office desk. Not the official vessel-traffic log or harbor incidents log. Not submitted, audited, or reviewed by the Lighthouse Service district. Eira has kept it since approximately 1937 for observations not yet ready to be formalized. First active entry made December 1943, concerning the Macomber departure anomaly.

**Heimweh** — German: home-ache; the ache toward a place one has left or lost. Hannah distinguishes it from the English 'homesickness': Weh is pain (ongoing), not sickness (recoverable). Albrecht Pfeiffer has used the word in the abstract at home; it enters Hannah's interior vocabulary in ch08 as a fully absorbed German word, not flagged as foreign in narration.

**der Junge** — German: the young one. Albrecht Pfeiffer's first reference to Karl Reinmar at the dinner table (December 1943) — by category, not by name.

**post driver** — A hand-held or two-man fence tool: a heavy iron cylinder open at one end, lifted over a fence post and dropped repeatedly to drive the post into the ground. Karl Reinmar is noted by Albrecht to be skilled with one — setting the weight before the swing, not after.

**Edna Locke** — History teacher at Coldwater Reach High School. Has been at the school since 1919 — longer than Principal Fenn. Wears the same small brooch at her collar every day. She is Marcus Klein's one overt faculty ally; in January 1944 she stays after the faculty meeting to warn him privately about December board talk regarding his appointment. First appearance ch09; returns as faculty representative at the board hearing in ch24.

**ablative absolute** — Latin grammatical construction: a noun and its participle, both in the ablative case, standing outside the main clause and providing contextual information without claiming a causal relationship. Marcus Klein teaches this to his Latin III class in January 1944 and finds it genuinely interesting on its own terms. Example from ch09 blackboard: *Caesar, Gallia victa, Romam rediit.* The construction's refusal to overstate what it knows is the quality Marcus identifies as a Latin virtue.

**Thursday chess** — The weekly chess game between Marcus Klein and Henry Crow, played on Thursday evenings at the kitchen table of the Crow house under the overhead lamp. Henry sets up the board in advance. Henry wins more often than Marcus. Established in ch09 as the regular forum where Henry gives Marcus counsel. The kitchen table context — Lillian in the back room, the radio low — is the established physical setting.

**Estelle Hartley** — Skipper of the coaster Wren (Rockland). Approximately forty years old in February 1944; has sailed the Maine coast since 1927. One of three or four women skippers operating the Maine coast trade during the war years. Runs a circuit from Rockland to Eastport carrying lath, shingles, lime, and general cargo. First appears ch10, when the Wren grounds on the inner-anchorage ice-ledge at Coldwater Reach.

**Wren** — A coaster of approximately sixty feet, wooden, working trim, Rockland registry, wheelhouse painted light blue. Skippered by Estelle Hartley. Runs the Maine coast circuit Rockland to Eastport. Grounded on the ice-ledge at the Coldwater Reach mooring field's north edge in February 1944; freed by the buoy tender Samoset under tow. First appears ch10.

**Navy First District (Portland)** — The U.S. Navy administrative district office in Portland, Maine, governing naval operations along the Maine coast. Holds vessel contracts by master and registration. Birch Macomber held a contract through this office beginning at least by fall 1943, per testimony from Estelle Hartley in ch10. Eira identifies the Portland district office as the source of a paper record that could explain Macomber's movements.

**word-cards** — Small cedar rectangles cut by Hannah Pfeiffer from butter-box planks beginning January 1944 (approximately six inches by two), one word per card in pencil, English on the left side and German on the right with a ruled dividing line. Used as a teaching and communication system between Hannah and Karl Reinmar, exchanged via the fence post nearest the icehouse under a gray field stone. The system begins three weeks before ch11's main action. First ten cards: barn/Scheune, gate/Tor, bucket/Eimer, rope/Seil, fence/Zaun, stone/Stein, field/Feld, morning/Morgen, two February cards (unspecified), harbor/Hafen. English sides wear fastest from Karl's handling. Recurring physical detail from ch11 through winter 1944.

**A. Schell** — The name on a piece of mail addressed to general delivery, Coldwater Reach, received in the first week of October 1943. No person of this name lives in Coldwater Reach. The Coast Guard collected the item in November 1943, apparently knowing the name before they asked for it. First revealed to Marcus Klein by Ada Gile in March 1944 (ch12). Eira Bowman will learn the additional detail that the item was a postcard in ch13. The name connects to the unidentified body on the breakwater; its full significance is developed in ch19 (Bangor) and later.

**Pentonville Road (London rooming house)** — The address in London, England, where Marcus Klein lodged for eleven months between the fall of France (summer 1940) and his departure for Canada. One of the waypoints in his displacement sequence: Vienna → Paris → London (Pentonville Road) → Sherbrooke, Quebec → Cambridge, MA → Coldwater Reach, Maine.

**Cambridge letter** — A letter dated February 4, 1944, from the Emergency Committee in Aid of Displaced Foreign Scholars at Cambridge, Massachusetts, offering Marcus Klein a visiting instructor position in German philology at a Boston-area university. One-year appointment, renewable, with possibility of conversion to assistant professorship. Marcus carries it in his coat pocket for twenty-two days before replying (March 1944) that he intends to remain in Coldwater Reach for the present year and will write again in the autumn.

**Bureau of Navigation** — U.S. government agency (under the Department of Commerce) that issues regulatory circulars to harbormasters and vessel operators. Eira Bowman's harbor office receives Bureau of Navigation district quarterly circulars as part of its standard mail. A real institution referenced for period accuracy.

**Portland postmark** — The postmark on the A. Schell postcard (general delivery, Coldwater Reach, first week of October 1943). Portland, Maine is the site of the U.S. Navy First District office and the administrative center for Maine coastal vessel contracts. The postmark connects the A. Schell correspondence to the Portland district from which Macomber's Navy contract also originated.

**mündig** — German: the state of being of legal age, having majority. Root: Mund (mouth). Literal meaning: having your own mouth — the condition of speaking for yourself in the record without a guardian speaking in your place. Marcus Klein teaches this word to his German class on April 14, 1944 (Hannah's 18th birthday). Hannah writes it in her notebook margin: 'mündig — having your own mouth.' The word becomes a seed in her arc.

**Mund** — German: mouth. Root of 'mündig.' Identified by Marcus Klein as the etymological core of legal majority in German civic vocabulary.

**post-driving** — The spring fence-repair work on the Pfeiffer farm: driving fence posts into the softened April ground with a twelve-pound sledge. Karl Reinmar's spring 1944 work assignment. Distinct from his November 1943 south yard duty (manure hauling).

**Konjunktiv I / indirekte Rede** — German grammar: the present subjunctive, used for reported speech (indirect discourse). The unit Marcus taught in German II on June 6, 1944. The mood signals that the speaker is conveying what was said rather than asserting a direct truth. Lesson examples established in ch15.

**Rue du Temple** — Street in the fourth arrondissement of Paris, on the border of the Marais. Marcus Klein's address from October 1939 to June 1940 (eight months), shared with two other scholars. The apartment faces a courtyard; bakery at the corner; Tuesday market nearby. In the old Jewish quarter.

**Mariahilfer Strasse** — Street in Vienna where Marcus Klein's parents' building was located. Marcus made a pen-and-ink sketch of the building from memory during his first Paris winter; the sketch (with slightly wrong door proportions) is kept in his bedside-table drawer in Coldwater Reach.

**Emergency Committee letter (first)** — Physical object: the Emergency Committee in Aid of Displaced Foreign Scholars' October 1940 letter to Marcus, sent to him at the Pentonville Road rooming house in London. Promises a place in Cambridge, Massachusetts, with a position to be arranged and travel documents prepared. Signed by the Committee's director, a professor of linguistics. Marcus has kept it in the back of his bedside-table drawer since at least 1940. Established as a physical object in ch15.

**Fenn, Principal** — Principal of Coldwater Reach High School. Not given to rhetoric. Delivered the D-Day assembly address on June 6, 1944, using the phrase 'the beginning of the end.' The school's BC-1206 radio is kept in his office.

**Commander Whitmore** — Naval district inspector, rank Commander. Travels the coastal inspection circuit on the Lighthouse Service buoy tender Samoset (out of Jonesport). Professional, methodical, not antagonistic. Inspects Coldwater Reach harbor office in 1942, 1943, and July 1944. Returns in Chapter 29 to close the August Schell case by telephone. First named in Chapter 16.

**SS Ångermanälven** — Swedish merchant vessel (neutral flag) that ran a Portland-to-Nova Scotia route in wartime. Named for the Ångermanälven river in northern Sweden. Filed a Seamen's Bureau missing crew report in November 1943 for August Schell. First named in Chapter 16.

**August Schell** — German-born, naturalized Swedish national, age approximately 40. Crew member of the SS Ångermanälven. Went missing from the Portland-to-Nova Scotia run second week of October 1943. Naval district conclusion (July 1944): probable same man as the unidentified body recovered from the Coldwater Reach breakwater October 13, 1943. No security implication; maritime fatality. The name 'A. Schell' appeared earlier (as the addressee of the Portland postcard, ch12–ch13); the full name is given in Chapter 16.

**Seamen's Bureau** — Federal maritime bureau that handled missing crew member reports. The SS Ångermanälven's missing crew report for August Schell was filed with the Seamen's Bureau in November 1943.

**Adger Willits** — Lobster boat owner, berth at the south dock, Coldwater Reach. His thirty-two-foot lobster boat was rebuilt from the keel up in 1939. First named in Chapter 16.

**gray-blue folder** — The color of closed-referral or pending-review folders in the naval district filing system, as distinguished from the standard buff district forms. Used by Commander Whitmore to carry the August Schell cross-reference material.

**limited off-duty movement** — The formal Geneva Convention POW welfare provision allowing Karl Reinmar limited unsupervised movement on Sunday afternoons. Approved through the provost marshal's office in Bangor, summer 1944, after a months-long bureaucratic process. Terms: Sunday afternoons, perimeter fence walk only, within sight of the barn or icehouse at all times, Lonzo Reed present as supervising guard. Lonzo countersigned the required form. The other three POWs at the Pfeiffer farm were not granted this privilege. First enacted in practice in ch17.

**Karl's father (Reinmar)** — Karl Reinmar's father, a butcher whose shop was located near the river road in Regensburg, Bavaria. Karl's status for his father as of July 1944: uncertain — 'Was. I think was. I do not know now.' Per ch28 planning, the father dies in Allied bombing of Regensburg before the end of the war. Karl learns this and tells Hannah in ch26. No first name established for Karl's father.

**river road (Regensburg)** — The street near which Karl Reinmar's father kept his butcher shop in Regensburg, Bavaria. Referenced by Karl in ch17: 'His shop is near the river road.' The river in Regensburg is the Regen (tributary of the Danube). Approximate geographic parallel to Straubing (Albrecht Pfeiffer's hometown), which is on the Danube downstream. Not a proper street name — Karl's English phrase for the road near the riverbank.

**Lonzo's paperback** — A paperback novel (woman on the cover, title not established) that Lonzo Reed has been reading on his chair arm at the Pfeiffer farm since before July 4, 1944. Used in ch17 as the prop of his deliberate inattention: he is visibly present and visibly occupied, not watching the north fence walk. Part of the pattern of Lonzo's chosen non-observation, which develops toward the absent headcount of ch22.

**Es riecht nach Salz** — German: It smells of salt. Hannah Pfeiffer's phrase for the particular smell of the harbor at Coldwater Reach, spoken to Karl in July 1944 (ch17) when her English ran out while describing the sea. Hannah switches to German instinctively; Karl understands. Established as the moment Hannah's German enters their fence-line exchanges directly rather than through word-cards.

**Seavey** — A member of the Coldwater Reach School Committee. One of three signatories to the August 1944 petition requesting a formal review of Marcus Klein's employment on grounds of foreign national status and uncertain loyalties. Henry Crow has seen him in the school corridor on two occasions when board meetings ran past six o'clock. Given name not established. Consistent with one of the two dissenting votes in the 5–2 board retention vote (ch24).

**Miss Talmadge** — Piano teacher at Coldwater Reach High School. Teaches summer students on Wednesdays and Fridays in August. Consistently leaves the music room piano lid open; Henry Crow closes it after each session. First named in ch18.

**the petition (Klein)** — A written petition against Marcus Klein's continued employment at Coldwater Reach High School, bearing three signatures as of August 1944 (two parents of students; one school committee member, Seavey). Found by Henry Crow crumpled in the teacher's lounge third wastebasket. Full title: 'Petition to the Coldwater Reach School Committee.' Opening sentence: 'We, the undersigned, respectfully request a formal review of the employment of Dr. Marcus Klein on the grounds of his foreign national status and the uncertain loyalties attendant thereto.' The petition is the organized opposition that leads to the formal board hearing in ch24.

**Captain Lindqvist** — Captain of the SS Ångermanälven, as named in the Bangor Daily News Portland shipping column, October 1943. Appears only in the newspaper record; not a character in the story. His name is established for factual consistency if the Ångermanälven's command is referenced elsewhere.

**Bangor Public Library** — Real institution in Bangor, Maine. Stone building, good periodicals collection, holds the Bangor Daily News on microfilm. Marcus visits in late 1942 (first trip, to consult a document) and again on a Wednesday in August 1944 to research the Ångermanälven shipping record. Periodicals room has two microfilm readers. Reading room has proper tables and lamp coverage.

**the Kurrent note (Schell)** — Physical object: a small notebook page written by Marcus Klein in the Bangor library periodicals room, August 1944, in his natural Kurrent script. Contains two entries: arrival/departure dates for the SS Ångermanälven in Portland (October 7–12, 1943) and August Schell's name, nationality, approximate birth year, vessel, and date. Not a summary — a fixing of facts. Kept folded in the inside pocket of Marcus's coat. Not transcribed into any other document. Not readable without training in Kurrent script.

**Passau corridor article** — A 1943 journal article on medieval Bavarian dialect transmission in the Passau sub-Alpine corridor, held by the Bangor Public Library. Marcus requested it two weeks in advance by letter and read it in the library reading room in August 1944, making three pencil Kurrent notes on a notecard. The argument addresses high-vowel persistence in sub-Alpine dialects. Marcus found it exactly as good as its abstract suggested. Not a symbolic act — he read it because he wanted to and because it was good.

**Addendum (harbor document)** — The document Eira Bowman writes on September 14, 1944, on harbor office stationery. Headed 'Addendum to Incident Report, October 13, 1943 (Unidentified Decedent, Breakwater).' Contains her complete account of the night of October 9, 1943: the mooring check at 0130, the man on the pier, the five-minute inattention, the man's disappearance, the pier shed assumption, and the omission from the vessel traffic log. Signed E.M. Bowman, Harbor Master. Folded in three and filed in Owen's pending drawer (chart cabinet, bottom right). Not forwarded to the Coast Guard. To be moved to the permanent record in ch29 (April 1945).

**Gertrude B.** — A lobster boat operating out of Coldwater Reach harbor. At mooring in the inner anchorage on the morning of September 14, 1944. Her engine is identifiable by sound to Eira. She cleared the north mooring at 0723 that morning.

**Calvert (barge)** — A barge at anchor in the inner anchorage of Coldwater Reach in September 1944, in transit to Lubec. Deck cargo covered in canvas. Arrived on the Monday before September 14, 1944.

**Owen's pending drawer** — The bottom right drawer of the harbor office chart cabinet. Designated by Owen Bowman for pending items in 1929. Contains at the back two manila folders in Owen's handwriting: 'Lighthouse Service correspondence, 1934' and 'Tidal survey, pending, 1935.' As of September 14, 1944, also contains Eira's addendum (placed in front of Owen's folders). These files have not been removed or reorganized since Owen's death in 1935.

**Martha (cow)** — Holstein cow on the Pfeiffer dairy farm, named by Rolf Pfeiffer when he was nine and Hannah was seven. Died July 1944 of a left-quarter mastitis infection after three days of veterinary treatment. One of the Pfeiffer herd's notable animals; her absence reduces the herd to nine. Hannah chose not to tell Rolf in her September 1944 V-Mail.

**Room 6** — The German and Latin classroom at Coldwater Reach High School, upper floor, second window from the east end. Marcus Klein's classroom. Has a map of Europe with pins marking campaign progress (Normandy, Florence, etc.). Marcus sometimes stays after hours here to read.

**Rolf Pfeiffer** — Hannah's older brother; American-born son of Albrecht and Magda Pfeiffer. Enlisted in the U.S. military; stationed in Italy as of fall 1944. Corresponds with Hannah and the family via V-Mail. Alive as of September 1944. His coat, two sizes too large for Hannah, is what she wears through the story's autumn and winter months. He named the Holstein cow Martha as a child.

**headcount book** — The logbook Lonzo Reed uses for the nightly 9 p.m. headcount of the four POWs. He signs each entry L. Reed, CP (Coastal Patrol). On the October 1944 night of ch22, he writes 'present' for Karl despite Karl's absence — the document that would incriminate him if reviewed. Kept in Lonzo's coat pocket.

**Lonzo's room (Pfeiffer barn)** — A single stall in the Pfeiffer barn, partitioned with board lumber, where Lonzo Reed sleeps for the duration of the POW work detail. Contains a cot (from Bangor provost's supply), a chair and table (Pfeiffer-provided), and a small iron stove taking kindling and a split of hardwood.

**Reed's folding camp chair** — A canvas-back-and-seat folding camp chair Lonzo has used since November 1943, positioned outside the icehouse. Left rear leg sticks in soft ground. He sits upright in cold weather due to his bad knee. This is the chapter's titular object.

**HARBOR (word-card on Karl's shelf)** — The English word visible on a butter-box lid (one of Hannah's word-cards) on Karl's shelf (a wooden crate) in the icehouse. English side up at the time of Lonzo's ch22 headcount. Established as the specific word Lonzo sees on the card.

**upper pasture** — The high field of the Pfeiffer farm, above the cows' nighttime field. Has a weather-checked pine gate with a bent iron latch at its top end. The back path runs through it at an angle from the farmhouse. The stone wall at its far edge marks where the farm road becomes the ridge road.

**the back path** — Hannah Pfeiffer's route from the Pfeiffer farmhouse to the ridge road: through the upper pasture at an angle, skirting the cows' nighttime field, through the pine gate at the top of the upper pasture, to the stone wall at the field's edge.

**the stone wall (upper pasture edge)** — The stone wall at the boundary of the Pfeiffer upper pasture where the farm road becomes the ridge road. Three courses high at the east corner, lower in the middle where frost has worked it. Used as the rendezvous point and separation point in Hannah and Karl's October 1944 meeting.

**the ridge road** — The road running from the Pfeiffer farm south and west approximately one mile to the high ground above Coldwater Reach. Used by farm horses in haying season; wide enough for two people side by side; lined with maples. First named in ch22 (Lonzo's chapter) as Karl's return route; established in ch23 as the path Hannah and Karl walk to the harbor view.

**the ridge (above Coldwater Reach)** — The high ground approximately one mile from the Pfeiffer farm, accessible via the ridge road, from which Coldwater Reach harbor is visible — including the dock lights, the boats at mooring, the Reach Road buildings, the harbor light on the breakwater, and the sea beyond. Hannah has been here before; Karl sees the sea for the first time from this point in October 1944.

**face-down convention** — Hannah and Karl's code for the fence-post stone: a cedar piece placed face-down means a message (non-word-card communication); face-up means it is a standard word-card exchange. Established in use by October 1944.

**Aldrich** — Chair of the Coldwater Reach school board. Presides over the November 1944 hearing on Marcus Klein's employment. Administrative in manner; reads the petition's concerns in a flat, non-authorial voice. Uses the word 'forthright' when closing the review.

**Ansel (board member)** — Member of the Coldwater Reach school board. Runs the feed store on the south end of Reach Road. Her son graduated in spring 1942 and enlisted in the Navy. She asks Marcus about his time in France at the November 1944 hearing.

**Camp N (Sherbrooke)** — The administrative designation for the civilian internee camp at Sherbrooke, Quebec, where Marcus Klein was held for approximately nine months in 1941 as an enemy alien before being cleared by Canadian and American authorities. Synonymous with the 'Sherbrooke internment' referenced in prior chapters.

**Modern Language Association (MLA)** — Academic organization of which Marcus Klein holds membership. Stated on the record at the November 1944 school board hearing.

**Linguistic Society of America (LSA)** — Academic organization of which Marcus Klein holds membership. Stated on the record at the November 1944 school board hearing.

**Ansel feed store** — Feed store on the south end of Reach Road in Coldwater Reach, run by board member Ansel.

**Dwight Orcutt** — Selectman, Coldwater Reach. Nephew of Briggs Orcutt (fish buyer, south pier). Somewhere under forty in November 1944; not called up for military service. In ch25, implies returning veteran Howie Beal should have Eira's harbor post. Referenced again in ch29.

**Briggs Orcutt** — Fish buyer, Coldwater Reach; operates from the south pier. Uncle of Dwight Orcutt. Does not appear as a speaking or POV character.

**Howie Beal** — Former marine engineer on the lobster trap-hauler Sentinel. Returning veteran, expected back in Coldwater Reach December 1944. Invoked by Selectman Orcutt in ch25 as a candidate for Eira's harbor post. Never appears in person.

**Sentinel (the)** — Lobster trap-hauler, Coldwater Reach harbor. Howie Beal was its marine engineer before enlisting. Status (mothballed, sold, or operating) unspecified as of November 1944.

**Friedrich Reinmar** — Karl Reinmar's father. A butcher in Regensburg, Bavaria. Died December 28, 1944, in Regensburg — cause unstated in the Red Cross notification but almost certainly the Allied bombing of the city. Karl received notification through the ICRC POW mail system. First named (by implication) in ch26.

**Die Bürde trägt sich nicht allein** — German phrase: 'The burden does not carry itself alone.' Albrecht Pfeiffer's phrase, spoken once to himself in the kitchen when Hannah was approximately fourteen. Hannah recalls it in January 1945 as the structure of what she is doing in holding Karl's grief.

**Mein Vater ist gestorben** — German: 'My father has died.' The words Karl says to Hannah at the north fence on January 13, 1945, in formal careful German — not their usual abbreviated register. The plainest possible phrasing of the fact.

**Das tut mir leid** — German: 'I am sorry' (literally, 'that does me sorrow'). The conventional German expression of condolence. Hannah says this to Karl at the north fence after he tells her his father has died. She means the whole weight of it.

**Gull Street** — Street in Coldwater Reach where Henry and Lillian Crow first rented two rooms when they arrived from Bangor in spring 1924. Rent was seven dollars a month for two rooms. Whether they still live on Gull Street in 1945 is not specified.

**Ansässigkeit** — German legal/bureaucratic term for sedentariness, fixed residence, being established in a place. Literally 'being-seated' in a place, from *sitzen* (to sit) + *an-* (at/in proximity to). Marcus Klein thinks this word for the first time since 1937 as a condition that might apply to himself, at the end of Chapter 27. The word appears in formal German as *ansässig* (adjective: domiciled, settled) and *Ansässigkeit* (noun: the condition of being so settled).

**Felling** — Principal of Coldwater Reach High School before Principal Fenn. Felling hired Henry Crow as custodian when Henry arrived in Coldwater Reach in 1924. Felling's current status (retired, deceased) is not specified in the narrative.

**The Landlord's Game (chapter)** — Chapter 27 of the novel. Set in the Crow kitchen, February 1945. Marcus's chess-game Thursday with Henry, during which Marcus says 'I think I have stopped moving' and Henry responds 'Then you should reorganize your defense.' The chapter resolves Thread T2. The title refers metaphorically to Henry as the settled landlord — the man who has had *Ansässigkeit* for twenty-one years — teaching Marcus the game of those who have stopped moving.

**bleiben** — German verb: to stay, to remain. Karl Reinmar's conceptual word in ch28 for his situation: in German, *bleiben* does not require an act of choosing — *er ist geblieben* means he remained, or simply that he has not yet gone. Karl cannot determine whether staying in America after the war would be a decision or something that merely happens. The word is central to his unresolved postwar question.

**the big POW (Hannover schoolteacher)** — The unnamed large man among the four German POWs on the Pfeiffer farm work detail. He was a schoolteacher in Hannover before his regiment was called up. He reads the German Bible every Sunday morning on his bunk. He snores with a regular, familiar rhythm each night. His name is not established in the narrative.

**Karl's mother** — Karl Reinmar's mother, who died before the story begins. Karl keeps a photograph of her — taken before her death — in the wooden box under his bunk. No name, no date of death, no further details are established.

**Karl's grandfather** — Karl Reinmar's paternal grandfather, who lived in a village outside Regensburg on a road from which the cathedral spire becomes visible on the approach to the city. Not named; his current status (alive, dead) is unspecified and should remain so.

**wooden box (Karl's bunk)** — A wooden box kept by Karl Reinmar under his bunk in the Pfeiffer farm icehouse. Contents as of March 1945: the Red Cross notification of Friedrich Reinmar's death (December 28, 1944); Karl's discharge papers; his identification documents; a photograph of his mother from before she died; and the first word-cards Hannah Pfeiffer gave him. These contents are canonical.

**Friedrich Reinmar's shop (Regensburg)** — Karl Reinmar's father's butcher shop in Regensburg, Bavaria, near the river road. Features: cream-colored tile floor with a dark blue border tile running around the room's edge (original to the building); an east window through which morning light entered; the smell of blood and sawdust in the walls and tiles. Karl apprenticed there. The shop was destroyed or severely damaged in the Allied bombing of Regensburg.

**Hilde** — The lead cow of the Pfeiffer dairy herd. She is the first to go through the pasture gate when the cows go out in spring; the others follow her without hurrying.

**Maren** — One of the Pfeiffer dairy cows. Hannah milks her second, after Bess, in the morning milking order.

**Bess** — One of the Pfeiffer dairy cows. Hannah milks her first in the morning milking order.

**Eastern Maine General** — A hospital in Bangor, Maine. Its two-year nursing school program accepts girls directly out of high school. Hannah Pfeiffer considers applying as of late April 1945.

**Belanger woodlot** — A woodlot along Hannah's road from the Pfeiffer farm to Coldwater Reach, passed on the three-mile walk to school.
