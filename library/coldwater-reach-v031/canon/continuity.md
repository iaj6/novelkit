# Continuity

Hard facts that must not be contradicted in any chapter, outline, or draft. Numbered for reference. Later phases may append to this list but must not overwrite or contradict existing entries.

---

1. Eira Bowman is 52 years old at the story's opening (autumn 1943). She was born approximately 1891.
2. Eira's husband Owen Bowman, the previous harbormaster, drowned in the November storm of 1935. He held the post since 1921.
3. Eira has been harbormaster for eight years at the story's opening. She was appointed in 1935 or 1936, immediately after Owen's death.
4. Eira and Owen had no children.
5. The unidentified man's body washes up on the breakwater of Coldwater Reach after a three-day blow in October 1943. It is the story's inciting object.
6. Eira knows what she saw on the dock the night before the body came in. This knowledge is disclosed gradually across the narrative; it must be consistent across all three POVs.
7. The Coast Guard takes the body. After the Coast Guard's involvement, official public information about the man is minimal.
8. Hannah Pfeiffer is 17 at the story's opening; she turns 18 during the story's eighteen-month span (spring or summer 1944).
9. Hannah's parents, Albrecht and Magda Pfeiffer, are Bavarian immigrants who arrived in Maine in 1922.
10. Albrecht Pfeiffer is originally from Straubing, Bavaria (near the Danube, approximately 50 km from Regensburg).
11. Four German POWs are billeted in the renovated icehouse on the Pfeiffer farm under armed escort by Lonzo Reed, a Coastal Patrol private aged 61.
12. Karl Reinmar is 19 years old, from Regensburg, Bavaria. He was captured in Tunisia in May 1943 during the North Africa surrender.
13. Karl speaks no English when he arrives in October 1943.
14. Marcus Klein is 47 at the story's opening. He was born in Vienna in 1896.
15. Marcus was interned at Sherbrooke, Quebec (Camp N, enemy aliens). He was released and cleared after approximately nine months. He does not discuss this period openly.
16. Marcus arrived in Coldwater Reach in March 1942 to teach German and Latin at Coldwater Reach High School.
17. Marcus boards with Henry and Lillian Crow. Henry is chief custodian at the school. Henry is 56; Lillian is 53. They came north from Norfolk, Virginia in 1924.
18. Marcus is Jewish, though he does not announce this. The town perceives him primarily as foreign/German-seeming.
19. The three primary POV characters (Eira, Hannah, Marcus) never all meet together as a group of three at any point in the narrative.
20. The story runs from approximately October 1943 to spring 1945, ending just before VE Day (May 8, 1945).
21. The total target length is approximately 80,000 words across 30 chapters, with approximately 10 chapters per POV character.
22. There may be 2–3 additional short chapters from a fourth perspective (Henry Crow, Lonzo Reed, or Karl Reinmar). These are exceptional, not recurring.
23. No spy thriller elements. No Nazi agents. No coded messages. No submarine landings. The body's origins are mundane, not espionage.
24. At least one narrative thread must be deliberately left unresolved. The factual question of who the body was may or may not be fully answered; the three characters' personal relationships to that question must resolve.
25. Real places are used accurately: Portland, Bangor, Houlton, Machias, Lubec, Rockland (Maine); Sherbrooke, Quebec; Cambridge, Massachusetts. Coldwater Reach is fictional, positioned roughly between Machias and Lubec, downeast Maine.
26. The POW labor program in Maine is based on the historical reality of Camp Houlton (est. 1944) and its satellite work details. The Pfeiffer detail operates under an earlier arrangement with the provost marshal's office in Bangor.
27. Magda Pfeiffer's youngest brother was killed on the Eastern Front in June 1942. This is the precipitating event of her near-silence.
28. Lonzo Reed's bad knee is from a lobster-trap winch accident in 1937.
29. Ada Gile is the postmistress of Coldwater Reach. She has held the post since 1928.
30. The Pfeiffer dairy icehouse is board-and-batten construction, insulated with sawdust packed between the walls.
