## 01-the-breakwater

### Sentence-After-the-Sentence

**Cut 1** — editorial label on a self-evident action:
> "They stood back from it six feet or so, **which was the right instinct**, and they said nothing when she came up **because they understood she needed a moment and they had nothing to add to what was already visible**."

Became: "They stood back from it six feet or so, and they said nothing when she came up."
The physical fact (standing back, silence) reads for itself; labeling it "the right instinct" and explaining the silence breaks the scene.

**Cut 2** — interpretive sentence after physical inventory:
> "He had worked outside, and not recently become someone who did so."

Cut entirely. "Weather-roughened. The nails were close-cut and one was cracked at the quick." already delivers the inference; the following sentence names it.

**Cut 3** — gloss appended to a parallel-look image:
> "Gervais turned and looked at the anchorage the way she had looked at it, **though he was looking for different things**."

Cut the subordinate clause. The parallel with Eira is visible in the action; the difference in their investigative purpose is implied by everything preceding it.

**Cut 4** — gloss on a dash phrase:
> "The column had been blank before, and it was filled now **— with a notation that meant one thing and described another**."

Cut the dash clause. "A notation that meant one thing and described another" restates the moral problem that the next three sentences then demonstrate concretely; announcing it first depletes the demonstration.

**Cut 5** — redundant restatement of a category already named:
> "There was no column in the vessel-traffic log for a man standing on the pier."

Cut entirely. "This was not vessel traffic" (the preceding sentence) already makes the point; this sentence says it again in different words.

Cut the second sentence entirely. "She noted this" is a classic cognition label; the inference ("carrying it in his head") is what any reader draws from "listened without writing anything down." The observation already contains the conclusion.
