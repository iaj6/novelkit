# Story Arc

One tight line per drafted chapter, in chronological order.

- **01-the-night-watch** — Eira sees an unidentified man on the north wharf at 2 a.m. on October 11; three days later his body washes onto the breakwater and the Coast Guard takes everything, including her silence about what she saw.
- **02-arrival** — Four German POWs are installed in the Pfeiffer icehouse; Hannah watches from the kitchen window and notices the youngest — Karl — last off the truck, looking at the tree line and not the house.
- **03-the-question-nobody-asked** — Marcus overhears school talk about the European-clothed body, forms a private hypothesis about a running man, and tests it obliquely against Henry, who closes the conversation with "Probably better that way."
- **04-what-the-ledger-holds** — Farris returns with the Coast Guard's findings — European clothing, a scar, no papers — and reveals the man died after the blow, not during it; Eira answers accurately, omits October 11, and reads her own log entry three times.
- **05-the-bavarian-accent** — Hannah accidentally answers Karl's German mutter about a fence post; Albrecht, watching from the kitchen window, speaks German at the dinner table for the first time in over a year.
- **06-english-only** — The school board bans spoken German in Marcus's classroom; Henry tells him Alderman Marsh questioned his loyalty; Hannah's homework exercise changes what Marcus sees when he looks at her.
- **07-december-protocol** — Naval Intelligence man Hale arrives with the Canadian-crossing theory; Eira gives him the log and nothing else, and spends the afternoon finishing Owen's winter protocol.
- **08-shorter-letters** — Will's V-Mails stop; Hannah runs the farm's official ledger through January's silence while Karl volunteers the first detail of his life she has been given.
- **09-what-a-spy-would-say** — Marcus answers Rolfe's public "spy or German?" question precisely and without wound; Henry's advice at chess—"you don't do a line, you just keep showing up"—is the chapter's turn.
- **10-lonzos-war** — Lonzo Reed watches Hannah and Karl's fence conversation from his truck, decides the word for what he sees is "ordinary," and writes nothing extra in the escort log.
- **11-portland** — Eira drives to Portland and obliquely confirms the small-craft Maritime crossing theory; on the road back a stranger in a dark coat makes her check the mirror for half a mile.
- **12-bread** — Magda falls and Hannah runs the farm alone; Karl tells her what he misses most is bread that smelled right; that night Hannah reads her mother's recipe and closes the book.
- **13-what-meaning-costs** — Marcus gives Hannah a University of Maine address after her essay stuns him, then receives Ellenbogen's letter confirming the sponsored-passage program — and files both, saying nothing at dinner.
- **14-the-dory** — Dakin tells Eira about a foreign dory and canvas bag on the north beach in early October; she walks the geometry of the crossing, then logs Dakin's information — her first oblique entry connecting to October.
- **15-wills-letter** — Will's V-Mail arrives after months of silence; Albrecht weeps privately and says "Er lebt. Das ist genug."; Magda touches his shoulder; Hannah passes the icehouse fence at dusk and says it too.
- **16-licensing** — Marcus goes to the harbor for a boat permit and gives Eira his hypothesis about the dead man; she turns down the radio and says nothing official; he leaves her his absolution at the door.
- **17-the-log-entry** — A French-Canadian sailor's careful English unlocks Eira's final interpretive piece: the man on the north wharf stayed silent because he was afraid of what she represented, and that fact settles without absolution.
- **18-the-columbine** — On her eighteenth birthday, Hannah finds a columbine Karl left on the fence post, takes it, presses it in her father's Duden, and resolves to say nothing at the fence tomorrow.
- **19-which-end** — On D-Day, June 6, 1944, Karl works the Pfeiffer fields hearing only fragments of the radio; that night Wicke asks "Which end?" and Karl answers "Both."
- **20-paris-1940** — D-Day empties the school; Marcus circles the memory of Paris 1940 without entering it, enters quiz marks instead, and is called home by Henry to chicken stew and Lillian's plain grace.
- **21-the-stranger-at-the-dock** — Werner Kahl arrives from Portland with a photograph of a missing brother; Eira answers every question technically and writes nothing of his visit in the log.
- **22-southern-france** — Karl tells Hannah he kept a transportation ledger in southern France and did not ask where the people went; she takes a week of distance from the fence, then returns.
- **23-the-photograph** — Henry finds the photograph while fixing Marcus's window sill, looks at it for twenty seconds and replaces it exactly; that evening he lets Marcus win at chess, and tells him, in four words, what he found and didn't read.
- **24-contract-review** — Marcus survives a mid-term board review engineered by Marsh, and a Cambridge appointment letter sits in his drawer read three times and unanswered — the not-deciding already deciding.
- **25-one-year** — On the breakwater's north end — one year to the day — Eira assembles everything she knows, names both the utilitarian and the self-protective argument for silence, cannot separate them, stops trying, and goes back to work.
- **26-last-day** — The POW detail ends a hour early; Hannah and Karl exchange four sentences in German at the fence, and she says "He will" about Will without being certain; Karl doesn't look back.
- **27-cornbread** — Marcus writes and mails the letter declining Cambridge, tells Henry and Lillian he is staying, tries to find words adequate to the kitchen and cannot, eats the cornbread; the Battle of the Bulge begins and changes nothing.
- **28-filed** — A Portland letter officially closes USCG Case No. 43-2291; Eira files it, logs "Filed," prepares the harbor for a Thursday front, and goes to bed.
- **29-the-good-cloth** — Will comes home changed and alive; Albrecht speaks English at the table; Magda says *Mein Kind*; Hannah closes the Duden on the dry columbine and thinks of Karl without grief.
- **30-all-moorings-secure** — Spring harbor opening, late April 1945: the dead man's sister asks Eira if she has seen him; Eira says no; the harbor opens; the log is written; the pen is down; the season has begun.
