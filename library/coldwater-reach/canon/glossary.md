# Glossary

Alphabetical. Definitions are brief and specific to this story's usage.

---

**Albrecht Pfeiffer** — Hannah's father. Bavarian emigrant (Amberg, 1922). Dairy farmer. Has retreated from English into German since the war started. Age 58.

**Amberg** — Town in Bavaria, Germany, approximately 50 km from Kirchheim. Albrecht Pfeiffer's hometown.

**BC-1206** — A U.S. Navy-surplus shortwave radio receiver. Eira has one in the harbormaster's office, used to monitor harbor traffic and weather channels.

**Breakwater** — The granite jetty at the entrance to Coldwater Reach harbor, built ca. 1890s. Approximately 400 feet long. Site of the body's discovery in October 1943.

**Braddock** — The previous German and Latin instructor at Coldwater Reach Consolidated School. Enlisted in 1942; Marcus was hired to replace him.

**Camp Houlton** — Real: the main German POW internment and work camp in Aroostook County, Maine. Parent facility for satellite farm work details including the Pfeiffer farm detail.

**Camp Sherbrooke** — Real: an enemy alien internment camp in Sherbrooke, Quebec, operated by the Canadian government 1940–1943. Marcus Klein was interned here June–December 1940.

**Coldwater Reach** — Fictional. A deepwater harbor town on the mid-Maine coast. Population approximately 1,400. The story's primary setting.

**Coastal Patrol** — The U.S. Coastal Patrol (also called the Coastal Picket Patrol), a civilian auxiliary that patrolled coastal areas. Lonzo Reed serves as a private in this capacity.

**Elias Crow** — Henry and Lillian Crow's son. Currently serving in the U.S. Navy.

**Eira Bowman** — Harbormaster of Coldwater Reach. Age 52. POV character. The widow of Owen Bowman.

**Emergency Committee in Aid of Displaced Foreign Scholars** — Real: a U.S. organization (formally the Emergency Committee in Aid of Displaced German Scholars, later broadened) that helped refugee academics secure positions. Marcus was on their rolls 1940–1941.

**Hannah Pfeiffer** — Daughter of Albrecht and Magda Pfeiffer. Age 17. POV character.

**Henry Crow** — Chief custodian of Coldwater Reach Consolidated School. Marcus's landlord. Age 54. Black; came north from Norfolk in 1924.

**Karl Reinmar** — German POW. Age 19. From Kirchheim, Bavaria. The youngest of the four prisoners on the Pfeiffer farm detail. Captured in North Africa, May 1943.

**Kirchheim** — Town in Bavaria, Germany, approximately 50 km from Amberg. Karl Reinmar's hometown.

**Lillian Crow** — Henry Crow's wife. Age 51. Works at the canning plant. Central domestic presence in Marcus's arc.

**Lonzo Reed** — Coastal Patrol private. Age 60. Coldwater native, WWI veteran. Escort/guard for the Pfeiffer farm POW detail.

**Magda Pfeiffer** — Hannah's mother. Age 55. Has largely stopped speaking; her withdrawal is unnamed but clinical in retrospect.

**Marcus Klein, Dr.** — German and Latin teacher at Coldwater Reach Consolidated School. Age 47. Viennese. Jewish. POV character.

**Owen Bowman** — Eira's husband. Previous harbormaster of Coldwater Reach. Drowned in the November gale of 1935. Age at death: 49.

**Pfeiffer farm** — 140-acre dairy farm, three miles inland from Coldwater Reach. Property of Albrecht and Magda Pfeiffer. The POW icehouse is on the eastern edge.

**The Reach** — The tidal inlet on which Coldwater Reach sits; also used as shorthand for the town itself.

**V-Mail** — Victory Mail. A WWII U.S. postal system in which letters to and from servicemen were microfilmed, shipped, and reprinted at the receiving end. The originals were destroyed. The reprinted letters were small and sometimes slightly blurred.

**Will Pfeiffer** — Hannah's older brother. Enlisted 1942; currently serving in the Pacific. Last V-Mail received by the family: August 1943.

**Dakin** — Lobsterman, age 61. Has fished Coldwater Reach his whole working life. Discovered the body on the north breakwater on the morning of October 14, 1943, and reported it to Eira. He will provide dory information in Ch 14.

**Farris** — U.S. Coast Guard petty officer, Rockland station. Responded to the October 14 body report with one unnamed seaman. Took Eira's notebook, asked four questions, and removed the body. Returns in Ch 4 with additional information.

**Aldrich** — County labor office representative who delivered and explained the POW work-detail paperwork to Albrecht Pfeiffer in mid-October 1943. Drove a black Ford pickup with a county seal. Thin-faced, approximately forty, very clean shoes for a farm call. First name not established.

**Mrs. Eldredge** — History teacher at Coldwater Reach Consolidated School. One of the faculty whose ambient commentary on the breakwater body Marcus overhears.

**Rolfe** — Student in Marcus's first-period Latin class. Talkative; a conduit for town news. Mentions the German prisoners at the Pfeiffer farm in class, prompting Marcus to notice Hannah's stillness.

**Marion Coles** — Student in Marcus's Latin class. Sits at the end of the second row; marks her margins in pencil with grammatical parsing. One of three genuinely prepared students.

**Strout** — Student in Marcus's Latin class. One of three genuinely prepared students; caught between genuine interest in Latin and the desire to appear unremarkable.

**Hatch** — Fourth-grade teacher at Coldwater Reach Consolidated School. Arrived from somewhere inland in September 1943. Appears in staff-room scenes.

**Wakefield** — Principal of Coldwater Reach Consolidated School. Marcus Klein's professional supervisor. Mid-forties. Delivers uncomfortable instructions with a face that arranges itself easily into apologetic expressions. Not malicious; apologetic in the way of a man who is not quite sorry. He defended Marcus at the school board meeting on grounds of professional utility.

**Alderman Marsh** — Member of the Coldwater Reach school board. The named source of institutional pressure on Marcus Klein. Not a villain — a force. Raised the question of 'thorough review of all personnel with European backgrounds' at the November 1943 board meeting; repeated the phrase in the manner of a man waiting for someone else to name his specific concern. Wakefield's utility defense satisfied him for the moment.

**Pelletier** — Student in Marcus Klein's German class. Reads aloud with the careful bafflement of someone deciphering an unfamiliar system.

**Hale, Mr.** — Naval Intelligence agent, Portland district. Arrives at the Coldwater harbormaster's office in early December 1943 to question Eira about the October body. Younger than Lt. Farris; trains his questions wide before narrowing. Presents a card (Naval Intelligence, Portland, partial district number) but pockets it before Eira can read it fully. Delivers the Canadian Maritime Provinces crossing theory. Says he may return; does not.

**Shep** — Fifteen-year-old dockhand working at Coldwater Reach harbor for school credit. Works alongside Ames in December 1943 on the winter harbor protocol. Has a good instinct for fender inspections.

**Owen's winter protocol** — A four-page handwritten document (titled 'Winterization — Harbor — Coldwater Reach — Annual') paper-clipped to the back of the first harbor log Eira inherited. Written by Owen Bowman in the winter of 1933. Twelve items covering: summer-to-winter mooring field swap, piling inspection with orange paint, fender checks, fuel pump winterization, storm drain clearing, and related tasks. Eira follows it every December without alteration.

**Mrs. Gaines** — Postmistress at the Coldwater Reach post office (located inside the general store). Has held the position eleven years. A steady presence in the town's fabric; watches the families waiting for wartime mail with a careful, undemonstrative sympathy.

**Meredith (Reed)** — Lonzo Reed's daughter. Lives in Augusta, Maine, with her husband, who sells insurance. As of February 1944 she visited her father at the end of January, leaving him an aluminum thermos with a green stripe that he uses every day.

**Bremer** — The oldest of the four German POWs on the Pfeiffer farm detail. A sergeant. Moves with deliberateness that reads as dignity or caution or both. First off the truck each morning.

**Wicke** — One of the four German POWs on the Pfeiffer farm detail. Stocky. Always cold. Always the first to check the sky.

**Fischer** — One of the four German POWs on the Pfeiffer farm detail. Has an alert, engaged expression and a tendency to find things funnier than the occasion requires.

**Satellite facility (Houlton road)** — A converted grange hall approximately three miles east of Coldwater on the Houlton road. Houses eight German POWs in two overnight-guard rotation. Lonzo Reed escorts four of them daily to Pfeiffer farm.

**Dr. Hastings** — The local physician in or near Coldwater Reach. Albrecht takes Magda to Dr. Hastings after her kitchen fall in late February 1944. First named in Ch 12.

**Bayerisches Roggenmischbrot** — A Bavarian rye-wheat bread, denser than white bread but not as dark as Schwarzbrot. Magda Pfeiffer's recipe is in her handwriting in the kitchen recipe book. Karl Reinmar names a bread like this as the thing he has missed most since being drafted. First named in Ch 12.

**Ellenbogen, Professor** — Marcus Klein's contact at the Emergency Committee in Aid of Displaced Foreign Scholars, based in Cambridge, Massachusetts. His office is on the third floor of the Yard. In March 1944 he writes to Marcus asking about European nationals who may have passed through the Canadian Maritime Provinces in 1943 under the informal sponsored-passage program.

**sponsored-passage program** — Informal church-and-labor network operating in 1942–1943 to move European refugees from Canada into the United States without full official documentation. Run by a Lutheran coalition in Quebec and a Methodist organization in New Brunswick, working with labor affiliates in Vermont and Maine. Marcus knows this category from proximity to the Emergency Committee's operations. This is the structural explanation for the dead man's likely crossing to Coldwater Reach.

**University of Maine at Orono** — The admissions office address Marcus Klein writes on Hannah Pfeiffer's essay in March 1944, with the note that there are programs for students like her. She puts the address in her bag; whether she writes remains open.

**Mörike, Eduard** — German poet (1804–1875). His short poem 'Auf ein altes Bild' (On an Old Picture) is the text Marcus assigns for the Chapter 13 translation exercise. The poem depicts a summer landscape with the Christ child and the cross already greening in the forest. Hannah's essay on the assignment generates her key observation about compound nouns and the cost of translation.

**Sommerbild** — The German compound noun (summer-image) Hannah Pfeiffer uses in her essay to illustrate what is lost in translation: a single unified word that English must unpack into two separate words, losing the unity in the gap between them. Her essay uses this as evidence that 'when you translate you do not move a thing from one room to another.'

**Briggs** — A soldier in Will Pfeiffer's Pacific unit. Mentioned by Will in his April 1944 V-Mail: showed Will a card trick that Will has been trying to figure out ever since. A minor but durable detail — appears again if Will comes home in ch29.

**South field (Pfeiffer farm)** — Approximately three acres of pasture ground on the Pfeiffer farm, running downhill from the barn to a stone wall. Not plowed as of April 1944; Hannah works it with a hoe to break frost-heave clods. Located on the opposite side of the barn from the icehouse fence.

**Sainte-Marguerite** — A small coastal lumber freighter (approximately 110 feet) out of Moncton, New Brunswick. Captained by Aubert. Carries sawn lumber south to Portland. Makes regular transit stops at Coldwater Reach harbor — at least her third season by May 1944. A recurring Maritime trade vessel in the harbor.

**Duden (1928)** — Albrecht Pfeiffer's German spelling dictionary: Duden: Die deutsche Rechtschreibung, 1928 edition. Sits on the second shelf of the front-room bookshelf at the Pfeiffer farmhouse. Hannah has used it for school compositions throughout her education. On June 2, 1944, she presses Karl's columbine flower between pages in the K section (near Kehren and Klee). The book recurs in Ch 29 when Hannah retrieves it after Will's homecoming.

**columbine (fence-post flower)** — A single red-and-yellow columbine stem Karl Reinmar places in the gap of the repaired east fence post on Hannah Pfeiffer's eighteenth birthday (June 2, 1944). The columbine plants grow at the base of the eastern fencerow where it meets the woodlot. Hannah takes the flower, presses it in the Duden, and says nothing about it at the fence.

**Elsa Reinmar** — Karl Reinmar's younger sister, from Kirchheim, Bavaria. Turned eighteen in April 1944. Works at a pharmacy two doors from the family hardware store on Kirchheim's main street. Karl has had no letter from her since February 1944.

**Reinmar hardware store** — Karl's father's hardware store on the main street of Kirchheim, Bavaria. Wooden bolt drawers behind the counter labeled in block letters (M6, M8, M10). Smells of machine oil and sawdust. Its current condition as of June 1944 is unknown to Karl.

**Werner Kahl** — A man of approximately fifty who appears at the Coldwater harbormaster's office in July 1944 asking about the October 1943 breakwater body. European-cut clothing (narrower shoulders, higher collar button than American cut); practiced English with a Central European accent and German underneath. Says he was directed to Coldwater by the Portland harbormaster's office. Claims to be looking for his brother Josef, who was traveling from Canada in autumn 1943. Has a limp in his right leg, visible only from behind. Was in Maine on 'business' of an unspecified nature.

**Josef Kahl** — Werner Kahl's brother, whom Werner is searching for as of July 1944. Josef was allegedly traveling from Canada in autumn 1943 and has not been heard from since September 1943. Werner shows Eira a photograph of Josef: a man of approximately 45, dark-haired, in a dark suit, photographed in a European studio. Whether Josef is the man whose body washed onto the Coldwater breakwater in October 1943 is never confirmed — the body's face had been three days in the water, and Eira cannot say yes or no with certainty.

**Aldous Cutter** — A Coldwater harbor user. Owner of a mooring float on the east inner basin (listing since late June 1944), three lobster traps, and a skiff not in the water for two years. Eira gives him until end of July 1944 to repair the float.

**Frye** — Selectman on the Coldwater Reach school board during the October 1944 contract review. Owns and operates the hardware store in Coldwater Reach.

**Coombs** — Selectman on the Coldwater Reach school board during the October 1944 contract review. Retired mill supervisor.
