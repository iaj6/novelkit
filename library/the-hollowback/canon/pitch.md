# Pitch

Four young adults — a wizard's apprentice still learning what magic takes out of her, a soldier with an army discharge nobody will explain, a half-elf rogue who jokes his way past every real question, and a dwarven hedge-healer whose god the Church has declined to acknowledge — take the worst-paying job in Thorngate's trade district: find the merchant Maret Ashford's nineteen-year-old brother Ren, who walked east into the Hollowback three weeks ago and hasn't come back.

Everyone already knows where he went and no one else will go after him. The Hollowback is border country — half-civilized old-growth forest, disputed charter lines, and at its heart the ruin of an Eastmark waystation built by an empire that hasn't existed in three centuries. The station is supposed to be empty. Ren is inside it. He is alive, he is not in danger (not exactly), and he does not want to come home. What he found in the ruins is the reason, and by the time the party drags the whole story into the light, they know something the kingdom doesn't — something they would have been much more comfortable not knowing.

The book is the journey in, the discovery, and the argument about what to do now. Stakes: real. Banter: constant. Romance: brewing and unresolved. Answers: expensive.
