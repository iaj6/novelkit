# Characters

---

## Sela Vane — Wizard's Apprentice
**Primary POV, approximately 6–7 chapters**

**Age:** Seventeen.

**Role:** Party member. The reader's anchor into how this world works.

**Background:** Apprenticed to a wizard in Thorngate's lower academic quarter — not the prestigious academy, but the kind of practice where a girl from a middling family could afford the tuition. Three years of study. She took the Ashford job because her master is in debt, the money is needed, and this looked like a job she could take without getting killed. She has reconsidered this assessment several times since.

**Magic capability:** Four reliable spells (light cantrip, force-push, single-target sleep induction, the unraveling for mechanical locks). Anything beyond these is improvisation at unknown cost. She is acutely aware of her threshold and has not yet learned where it actually is.

**Inner life:** Careful. Structured. She builds internal taxonomies of things she doesn't understand, files observations for later, and is often three steps ahead of whatever conversation she's actually in. She reads people well and says nothing about it. Her thoughts are more articulate than her words — not because she is shy, but because she is conserving. She notices the exact angle of afternoon light and what it means for timing. She notices when Evren laughs a half-beat too fast. She does not editorialize her observations; she simply has them.

**Drives:** Understand how things work. Don't die. Don't let the others die. Find out what she's actually capable of before the situation forces the question.

**Voice (interior):** Clean, precise, occasionally dry. Organized. She will name the logical structure of a problem before she acts on it. Her prose chapters feel slightly more formal than the others — not stiff, but exact.

**Relationships:**
- *Corvin:* Respects his competence without reserve. Has not named the reason she keeps wanting to watch him be competent. Stays half a page behind the feeling in her own interiority.
- *Evren:* Finds him genuinely funny, which annoys her. Sees through most of the deflection and says nothing. Suspects there is something he's not saying about why he took this job.
- *Agda:* The closest thing to easy trust she has in the party. They communicate in practical shorthand and do not require each other to perform.
- *Ren:* Complicated from first meeting. He is not what she expected. He is interesting in a way that alarms her, and the alarm is itself informative.

**Romance threads:** Drawn to Corvin (his competence, his quiet, the way he does not demand her attention) and to Ren once she meets him. Both threads remain unresolved. She would sooner take a spell at full cost than say anything aloud.

## Corvin Ash — Former Kingdom Soldier
**POV, approximately 2–3 chapters**

**Age:** Twenty-four.

**Role:** Party's combat lead. The one who decides in the first three seconds whether a fight is winnable and on what terms.

**Background:** Seven years in Aldenmere's army — infantry, then sergeant, then formerly sergeant. Discharged under circumstances the army's paperwork describes as dereliction of duty. The actual circumstances were: he refused a legal order he knew to be morally wrong, targeting civilians he was certain were non-combatants. He was right. He was still discharged. He has been doing hired-blade work in Thorngate for fourteen months and is clearly doing it to avoid having time to think.

**Inner life:** Dry. Declarative. He sees in systems: sightlines, terrain, who is holding their weapon incorrectly and how that resolves in thirty seconds. He doesn't narrate his emotional life to himself — he has reactions and then acts on them, with the narration occurring after the fact if at all. He is aware he is running from something. He has not decided what to do about that yet.

**Drives:** Keep everyone alive. Not be asked about the army. Not need anyone's help. (He will fail at least two of these by the end.)

**Voice (interior):** Short sentences. Tactical present tense even in retrospect. He does not name feelings — he notes facts that point toward a feeling. *She'd looked at him for a second too long* rather than *he wondered if she noticed him.* Combat chapters in his POV are physically specific and almost impersonal — not emotionless, but processed through the vocabulary of assessment.

**Relationships:**
- *Sela:* He noticed her noticing things first. She sees more than she says, which he finds simultaneously unsettling and deeply reassuring. He doesn't pursue. He stays near.
- *Evren:* A functional exasperation with real affection under it. He doesn't trust Evren's explanations. He trusts Evren's instincts.
- *Agda:* Respects her without condition. She is serious when serious is called for. He wishes she'd talk less about Duhr but he has never said this.

**The discharge:** The full version of what happened has not been told to anyone in the party. Agda has guessed something closer to the truth than Corvin has admitted. He does not know this.

## Evren Cael — Half-Elf Rogue
**POV, approximately 2–3 chapters**

**Age:** Twenty-two.

**Role:** Party's information, infiltration, and trap-handling specialist. Primary source of comedy. Secondary source of complications.

**Background:** Half-elf (human mother, elven father not mentioned and not to be asked about). Has been in Thorngate for two years. Before that: a story that changes slightly every time someone asks. His actual skill set — lock-picking, sleight of hand, reading people, moving quietly, knowing which tavern owners are also information brokers — implies a fairly deliberate background he presents as though he stumbled into it by accident.

**Inner life:** Fast, associative, deflective — and then, when no one is looking, something else entirely. He jokes because he is genuinely good at it, and because it keeps people at a comfortable distance, and because the alternative is the actual question, which is: what is he really doing here, and why did he specifically say yes to this job. He knows more about the Hollowback than he has said. He lies about small things out of habit. The large things he simply does not discuss.

**Hidden reason:** Evren has a personal connection to Vethic ruin exploration — someone who went into a ruin like this one and didn't come back. He is here to find out what's inside the Eastmark Waystation. The party's contract is cover. He has not disclosed this to anyone and will resist disclosing it until the story makes concealment impossible.

**Drives:** Make the joke. Stay ahead of the moment. Find out what's in the waystation. (The third one is the real one.)

**Voice (interior):** Fast. Associative. He has four observations before landing on the one he'll act on. In his POV chapters, the humor is not a performance — it is how he processes. The serious moments in his chapters are stark precisely because they are surrounded by the other thing. His interiority does not announce when it is being serious; it just is.

**Relationships:**
- *Agda:* She doesn't laugh at his jokes and he finds this fascinating rather than discouraging. He keeps trying. She occasionally lets one land. He is in trouble and does not know it.
- *Sela:* She sees through him. He is not sure if this is a problem or a relief. He suspects it is a relief, which is itself a problem.
- *Corvin:* He doesn't try the jokes on Corvin. He cannot explain why. (It's because he respects him, and respect makes the deflection feel small.)

## Agda Stonebrook — Dwarven Hedge-Healer
**POV, approximately 2 chapters**

**Age:** Thirty-one. Young for a dwarf — in dwarven terms, still in early adulthood.

**Role:** Party's healer. Moral compass. The one most likely to say the uncomfortable thing aloud, delivered flatly, while continuing to do something practical with her hands.

**Background:** Raised in a dwarven enclave in the hills northwest of Thorngate. Trained as a healer by her enclave's practitioners in the tradition of Duhr, a dwarven deity of thresholds — the line between living and dying, before and after. The Church of Aldenmere considers Duhr a minor and unverified deity at best and an unregistered heretical practice at worst. Agda has no Church certification. She has good hands, a comprehensive kit, and a god who has never once failed to answer when she called.

**Inner life:** Gruff is the surface; it is not the interior. She feels things deeply and has learned not to lead with it. She has strong opinions — about the Church's opinions, about healing ethics, about what constitutes a real god versus one that the bureaucracy happens to have paperwork for — and she shares them when asked and sometimes when not asked. Privately, she is warmer than she intends to be. Evren has gotten further under her defenses than she has acknowledged, even to herself.

**Drives:** Keep the party alive (professional obligation that has become something else). Not let Evren's insufferable jokes work on her (failing). Be honest about what Duhr asks, even when it makes the Church uncomfortable.

**Voice (interior):** Concrete. Gruff. She notices a person's breathing before their expression. She names a feeling once and stops talking about it. Her warmth comes through in what she does, not in her narration of what she does. Her POV chapters are the shortest and among the most emotionally dense.

**Duhr:** A dwarven deity of thresholds — the boundary between living and dying, before and after, arriving and leaving. Agda's healing rituals are accompanied by brief invocations that sound, to outside ears, like arguments. She argues with Duhr the way you argue with family: expecting to be heard, irritated when she isn't, not doubting for a moment that there is someone listening.

**Relationships:**
- *Evren:* Refuses to admit she looks forward to the jokes. Is beginning to notice that he is quieter with her than with the others, and that the quiet is different from his ordinary quiet.
- *Sela:* Practical alliance. They communicate in useful shorthand and do not need to perform camaraderie to have it.
- *Corvin:* She has guessed something about his discharge that she has kept to herself. She will keep it to herself until there is a reason not to.

## Ren Ashford — The Missing Brother
**Not a POV character. The mission objective. Not a damsel. Not a villain.**

**Age:** Nineteen.

**Background:** Younger sibling of Maret Ashford. Was apprenticed to a cartographer — he can map things, understands old roads, reads Vethic Imperial survey notations that most people treat as decorative marks. Three weeks before the story opens, he packed everything he'd saved, bought provisions, and walked east into the Hollowback. Left a note saying he'd be back. Did not come back.

**What happened:** He knew from an old cartographer's record that the Eastmark Waystation was not empty in the way that people said it was empty. He went to see. He found something. He has been there since, documenting it and making decisions about it that are not "tell my sister and come home." His reasons are real and make sense to him. He is possibly wrong about what to do with what he found. He is not wrong that he found it.

**Character:** Earnest, stubborn, younger than he thinks he is. He has been told he is clever often enough that he has partially confused this with being right. He argues in complete sentences. He is a genuinely bad swordsman. He is not accustomed to people who are as perceptive as Sela, and this makes him uncertain in a way that most things don't.

**His role in the romance:** He and Sela develop a connection that neither of them names and that the mission context makes structurally impossible to resolve. By the time the party leaves, the situation (not either of them) has foreclosed the easy version.

## Maret Ashford — The Client

**Age:** Mid-thirties.

**Role:** Minor. Posts the job, pays the fee, is the human face of the contract.

**Background:** Minor merchant, runs a small import business in Thorngate's trade district. Competent at her work, frightened about her brother, slightly embarrassed that she cannot afford better help. She is not hiding anything. She is genuinely scared. She will have complicated feelings about what the party eventually brings back — not because she is ungrateful, but because what they bring back is more complicated than she bargained for.
