# Threads
## *The Worst Job on the Board*

Five threads braid this story. Three are internal (character questions). One is an external moral question. One is the relationship architecture of the whole party. None of the main plot question ("will they find Ren?") counts as a thread — that's the spine. These are what wrap around it.

---

## Thread 1: The Shape of the Discharge

- **Question:** Will Corvin's real reason for leaving the army come out — and if so, what will it cost him in front of these people?

- **Enters:** Ch 03 (*Dereliction*) — A deserter from Corvin's old unit names it in front of the whole party: *I heard you got derelicted. Heard the whole story, too.* The word hangs. Corvin says nothing.

- **Advanced in:**
  - Ch 04 (*Aftertaste*) — Evren opens the question; Agda says *leave it* with the specific weight of someone who already knows more than she's saying. Corvin says: *Seven years. It ended.*
  - Ch 11 (*The Thing the Empire Kept*) — Agda watches Corvin say *they left it running* about the Imperial garrison. She confirms privately what she's guessed: he refused an order he knew was wrong, targeting civilians. He was right. He was still discharged. She keeps it.
  - Ch 15 (*The Road Tells You Things*) — Sela, during night watch, names the shape of it to him directly: *you refused an order you knew was wrong, and it cost you. And you did the same thing in the chamber.* He says nothing for a long time. His saying nothing is the confirmation.

- **Resolves in:** Deliberately unresolved. The full account is never given aloud. Corvin never confirms or corrects anyone's version of events. Sela has the shape; Agda has the shape; neither has the words from him. He goes back to Thorngate holding it the way he held it when he left. The thread earns its non-resolution: a man who paid a real cost for doing the right thing and has not yet found the vocabulary to say so is not going to find it in eleven days on the road.

- **What it's about underneath:** Every character in this book has a method for keeping people at arm's length. Corvin's is silence — the refusal to explain himself. The thread pressurizes whether the party can become real to him without requiring his story, and whether he can accept being known in outline when he has never allowed the full picture. It also asks what the army did when it punished the right decision: the Empire left its problem running; the army discharged its honest man. The garrison question and the discharge question are the same question in two eras.

## Thread 2: The Cartographer Who Didn't Come Back

- **Question:** Why did Evren really take this job, what does he know about the Eastmark Waystation that he hasn't said, and will anyone find out before the truth becomes impossible to keep?

- **Enters:** Ch 01 (*The Worst Job on the Board*) — Sela notices Evren had the Ashford contract before it was posted. She files it without comment. Ch 02 (*East*) — In his night-watch interior, the joke mechanism stops: Evren thinks about *her* — a cartographer who went into a ruin like this and didn't come back, whose survey notes he found three years ago and has been looking for an excuse to follow. He shuts it down. By morning he is already planning the next attempt to make Agda laugh.

- **Advanced in:**
  - Ch 06 (*What the Empire Paved*) — Walking the road, Evren says: *The cartographer I mentioned — she said the road would still be there.* One pronoun and a stop. Sela notices; says nothing.
  - Ch 07 (*Watch*) — In his interior, her name is present: *Lira. She said the road would still be there, and she was right about that.* Later, Agda wakes early and says: *You know more about where we're going than you've said.* He confirms it with one word: *Some.* She doesn't push. He sits with her for an hour afterward feeling something he does not have a word for.
  - Ch 08 (*The Antechamber*) — He reads Ren's Vethic notation scratch marks aloud. Second time in front of the party. He was supposed to learn this from a cartographer, he says. He learned it from Lira. He does not say this.
  - Ch 09 (*Interior*) — He reads the maps of the waystation fluently, without hesitation, third time in front of the party. It is no longer deniable. *We should find him now.*
  - Ch 12 (*What We Do Now*) — He breaks. Not fully, but enough: *There was a cartographer who went into a station like this one, three years ago, and didn't come back.* He asks Ren if there is documentation of the original subject's fate. He does not say Lira's name. The party now knows there is a specific person.
  - Ch 14 (*Threshold*) — Outside the waystation, he asks Agda: *Do you think she knew what she was walking into?* Agda answers with what little she has. He uncrosses his arms.

- **Resolves in:** Deliberately unresolved. Lira's name is never said aloud. The relationship is never described. What her fate was inside a ruin like this one is implied but not confirmed. The sealing of the Hollowed One is Evren's version of an answer — it settles the present danger — but it does not tell him what happened to her, and he does not get to know. The thread earns its non-resolution: some grief is not closed by evidence; it is only carried differently when you stop being alone with it.

- **What it's about underneath:** Evren's comedy is structural — it keeps people at a comfortable distance and keeps the real question from being asked. This thread is the question he has been rehearsing for three years under the jokes: *what happened to her, and am I going to the same place she went?* The closer he gets to the answer, the less the jokes work. By Ch 14, he is standing outside a sealed waystation with his arms crossed, and Agda is standing next to him without asking anything. The thread is not resolved because Evren doesn't get closure — he gets company, which is a different thing and possibly the right thing.

## Thread 3: The Thing the Empire Left Running

- **Question:** What is in the Eastmark Waystation — and once the party knows, what do they owe to that knowledge?

- **Enters:** Ch 05 (*Charter*) — Grenn Vott, in Dwarven, tells Agda specifically: there is something in that waystation that doesn't respect thresholds properly. People go in and come back different. He doesn't know what it is. She translates the warning flatly to the party and doesn't communicate the full weight of how he said it.

- **Advanced in:**
  - Ch 06 (*What the Empire Paved*) — Something moves at the camp's edge in the night: careful, intentional, not animal. Not an immediate threat. The forest says: *you are not the first things to have been here.*
  - Ch 08 (*The Antechamber*) — Inside the waystation, in the dark beyond the antechamber: a sound. Too regular for water dripping. Something is active in there.
  - Ch 09 (*Interior*) — Ren's maps surface. His notation reads: *it is still here.* The sound has moved closer without being heard to move.
  - Ch 10 (*Found*) — Ren shows them the containment chamber. The Hollowed One: human-shaped, not still, inside a precision Vethic cage that is no longer fully intact.
  - Ch 11 (*The Thing the Empire Kept*) — Full reveal: a Vethic experimental subject, most of their vital energy extracted in a failed transfer procedure, suspended at the threshold of living and dead for three hundred years. The containment is degrading. Weeks until filter failure. Agda asks Duhr and gets back: *a wound that hasn't been allowed to heal.*
  - Ch 12 (*What We Do Now*) — The argument: Corvin says leave it to authority; Ren says authority will weaponize it; Agda says there is no time for authority; Evren says the cartographer who didn't come back is part of this. The plan: reseal it themselves, then decide who to tell.
  - Ch 13 (*The Hollowed*) — Resealing executed under active draw. Corvin holds the line. Agda invokes Duhr on her knees. Sela casts the unraveling past her reliable threshold. The Hollowed One is contained. The sealing will hold for years.
  - Ch 14 (*Threshold*) — The party escapes. Agda gets Duhr's door-closing response: *the threshold settled.* Ren keeps the documentation.
  - Ch 15 (*The Road Tells You Things*) — The five-person decision: tell Maret her brother is alive. Keep the detailed documentation in two copies. Do not report to the Church or the crown. Not yet. Not until they know who to trust with it.

- **Resolves in:** Deliberately unresolved (and intentionally so). The Hollowed One is contained but not resolved — the sealing holds for years, not forever. The party holds the knowledge. The kingdom does not know. Ch 16 closes on this explicitly: *the sealing will hold for much longer than the degraded version — years, not weeks. Long enough to go to someone with authority. But not permanent.* The thread earns its non-resolution because this is the book's claim about the cost of discovery: you cannot go back through the door you came in. The party knows something they cannot unknow, and the decision about what to do with it is the book's permanent loose thread, available to press on in any sequel.

- **What it's about underneath:** The Vethic Empire built something and did not finish dealing with it and then stopped existing, leaving the wound to run. The garrison was withdrawn in order, not driven out — they just stopped being responsible. The thread asks: what does the living world owe to the unfinished business of the dead, when the dead left it running and walked away? It pressurizes every member of the party differently — Corvin sees the garrison and his own army; Ren sees a volunteer whose consent was honored in the wrong terms; Agda sees a threshold held open by force; Evren sees the place that took Lira. The decision to reseal and not report is the party choosing to be more responsible than the Empire was, by exactly the small margin that small groups of people can manage.

## Thread 4: The Limit Sela Hasn't Found Yet

- **Question:** Will Sela discover where her actual threshold is — and what will it cost her when the situation forces the question?

- **Enters:** Ch 01 (*The Worst Job on the Board*) — Four reliable spells established. The cost is physical and real. She has not mapped her actual limit — she knows her four spells; she does not know what happens when she goes beyond them. Her master taught threshold management before anything else, because the first-year fatality rate used to be significant.

- **Advanced in:**
  - Ch 03 (*Dereliction*) — She holds force-push prepared for nearly six minutes during the ambush and decides not to release it. The cost is real anyway: light-headedness, pressure behind the eyes. She pays for what she didn't cast.
  - Ch 04 (*Aftertaste*) — Agda notices she held something (*you held something* — not a question). The dried herbs partially clear the headache. Corvin notices her non-cast and says: *Good decision.* She catalogs this.
  - Ch 08 (*The Antechamber*) — Two light-cantrip casts in the puzzle room — drops it once to avoid the magic-sensing trap, re-casts immediately after in the dark. The headache starts behind her right eye. She has paid twice today and still has force-push, sleep induction, and the unraveling available, but she is not fresh.
  - Ch 12 (*What We Do Now*) — She agrees to cast the unraveling in the worst possible conditions: in proximity to something that actively draws vital energy, on a non-standard Vethic target, under pressure. She runs the cost calculation and says yes anyway. Corvin says: *You know what you're agreeing to.* She says: *Yes.* He says: *All right.*

- **Resolves in:** Ch 13 (*The Hollowed*) — She casts the unraveling against a corroded, fused Vethic lock component while the Hollowed One draws on her vital reserves. The spell costs additively with the draw. She pushes. Her nose bleeds. She pushes harder. The component dissolves. Her vision goes black at the edges. She puts a hand on the floor. Corvin catches her before she hits the stone. The cost was real and she survived it, which is what she needed to know. Ch 16 confirms the resolution: *the knowledge of where her threshold actually is, which she spent eleven days finding out.*

- **What it's about underneath:** Sela's interior is more articulate than her exterior — she structures, catalogs, conserves. The thread is about the thing she cannot structure: the edge of herself she hasn't mapped because mapping it requires paying it. The resolution is not triumphant (she ends up on the floor, carried out). It is informational: she now knows. The book's claim is that this is worth knowing, even at cost — that competence without destiny means you have to find your own limits by finding them. The thread also carries the Sela/Corvin relationship at its most structural: every notable beat of that pairing is threaded through the magic cost. He noticed her non-cast. He said *good decision*. He said *you know what you're agreeing to*. He caught her. The thread resolves; the relationship doesn't.

## Thread 5: The Argument Against Distance

- **Question:** Will any member of this party let someone else past the specific method they use to keep people at arm's length — and if so, at what cost?

- **Enters:** Ch 01 (*The Worst Job on the Board*) — Sela/Corvin: she finds his competence immediately arresting and files it as *a separate problem.* Ch 02 (*East*) — Evren/Agda: he tries three jokes; she doesn't laugh; he finds this fascinating rather than discouraging and starts adjusting his strategy.

- **Advanced in (by pairing):**

  *Sela / Corvin (inter-party slow burn):*
  - Ch 04: He says *good decision* after the ambush — the most direct personal acknowledgment he's offered. She catalogs it under *significant.*
  - Ch 06: They move at the same pace on the Vethic road without speaking. He says *three hundred years* and she notes that the nothing-else sounds like awe in his register.
  - Ch 12: *You know what you're agreeing to. / All right.* She knows this is his version of *I'll be there.* Neither moves.
  - Ch 13: He catches her when she goes down. He doesn't editorialize. He picks her up and says *out, everyone out, now.*
  - Ch 15: Night watch. She names the shape of his discharge. He says *for what it's worth* and cannot finish the sentence. She waits. He says *you paid a real cost in there.* She says *it healed.* Neither moves. He watches the road and thinks: *yes.*
  - Ch 16: He waited for her outside Maret's office. She walks beside him without deciding to, at the pace that means they're going the same direction without having said so.

  *Evren / Agda (inter-party comedic-becomes-real):*
  - Ch 05: She steps in front of Evren when the fight tips. He holds her light when she tends her shoulder. Neither discusses this.
  - Ch 07: The watch. Agda wakes unprompted, says *you know more than you've said,* and doesn't push when he confirms it with *some.* He goes twenty minutes without making a single joke. She falls asleep near him. He sits for an hour afterward with something he doesn't have a word for.
  - Ch 14: Outside the sealed waystation. She stands next to him. He asks if Lira knew what she was walking into. She answers what she can. She stays until he uncrosses his arms. Then she goes to check Sela's color.
  - Ch 16: He makes a joke at something close to full power — *getting there.* She says *that's the standard.* They disperse in the same direction. Nobody comments.

  *Sela / Ren (external, complicated by the mission):*
  - Ch 10: She asks the real question about the containment chamber while Corvin argues about the contract. Ren looks at her as a specific person for the first time. She notes: he files people by what they know. She does this too. The alarm this produces is informative.
  - Ch 11: Agda observes from outside: Sela and Ren are having a parallel conversation to the main one. He answers her technical questions with the attentiveness of someone not usually met with that level of interest.
  - Ch 12: They work through the mechanism documentation together across the table. It is entirely practical. It is not entirely practical.
  - Ch 15: From Corvin's POV: she invites his reciprocal conversation, which she doesn't do with many people. He stops and thinks before answering, which means she asked a good question.
  - Ch 16: He says goodbye to her specifically. She says *you left the door open for us.* He says *if you need the documentation, I'll know where you are.* She notes the alarm is still present. She lets it wait.

- **Resolves in:** Deliberately unresolved for all three pairings. Ch 16 closes the thread by declining it: *the reader knows where everyone stands. The characters mostly don't.* This is the brief's explicit structural requirement and the book's earned position: eleven days is not enough time to resolve what it took years to build. The near-misses are the mode. The argument against distance has been lost, by everyone, in small private ways that have not yet been acknowledged out loud.

- **What it's about underneath:** Sela's careful observation-without-disclosure, Corvin's refusal to explain himself, Evren's comedy as deflection, Agda's gruffness as armor — these are four people who are all very good at being alone with themselves in a group. The thread is what happens when the situation refuses to let those methods work. It runs the full length of the book because the book's relationship arc is the point: strangers → reluctant colleagues → people who would actually die for each other. The romance pairings are how that arc is felt by each specific body. None resolve because the book ends before anything has had time to settle, and because the reader's pleasure is in the tension, not its release.
