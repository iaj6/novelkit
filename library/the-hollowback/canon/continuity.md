# Continuity

Hard facts this project must not violate at any stage of drafting.

1. The four party members are: **Sela Vane** (wizard's apprentice, female, age 17), **Corvin Ash** (former soldier, male, age 24), **Evren Cael** (half-elf rogue, male, age 22), and **Agda Stonebrook** (dwarven hedge-healer, female, age 31).
2. The party's contract is to find **Ren Ashford**, 19, younger sibling of **Maret Ashford**, a minor merchant in Thorngate.
3. Ren walked into the Hollowback three weeks before the story opens. He is alive, he is inside the Eastmark Waystation, and he does not want to be rescued.
4. Ren is not a victim and not a villain. He made a deliberate choice to go to the waystation and is choosing to stay. His reasons are real and coherent.
5. The Eastmark Waystation is a ruin of the **Vethic Empire**, a civilization that ended approximately three centuries before the story's present.
6. By the book's final chapter, the party possesses knowledge about the waystation that the Kingdom of Aldenmere does not. They have not reported it. This is a loose thread, not a resolution.
7. **Magic costs the caster's own vital energy.** Overuse causes real physical harm. This cost is never suspended, hand-waved, or forgotten. Sela pays it every time she casts.
8. Sela's four reliable spells: light cantrip, minor force-push, single-target sleep induction, the unraveling (mechanical lock dissolution). Any spell beyond these four is improvisation at unknown cost.
9. **Corvin's discharge:** He refused a legal order to act against civilians he knew to be non-combatants. He was right. He was still discharged. He has told no one the full version. The army's paperwork says dereliction of duty.
10. **Evren's hidden reason:** He has a personal connection to Vethic ruin exploration — someone who went into such a ruin and didn't come back. This is the real reason he took the job. He does not disclose this during the book.
11. **Agda's god Duhr** is real, answers when she calls, and is not recognized by the Church of Aldenmere. Agda's healing rituals include brief invocations to Duhr. Duhr is a deity of thresholds.
12. **Three romance pairings, none resolved by the final chapter:** Sela/Corvin (inter-party slow burn), Evren/Agda (inter-party comedic-becomes-real), Sela/Ren (external, complicated by the mission's terms). Each pairing gets at least three notable scenes.
13. **The party is fond of each other by the end.** The arc is strangers → reluctant colleagues → people who would actually die for each other. This is earned through shared action and stakes, not declared.
14. **At least four named action set pieces:** a road ambush, a dungeon-puzzle room inside the waystation, a combat against something the party should not be able to beat, and a confrontation resolved through character rather than combat.
15. **At least one moment of real awe** inside the Hollowback. It must be earned by what surrounds it.
16. **No chosen-one framing.** The party succeeds because of skill and luck. No prophecy, no "only you can" device.
17. **YA-readable throughout:** no graphic sex, no torture-pornographic violence. Combat is specific and has real stakes; it is not gratuitous.
18. **No DM-coded prose.** The narrative does not address a player, reference D&D mechanics, or break the fourth wall. This is a novel set in a fantasy world.
19. Worldbuilding emerges through use. No expository monologues explaining the world to the reader.
20. The book is **16 chapters, target ~56,000 words**, structured episodically with most chapters centered on a single encounter and two or three chapters as travel/character/banter beats.
