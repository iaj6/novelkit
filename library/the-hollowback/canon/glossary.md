# Glossary

Alphabetical. Later phases may add entries; do not remove or contradict existing entries without updating continuity.

---

**Agda Stonebrook** — Dwarven hedge-healer, age 31. Party member. Follower of Duhr. No Church certification.

**Aldenmere** — The kingdom in which the story is set. Human-dominant, mid-sized, with a professional army, a state Church, and a royal charter system.

**Ashford, Maret** — Minor merchant of Thorngate, mid-thirties. Posts the Ashford contract. Ren's older sibling.

**Ashford, Ren** — Nineteen-year-old former cartographer's apprentice. The mission objective. Found alive inside the Eastmark Waystation, choosing to remain.

**Charter holder** — A person or institution holding royal charter rights over a defined territory. In border regions like the Hollowback, charter holders may enforce their territorial rights without crown presence.

**Church (the)** — Aldenmere's state religious institution. Issues licenses for magic use. Recognizes deities it considers legitimate; declines to recognize others, including Duhr.

**Corvin Ash** — Former Aldenmere army sergeant, age 24. Party member. Discharged under circumstances the army's paperwork describes as dereliction of duty.

**Duhr** — Dwarven deity of thresholds: the boundary between living and dying, before and after, arriving and leaving. Agda's god. Answers when called. Not recognized by the Church of Aldenmere.

**Eastmark Waystation** — A Vethic Imperial ruin deep in the Hollowback, at the junction of two old imperial roads. Lower level intact. Contains a puzzle-trapped antechamber and something further in that should not still be there. Where Ren is found.

**Evren Cael** — Half-elf rogue, age 22. Party member. Lock-picking, infiltration, information, trap-handling. Hidden agenda regarding the Eastmark Waystation.

**Hollowback (the)** — Old-growth border forest east of Aldenmere's settled territory. Nominally within the kingdom's claims; practically ungoverned. Contains the Eastmark Waystation. Travelers who know what they're doing take the northern route.

**Sela Vane** — Wizard's apprentice, age 17. Primary POV character. Party member. Four reliable spells; learning what magic costs.

**Thorngate** — Mid-sized trading city where the story begins. The Ashford contract originates here.

**Unraveling (the)** — Sela's informal name for a spell that dissolves simple mechanical locks. Succeeds roughly two-thirds of the time; always costs her a headache.

**Vethic Empire (the)** — A civilization that preceded Aldenmere and all current kingdoms by approximately three centuries. Fell under disputed circumstances. Built the Eastmark Waystation, the roads the Hollowback has swallowed, and engineered stonework that outlasted the civilization itself.

**Founder's Street** — A street in Thorngate's trade district. The hiring hall where job contracts are posted is located here.

**Hiring hall (Thorngate)** — A narrow room on Founder's Street, Thorngate, smelling of chalk dust and old oilcloth. A board along the east wall displays available contracts. The standard mechanism for assembling adventuring parties in Thorngate.

**Copper Lane** — A street in Thorngate's warehouse district. Maret Ashford's import business warehouse is at number 14.

**The Toll** — Trade-district nickname for Thorngate's customs building. Nobody who works in the trade district uses the official name.

**Cartographer Dunne** — A cartographer based on the west road in Thorngate. Ren Ashford's former apprenticeship master; the apprenticeship ended the summer before the story opens.

**Millhaven** — The first named settlement east of Thorngate on the road toward the Hollowback.

**Carters' Cross** — The second named settlement east of Thorngate on the road toward the Hollowback.

**Edgemark** — The last named settlement on the eastern road before the road enters the Hollowback forest.

**The Wayward Ox** — An inn twelve miles east of Thorngate on the eastern road toward the Hollowback. One room with four pallets, a fire, and indifferent soup. The painted sign shows an ox that appears to have arrived rather than wandered.

**Duhr invocation (three-word form)** — Agda's practical, brief healing invocations to Duhr — three words, low and quick, spoken with the expectation of response. Not prayer in the Church sense; not instruction in the mundane sense. First rendered (from outside POV) in Ch02, when Agda speaks over a blister wrap.

**Survey notes (Eastmark Waystation)** — Old field notes describing the Eastmark Waystation's approach road, junction, and outer gateway stonework. Found by Evren approximately three years before the story opens, in a cartographer's discarded paper stack. Corners browned. Written in a hand Evren had come to know. Stop mid-page mid-sentence. Evren has them memorized.

**Radwick** — A former soldier from Corvin Ash's regiment — infantry, two grades below Corvin's sergeant rank during the Eastern Rotations. Met the party on the east road in the Hollowback as part of a deserter ambush on Day 3. He hung back, never engaged in the fight, and used the encounter to announce Corvin's discharge in front of the whole party: 'Ash. I heard you got derelicted. Heard the whole story, too.' Older than his companions; scar along his jaw from below his right ear toward his mouth. Departed west when Corvin gave him the instruction to do so. Minor character.

**Dereliction of duty** — The formal army term in Aldenmere's military for a soldier who abandons a position or refuses an order without a court-martial proceeding. The paperwork says what the army decided; it does not say why. This is the charge on Corvin Ash's discharge record. Named aloud for the first time in front of the party by Radwick on Day 3 of travel.

**Eastern Rotations** — A posting cycle in Aldenmere's army during which Corvin served as sergeant (his second year in that rank). Radwick was a subordinate during this period, two grades below Corvin. Referenced as the context in which Corvin recognizes Radwick's face.

**Meadow mallow (herb)** — One of the ingredients in Agda's dried herb bundle remedy for magic-cost headache. The bundle also contains two other unspecified herbs. Together: eat the bundle, drink water; reduces (does not eliminate) the held-spell physical cost by approximately thirty percent. First given to Sela in Ch04.

**Herb bundle (Agda's remedy)** — A small dried bundle, tied with cord, approximately thumb-length, containing meadow mallow and two other herbs from Agda's healer's kit. Used to partially relieve the physical cost of held-but-unreleased magic. Not a cure; a reduction. Established Ch04.

**Grenn Vott** — Hill-dwarf charter holder who controls the road checkpoint at the eastern Hollowback border junction. Mid-fifties, stocky, short practical beard, wears charter papers in a copper case at his belt. Has held the gate for 22 years. Not corrupt, not foolish, not wrong about the law. Knows the waystation exists and has never entered it. Gives Agda a private threshold warning in Dwarven before the party passes through (Ch05). Minor character with weight.

**Third Consolidation** — An Aldenmere Crown statute establishing public right-of-way provisions for certain roads. Predates Vott's charter grant for the Hollowback road junction by approximately 80 years. Evren had read this statute and attempted to use it to contest the surety bond requirement (Ch05).

**Charter Enforcement Supplement** — A supplement to the Aldenmere charter statutes, adopted 11 years before the Third Consolidation and therefore not superseded by it. Section Four specifies that unlicensed healers from non-accredited (i.e., non-Church) institutions require a surety bond. The legal basis for Vott's four-mark bond requirement for Agda (Ch05).

**Vel thanis (Duhr invocation)** — Short-form practical Duhr invocation used by Agda: 'Duhr, vel thanis.' The simple ask for focused healing attention. Response is warmth in the tissue, accelerated but not miraculous. Distinct from the three-word invocation over the blister wrap (Ch02). Established Ch05.

**Threshold practitioner** — A healer trained in Duhr's tradition who works the boundary between the living and dying. Agda is a threshold practitioner of the Stonebrook line, four generations back. The title has legal standing in old dwarven customary tradition and is the basis for Agda's invocation at Vott's checkpoint. Grenn Vott recognizes the title and its implications for the waystation warning.

**Vel-tharrak right (threshold walk)** — The pre-Aldenmere dwarven customary right of a threshold practitioner to walk border roads in service of the living. Predates Aldenmere crown law and Church certification. No standing in current crown law; recognized in practice by dwarven charter holders. Invoked by Agda at Vott's checkpoint (Ch05) to resolve the surety bond dispute.

**Vethic road (Hollowback)** — The paved road built by the Vethic Empire through what is now the Hollowback forest. Survives intact under three centuries of forest floor — geometrically flat stone, joins so tight a fingernail cannot be inserted, darker and harder than local limestone. The forest grew over it rather than through it. Intermittently visible where roots have not covered it. Produces a barely-visible thermal shimmer in afternoon light (stored heat releasing into cooler air). Descends into a flooded, impassable section east of the low rise where the party found it in Ch06.

**Ren's working notes** — Three pages of field notes in Ren Ashford's handwriting, given to Sela by Maret at the warehouse office. Contain survey observations, measurements in Vethic notation (student hand, confident in some marks), and a sketch of what appears to be the outer gateway configuration of the Eastmark Waystation. Distinct from Ren's departure note and from the hand-annotated map in Maret's office. Sela carries these throughout the journey.

**Lira** — The cartographer Evren knew personally. She went to the Eastmark Waystation to verify the survey notes she had written and did not come back. She taught Evren Vethic survey notation. Her name exists only in Evren's interior thoughts; it is never spoken aloud in the book and never disclosed to the party. First named in Ch07.

**Medway Lane** — A street in Thorngate. The location of the cartographer's office where Evren found Lira's discarded survey notes three years before the story opens. The back of the office, not the front.

**Magnetic deviation zone (Hollowback)** — A navigational feature of the Hollowback near the Eastmark Waystation's approach road. A 1.5-degree north magnetic deviation documented by Vethic Empire surveyors. Without correction, the traveler arrives at the wrong section of the waystation's approach road. Evren knew of this from Lira's survey notes and corrected for it silently on Day 4.

**Antechamber (Eastmark Waystation)** — The first room inside the Eastmark Waystation's lower level, accessed by a 16-step stairwell. Rectangular, five meters by ten meters. Contains: a full-floor grid of pressure plates (nine-square safe path marked by Ren in chalk on the left wall); an iron combination-ring door at the far end (Vethic notation, Ren's solution scratched on the frame); and a secondary magic-sensing trap calibrated to active magical signatures. All three obstacles navigated by the party in Ch08.

**Magic-sensing trap (antechamber)** — A secondary security mechanism in the Eastmark Waystation antechamber, activated partway through the combination-lock sequence when an active magic signature is present. Manifests as a pressure change in the air and a harmonic vibration in the floor. Requires the caster to drop all active magic within approximately four seconds to avoid escalation. Calibrated to Vethic-style magic but close enough to modern casting to trigger on a light cantrip.

**Combination-ring door** — The iron door at the far end of the Eastmark Waystation antechamber. Three concentric rings, each marked with Vethic notation, rotating on a central spindle. Must be aligned in a specific sequence (astronomical positions plus a Vethic date-cycle correction) to open. Ren scratched the correct sequence onto the door frame. Opens on a center pivot via a counterweight system — completely silent.

**Preservation dust** — The undisturbed accumulation of centuries of fine particulate matter on the lower-level corridor floor of the Eastmark Waystation. Because the space has been sealed, the dust is an even, flat coating that records footsteps with precision. Ren Ashford's three-week-old prints are visible in it going left at the first corridor branch.

**Vethic survey equipment (chest set)** — A set of Vethic Imperial survey instruments found sealed in an iron-banded chest in the third storage chamber of the Eastmark Waystation's lower level. Contents: a sighting tube in brass and Vethic hard stone engraved with Vethic notational scales; four measuring arms (pinned at one end); a meridian indicator corrected for the local 1.5-degree magnetic deviation; a folded Vethic vellum survey sheet. All items still intact and functional after three centuries. Evren recognized the sighting tube from Lira's descriptions of it.

**Containment chamber** — A room three levels below the garrison corridor of the Eastmark Waystation, documented in the Vethic architect's schematic on Ren's vellum maps. Notation: 'reinforced, double threshold, not for maintenance access.' Marked with a closed-ring survey symbol indicating active operation. Ren's margin annotation beside it reads: 'it is still here.' Not yet physically reached by the party as of Ch09.

**Closed-ring symbol (Vethic survey notation)** — A Vethic survey symbol indicating a system in active operation — a mechanism still running at the time of survey or, in the architectural schematic context of the waystation, continuously running. Found on the third vellum sheet in Ren's workspace, marking the containment chamber.

**Vethic archive (Eastmark Waystation)** — A room in the lower levels of the Eastmark Waystation, two levels below the garrison corridor, originally used to store records or documents. Most of its shelving and contents collapsed centuries ago. The northeast corner was intact and converted by Ren Ashford into a working space. It is reached by a secondary service stair (18 steps), distinct from the main garrison approach.

**Containment chamber (Eastmark Waystation)** — A large vaulted room five levels below the surface of the Eastmark Waystation, accessible via a narrow pale-stone passage and a massive iron pivot-door with four locking mechanisms. At its center stands the Vethic containment structure housing the Hollowed One. The pale-stone panels of the structure carry carved channels through which a cold pale light runs continuously. The air vibrates imperceptibly around the housing. First seen by the party in Ch10.

**Vethic containment structure** — A precision housing at the center of the Eastmark Waystation's containment chamber. Dark Vethic alloy uprights at geometric intervals; vertical pale-stone panels with carved channels between them; a cold pale moving light in the channels. Built to contain the Hollowed One. Engineered to the room's exact center. Distinguished from a cage by its precision and deliberate engineering. First seen Ch10.

**Hollowed One** — The entity contained in the Eastmark Waystation's containment chamber. Human-shaped in proportion (head, torso, limbs). Not moving, not still — the stillness of suspension rather than motionlessness. First seen visually in Ch10 without a name; explanation deferred to Ch11. Described by Vott (Ch05) as something that does not respect the threshold between living and dead. Draws continuously on local magical energy, creating a gradient Sela's cantrip pulls against below the second landing of the descent.

**Hollowed One (name)** — The name used by the party (first introduced by Ren in Ch11) for the Vethic experimental subject in the Eastmark Waystation containment chamber. A volunteer mage whose vital energy was partially extracted in a failed transfer procedure three hundred years ago. Suspended between living and dead by the containment structure's filter mechanisms, which are degrading. Not dead; not alive; not at rest (Duhr's verdict via Agda). No functioning sensory orientation.

**Vital-energy transfer program (Vethic)** — A Vethic Empire late-period research program aimed at extracting vital energy from a living donor and transferring it to an object or another person, eliminating the cost-to-the-caster problem. The Eastmark Waystation was one of three research sites. The extraction worked; the transfer stage failed. The Empire fell before the problem was resolved. The subject remained in suspension in the containment structure.

**Containment chamber grille** — A small iron opening approximately a palm's width, set into the upper frame of the containment chamber's iron door at standing eye level. Provides a view into the housing interior from outside the chamber without requiring the door to be opened. First used by Agda in Ch11 to observe the Hollowed One before the party left the containment level.

**Filter mechanisms (containment structure)** — The Vethic-engineered components of the containment structure that limit the Hollowed One's draw radius. Design rate: infinitesimal draw, below any organism's detection threshold. Current state (Ch11): degraded but not failed. Current draw radius: approximately six meters past the housing. When filters fail completely: draw radius extends to a minimum of twenty meters, the Hollowed One becomes mobile. Ren estimates two weeks to a month until failure.

**Sealing nodes (containment mechanism)** — The three mechanical lock components within the Eastmark Waystation's containment structure that fix the channel configuration in place. Two remain operable with external engagement. The third has partially fused to the surrounding mechanism via corrosion and requires Sela's unraveling to address. The designed engagement sequence is fused node first, then the two operable nodes in the designated order. Established Ch12.

**Halveth** — The Duhr word for the space between an ending and what comes next — the not-yet-over and the not-yet-begun, the threshold between the door that has just closed and the door that has not opened yet. No Common equivalent. Spoken by Agda at doorways and thresholds to mark a completed action's settling. Duhr responds to halveth with the sensation of a door settling properly into its frame: the threshold settled. First spoken aloud in Ch14 at the Eastmark Waystation's exit.

**Duhr door-settling response** — Duhr's answer to halveth: the sensation of a door settling into its frame with the solid finality of a door properly hung. Not triumph; not warmth. The specific functional rightness of a threshold allowed to close. Agda's internal name for it: 'the threshold settled.' Distinct from the door-held-open-by-force sensation Duhr communicated about the Hollowed One in Ch11. First received by Agda in Ch14 at the waystation's exit.

**Agda's sustained healing pass** — A full twelve-minute Duhr healing pass requiring settled mind, both hands, and a fixed position. Calls Duhr in the register used for real wounds — naming specifically what has been spent and what needs to mend. Distinguished from the short-form vel thanis (the quick practical ask). Performed on Sela in Ch14 after the sealing; performed twice previously by Agda (once on herself, once on another patient).

**Vott's parting acknowledgment** — 'Still here' — the two words Grenn Vott said in Dwarven to Agda as she passed through his checkpoint on the party's return (Day 9, Ch15). Agda said 'Still here' back. Corvin interpreted it as the specific acknowledgment of someone who had prepared for the possibility of not seeing someone again and is noting that they did. Not a greeting; not small talk; a precise observation with weight in it.

**Merchant Quarter** — A street or district in Thorngate. Location of the apothecary Agda uses for healing supply restocks — the only Thorngate source for at least one item in the correct grade. Agda walked north to Merchant Quarter after the contract closed on Day 12.

**Sela's master's house** — A two-story building with mismatched shutters on a lane off the corner of Founder's Street, Thorngate. Six doors down the lane. The shutters have been mismatched since before Sela's apprenticeship. The study has northern windows, books stacked to the left of the entry, a worn desk, and candles at low burn. The master uses a slightly-too-thin ink that spreads on application.

**Final margin note (Sela's map)** — A note Sela wrote on the eastern edge of her annotated Hollowback map at the end of the book, standing before her master's door on Day 12. Content not shown. She folded the map and pocketed it before crossing the threshold.
