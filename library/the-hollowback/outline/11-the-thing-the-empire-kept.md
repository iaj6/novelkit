# Chapter 11 — The Thing the Empire Kept

**One-line summary:** Ren explains what's in the containment chamber; Agda's god of thresholds has strong opinions about it.

**POV character:** Agda Stonebrook
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** The containment chamber and the vaulted room outside it. Day 5, night. They are not going anywhere tonight.
**Target words:** ~3,500

---

## Chapter Shape
**Interiority-heavy.** Ren talks; the party listens; Agda processes. This chapter is Agda's confrontation with what the Empire built, rendered through her particular interior register: clinical, concrete, and — in the private layer the narration allows access to — deeply moved. The theology of thresholds is at its most present here. Duhr answers when called, but the answer is not simple.

## Ending Mode
**Unresolved.** They understand what the Hollowed One is. They understand the containment is degrading. They do not yet have a plan. They go back up to Ren's workspace for the night, leaving the chamber behind. The weight of what they've learned goes with them.

---

## Goal
Understand what they're dealing with. Agda wants to know if it can be helped — not as a question about whether healing is applicable, but as a question about whether this thing is alive in any sense that Duhr would recognize.

## Obstacle
The Hollowed One is not something in her tradition's experience, exactly. The threshold between living and dead is Duhr's domain — but this thing has been suspended at the threshold for three hundred years by Imperial mechanisms rather than by any natural process. It is the Empire's violation of the threshold, preserved. What does Duhr say about a violation that has been running uninterrupted for three centuries?

---

## The Hollowed One — Full Reveal

Ren explains what he has pieced together from the Imperial documentation in the archive:

The Vethic Empire's late-period mages were studying the mechanism of vital energy — the same principle that makes magic cost a caster's physical reserves. They believed that vital energy could be transferred: extracted from one living being and installed in an object or another being, creating a vehicle for power that didn't require the caster to pay. The research was considered high-value and extremely dangerous. The Eastmark Waystation was one of three research sites.

The subject in the chamber — Ren has found documentation indicating it was a volunteer, a Vethic mage who agreed to the procedure under terms that did not include "and you will spend three centuries in a suspended cage" — had most of their vital energy extracted. Not all. Enough remained to keep the biological substrate alive in a kind of undead suspension. The extracted energy was stored in the containment structure itself. The experiment failed at the transfer stage — the Empire fell before they could complete it, or the transfer mechanism never worked properly, or both. The subject remained in the cage. The extracted energy remained in the cage. The thing that remains is neither fully alive nor fully dead: it exists at the threshold, hungering to be whole, drawing the vital energy back in from anything living that comes close enough.

This is the Hollowed One. It has been drawing infinitesimally on the Hollowback's living things for three centuries, through the cage's degrading filters. The rate is too slow for any organism to notice. But the filters are nearly gone now. When they fail, the draw will become significant, and immediate proximity — the kind the party has been providing — will cost people their strength very quickly.

## Action Beats

1. **Ren's explanation.** He has prepared for this conversation. He has notes. He explains what the experiment was, what went wrong, what has been running in the chamber since. He is precise and slightly too confident — he is explaining something enormous to people who are reacting to it, and he is so accustomed to being alone with this knowledge that he has lost track of how it lands. Agda watches his face while he talks. She identifies: a nineteen-year-old who has been alone with a terrible fact for three weeks and is relieved, in some visible way, not to be alone with it anymore.

2. **Agda calls Duhr.** Not a long invocation — three words and a silence and her hand open in her lap. The way she explains it if asked: Duhr is the deity of thresholds; this is a threshold question. She wants to know what she's looking at from her god's perspective. What she gets back is not language — it is a feeling, the specific quality of the space around a threshold that is being held open by force, rather than passing through naturally. Like a door being kept open. Like something that should have been allowed to close three hundred years ago and was not. Duhr's response, as she translates it for herself: *this is not dead. It is not alive. It is a wound that has not been allowed to heal.* She tells the party: *it is not at rest.* That's all she says aloud.

3. **What this means for the containment.** Evren asks the practical question: *how much longer does the containment hold?* Ren consults his notes. His estimate: weeks, maybe a month. The filters are not broken; they are worn. The draw has already been slightly elevated for the two years before he arrived — locals who go too close to the waystation come back tired, which explains the charter holder's description. When the filters fail completely, the draw radius extends. Anything living within twenty meters will feel it. The Hollowed One itself will become mobile.

4. **Agda's private processing.** While the others argue over Ren's timeline and Sela maps the implications on the margin of her Thorngate map, Agda goes back to the containment chamber door and looks through the grille at the top. At the Hollowed One. It does not look at her — it has no functioning sensory orientation, Ren has said. But the air near the grille is wrong: cold in a way that is not temperature, pulling in a way that is not quite breath. She thinks about what three hundred years of holding open a door must feel like. She does not know if it can feel. She thinks about what Duhr would say about a threshold being held open for three centuries by a machine. She thinks: *that was someone's person.* She goes back to the others.

5. **The discharge question, finally.** In the exhausted quiet of the discussion, Corvin says something that prompts Agda to realize: he is processing this waystation as a soldier. The garrison pulled out when the Empire fell and left this problem for someone else. He says — very quietly, to no one in particular — *they left it running.* He says it with the specific weight of someone who knows what it is to have the people above you make decisions that someone below them has to live with. Agda looks at him. He doesn't return the look. She thinks: *yes, that is what happened to you, isn't it.* She does not say this. She keeps the thought, neatly and carefully, and it costs her something to do so.

6. **Going back up.** The group decision: they cannot resolve this tonight. They will sleep in Ren's workspace and make a decision in the morning. Ren says he has enough provisions for two days more if they share. He says this with the practical matter-of-factness of someone who has already worked out the arithmetic. Agda notes: he is very young and very alone and has been managing this for three weeks without losing himself. She finds this, against her better judgment, admirable.

---

## Outcome / Turn
The Hollowed One is understood: a Vethic experimental subject, suspended in threshold state, contained in a degrading cage. It is not dead. It cannot be allowed out. The containment has weeks, maybe. And the party — including Ren — now has to decide what to do about this.

## New Question / Pressure
Is the containment going to hold through the time needed to make and execute a decision? And who makes that decision — the party, Ren, someone with more authority than any of them?
