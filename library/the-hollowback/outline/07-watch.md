# Chapter 07 — Watch

**One-line summary:** A sleepless night in the Hollowback; Evren finds himself quiet, and Agda notices.

**POV character:** Evren Cael
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** The camp from Ch 6, deep night into very early morning. Fire low. The forest is still. Day 5 begins at the chapter's end.
**Target words:** ~2,500

---

## Chapter Shape
**Bottle.** One location, near real-time. The chapter spans a few hours of Evren's watch in the dark, interrupted briefly by Agda waking early. Tight space, low action, high interiority. The humor in Evren's POV is present but quieter than usual — this is one of the chapters where the joke being absent is more significant than the joke being present. The serious moment in his interiority does not announce itself; it is surrounded by the ordinary texture of a cold watch and therefore starker.

## Ending Mode
**Turn.** Morning arrives. They find Ren's three-week-old camp debris — food wrapping, a small cairn of stones, a notation scratched into a tree in Vethic shorthand. The notation confirms: Ren read the same maps Evren has been thinking about. And the way Evren reads that notation — fluently, immediately, without hesitating — is something he does before he realizes the others might notice.

---

## Goal
Evren is on watch. He should be watching the forest. He is mostly watching his own thoughts, which is worse.

## Obstacle
He knows what's in the waystation. Or he suspects. Or he suspects he knows, which is worse than not knowing. He has been here before in his head, a hundred times, and now he is here in the forest, and the forest is confirming things he would have preferred to keep abstract.

## Action Beats

1. **The watch begins.** The others are asleep. The fire has burned to coals. Evren sits with his back to a tree at the edge of the firelight where he can see in multiple directions — he does this without thinking, the same way Corvin always faces the door. He does not always think about where that habit comes from. He is thinking about it tonight.

2. **Evren's internal inventory.** The Hollowback at night: no wind, the sound of water somewhere off to the east, a quality of silence that is not actually silent but is the sound of very old trees doing very old things. He runs through what he knows — the survey notes he'd found three years ago, the cartographer's record that first named the waystation, the fact that the cartographer had gone there to verify the survey and hadn't come back. Her name was Lira. He does not say this in the text; he thinks it in the way a person thinks a name they've repeated so many times it has worn smooth: *she said the road would still be there, and she was right about that.* He stops. The coals pop.

3. **What he's actually doing here.** He's here because the waystation was the last place she was known to be going and the contract gave him a path inside without explaining why he wanted one. He does not need to rescue Ren. He needs to know what happened to her. These two objectives are overlapping in a way that feels convenient and is probably not. He has not told the party. He is not going to tell the party. He is aware that not telling them is a calculation he keeps making and that the calculation is becoming harder to justify the more the party becomes people he would actually be sorry to get killed.

4. **The forest noise.** Something moves at the edge of his vision — the same thing Sela half-woke to, gone before he can track it. He doesn't relax after it passes. His hand is on his knife for five full minutes. Then nothing, and he lets go.

5. **Agda wakes.** Not her watch. She sits up, looks at him, and apparently reads his face in the dark better than she should be able to, because she comes to sit near the fire instead of going back to sleep. She doesn't ask what he's thinking about. She stokes the coals. After a while she says: *You know more about where we're going than you've said.* It is not a question. It is a statement delivered in Agda's particular register: flat, certain, not accusatory. Evren's first response is to prepare a deflection, and then he notices she's watching him prepare the deflection, and he puts it down. He says: *Some.* She nods. She does not push. He is aware that she could push and chose not to, and this is — for reasons he does not want to examine — the most unsettling thing that has happened since the ambush.

6. **The almost.** They sit by the fire for twenty minutes and Evren does not make a single joke. This is possibly the longest he has gone without making a joke since he was fourteen. Agda doesn't comment on this. She just sits there, which is more than he expected, and the warmth of the fire is good. He notices at some point that she has fallen asleep sitting up, which dwarves apparently can do, and he sits for another hour feeling something he does not have a good word for and would greatly prefer not to examine.

7. **Dawn: Ren's traces.** First light. Evren walks the camp perimeter as the others wake, and finds it: twenty meters into the trees, a three-week-old camp. Ren's, definitely — the food wrapping is Thorngate-made, and there's a small cairn of four stones that might be navigation marking. And scratched into a birch tree at eye height: a line of Vethic survey notation. Evren reads it before he realizes the others are watching him read it. The notation is a bearing marker — Ren was confirming his heading for the waystation — and Evren reads it fluently, without the pause of someone sounding out an unfamiliar script. He looks up. Sela is watching him with the specific expression she uses when she has filed something. He says: *Old cartographer's trick. You pick it up.* She says nothing. He has never once picked up anything from a cartographer; he learned this notation because Lira taught him. He does not say this.

---

## Outcome / Turn
Morning: Ren's camp found, the bearing marker read. The party knows they are within a day of the waystation. Evren knows he made an error in the notation moment and cannot unsay it. Sela knows he made an error. Neither of them names it yet.

## New Question / Pressure
Evren reads Vethic notation fluently. He did not learn it as a rogue. Where did he learn it, and what does it say about what he knows and hasn't disclosed?

## Continuity Notes
- Lira: Evren's private name for the person who didn't come back. She was a cartographer. She taught him Vethic notation. He does not say her name aloud in this chapter or any other; it exists only in his interior in Ch 7 and possibly a brief interior mention in Ch 9. She is never disclosed to the party.
- Evren reads Vethic notation: this is a planted fact that will matter in Ch 8 (antechamber) and Ch 9 (Ren's notes).
- The thing moving at the camp's edge: same as what Sela heard in Ch 6. Ambiguous. Not named.
- Agda waking unprompted and sitting with Evren: this is the first real Evren/Agda scene — not banter, but a specific kind of company. She said "you know more than you've said" and didn't push when he confirmed it. This is trust being extended without being demanded.

## Romance Threads in This Chapter
- **Evren/Agda:** Third and most significant beat so far. She wakes, reads him, sits with him, doesn't push him, and falls asleep near him. He sits for an hour afterward feeling something he doesn't have a word for. The joke mechanism has failed completely in her presence and he is only beginning to understand this is a symptom, not a lapse.
