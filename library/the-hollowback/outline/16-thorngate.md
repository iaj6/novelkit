# Chapter 16 — Thorngate

**One-line summary:** Back in Thorngate: Maret, the payment, the knowledge they're keeping, and nothing resolved.

**POV character:** Sela Vane
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** Thorngate — the east gate, Maret's warehouse office, the street outside, and finally the trade district at dusk. Day 12.
**Target words:** ~3,000

---

## Chapter Shape
**Multi-scene.** Three or four distinct beats in Thorngate, each one a closure that is also an opening. The chapter is a coda — not resolution, but the shape of what things look like after resolution has been declined. Sela's POV closes the book because she opened it; the arc of her chapter voice, from careful and uncertain to careful and something more, is the book's through-line given its final form.

## Ending Mode
**Coda.** The story is told. The world has changed slightly. Nothing is settled. Everyone knows where they stand; the characters mostly don't.

---

## Goal
Complete the contract. Decide what the party is now that the contract is over.

## Obstacle
The contract is easy to complete. What comes after the contract has no map.

## Action Beats

1. **Into Thorngate.** The east gate at mid-morning. Thorngate smells like rope and bread and faintly like dye from the fabric district. Sela has been here her whole life and the city looks slightly different than it did eleven days ago — not the city, she thinks. The angle of the light. She is aware she has this thought and notes it. She has changed the calibration on something.

2. **Ren's homecoming.** They bring Ren to Maret's warehouse office. The reunion is not Sela's to narrate — she stands near the door with the other party members, who are all doing the same thing: giving the siblings the space to be siblings. Maret holds her brother for a long time. She cries briefly and then she stops and she becomes again the competent merchant, looking at the four strangers who brought him back. She says thank you in a way that means it. She pays the fee.

3. **Maret's complicated feelings.** Maret asks what happened. Ren gives her a version of it — true but incomplete, the professional version. He says the waystation was not empty, the situation has been addressed, and he'll explain the full picture when they're home. Maret's face does a thing that Sela reads as: *I am relieved and I am certain there is more to this and I am choosing, right now, to be relieved first.* She gives Ren the look of someone deciding to let the more wait. Sela respects this. Maret is not naive; she is choosing her order.

4. **The payment, and what it means.** The fee in Sela's hand: the amount they agreed to, which is not very much, for the work they did. She does the arithmetic of her master's debt and it helps more than it doesn't. This is the job she took the job for. She stands on the street outside Maret's office with coins in her hand and thinks about what she came here as — a seventeen-year-old apprentice who needed money and didn't quite understand what the Hollowback meant — and what she came back as. The coins are the same weight as they were when she agreed to take them.

5. **The party on the street.** The four of them outside Maret's office, plus Ren, who has followed them out to say his own goodbye. Evren says something that is technically a joke about the pay-to-danger ratio, and the joke is real but not quite at full power, which means he is feeling something he is managing through the joke mechanism. Agda says the job is done. Corvin says: *It went all right.* Evren says: *We lived.* Agda says: *That's the standard.* The exchange is short and it means more than it says.

6. **Ren and Sela, the last beat.** Ren says goodbye specifically to Sela, which he doesn't owe her any more than he owes it to the others, but he does it. He says: *You asked the right question.* He means: in the waystation, when she asked what was in the containment chamber. She says: *You left the door open for us.* She means: the notation on the antechamber doorframe. He understands what she means. He says: *If you need a copy of the documentation — when you decide what to do with it — I'll know where you are.* She says: *Yes.* He says: *Good.* He goes back inside. She stands on the street for a moment. She thinks about the alarm she felt when she first found him interesting. She notes that the alarm is still present. She notes that she is choosing, right now, to let it wait.

7. **Corvin and Sela, the last beat.** The party has been dispersing by ones — Agda to restock her kit, Evren to return the crossbow to someone he borrowed it from (he says he borrowed it; this is probably not true; nobody asks). Corvin is still standing on the street when Sela turns around. He has been waiting, which is something Corvin does not advertise but is doing. She says: *What are you going to do now?* He says: *Haven't decided.* She says: *You could stay available. In case this comes up again.* He looks at her for a moment. He says: *It probably will.* She says: *Yes.* He says: *All right.* He starts to walk. She falls into step beside him without deciding to, and they walk down the trade district street in the late afternoon light, and she is aware that she is walking beside him at the exact pace that means they are going the same direction without having said so. She notes this. She keeps walking.

8. **The last beat: Sela alone.** Brief. She splits from Corvin at the corner where her master's street begins. She has the coins. She has a map with margin notes that are considerably more detailed now than when she left. She has four reliable spells — and, potentially, the knowledge of where her threshold actually is, which she spent eleven days finding out. She walks toward her master's door. The city is loud and familiar and smells like rope. She takes out the annotated map and writes one last margin note, which the reader doesn't see. She folds it and keeps walking.

---

## Outcome / Turn
The contract is complete. Ren is home. The payment is made. The knowledge of the waystation is in two copies, held by the party, not reported. Nothing is settled — not the romance threads, not the decision about the Hollowed One's long-term fate, not the question of what the party is now. The book ends on the party dispersed but not disbanded, in the city they started in, changed in ways they have not yet named.

## New Question / Pressure (for the sequel, or the reader's imagination)
The kingdom does not know what is sitting in the Imperial ruin. The party knows. The sealing will hold for years — but years end. What happens when they decide who to tell, or when they decide the decision cannot wait any longer?

## Continuity Notes
- **Continuity item 6 honored:** By the final chapter, the party possesses knowledge the Kingdom does not. They have not reported it. This is a loose thread, not a resolution.
- **The party is fond of each other by the end (Continuity item 13):** Honored throughout this chapter and Ch 15. The fondness is never declared; it is rendered in the way they stand together on the street and in the specific quality of Corvin waiting.
- Sela's map with margin notes: the book opened with her annotating maps. It closes with her making one more notation. The content is not shown — it is hers.
- Sela's threshold knowledge: "the knowledge of where her threshold actually is, which she spent eleven days finding out." This is the payoff on her arc — she was an apprentice who didn't know her own limits. She does now.
- The party does not formally agree to stay together; they do not sign a new contract or form an official party. But Sela walks with Corvin toward the corner without deciding to. This is the brief's instruction: *the party is fond of each other by the end.* It shows.

## Romance Threads in This Chapter
- **Sela/Ren:** Third major beat, and final. He says goodbye specifically. She makes a joke about the open door; he understands it. The situation forecloses the easy version — he's going home, she's going back to her apprenticeship, the mission's terms have ended without creating the circumstances for anything to begin. She notes the alarm is still there and lets it wait. This is how this thread ends: open, real, not resolved.
- **Sela/Corvin:** Third and final major beat. He waited for her. She walks beside him without deciding to. Neither of them names it. They split at the corner. The thread does not resolve; it is exactly as open and warm as it was during the night watch, and no closer to resolution. The reader knows where they stand. The characters mostly don't.
- **Evren/Agda:** (Not directly seen in Sela's POV, but present in texture.) Evren makes a joke at something close to full power — getting there. Agda says *that's the standard.* They disperse in the same direction. Nobody comments. The thread remains open and warmer than either of them has admitted.
