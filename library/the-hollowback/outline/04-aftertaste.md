# Chapter 04 — Aftertaste

**One-line summary:** Ambush aftermath; Sela pays in full for what she almost cast, and watches Corvin not explain anything.

**POV character:** Sela Vane
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** Camp at dusk and into early night, day 3. They've made camp off the road in a clearing with a fire. The scrubland is behind them; the first real forest smell is starting.
**Target words:** ~3,000

---

## Chapter Shape
**Interiority-heavy.** The action was last chapter. This chapter is the night after: what people do in the absence of immediate danger. Sela's cataloging interiority is at its most focused. She is not quite over the physical effect of having held a spell prepared — even that costs something. She watches the others. She thinks carefully and says almost none of it aloud.

## Ending Mode
**Unresolved.** Corvin doesn't explain his discharge. Sela doesn't ask. The road ahead is unclear enough that the party goes to sleep with several things unresolved. The forest has started, and it sounds different from the scrubland.

---

## Goal
Sela wants to understand what she signed up for — the people, the job, the thing she doesn't know about Corvin. She does not want to need things she doesn't have.

## Obstacle
She is slightly wrung out from holding magic prepared during the fight. Her body's cost is real even for the spell she didn't cast — preparation draws something. Also: Corvin is very good at not answering questions, and she hasn't quite decided if she's going to try.

## Action Beats

1. **Making camp.** The physical work of making camp while Sela is slightly more tired than she should be after a fight she didn't participate in. She takes inventory of the feeling: light-headedness, a faint pressure behind her eyes. She kept the force-push ready for nearly six minutes. That's not nothing. She builds the fire anyway because no one else has started it and it needs to be started.

2. **Agda checks everyone.** Agda does a quick clinical pass over all four of them — checks Corvin's forearm where one of the deserters grazed him, checks Evren's knuckles, stops in front of Sela and looks at her for three seconds. Sela says she's fine. Agda says: *You held something.* This is not a question. She hands Sela a small dried-herb bundle and says to eat it and drink something. Then she moves on. Sela eats the herbs. They taste like old grass. Her headache fades about thirty percent.

3. **Evren processes.** Evren is, unusually, quiet. Not performing quiet — actually quiet. He is cleaning the crossbow he took from the ambusher (he has kept it, which tracks). Sela watches him work and thinks about the moment in the road when he moved to take the crossbowman out: he was fast in a way that implied serious practice or something worse. She files this. Not asking. She is collecting information about all three of them without comment, and she is vaguely aware that this is a habit she has and that the habit is not entirely comfortable to have.

4. **Corvin at the fire.** Corvin is sitting with his knife out, cleaning it — the practical version, not the aggressive version. Sela observes: the way he's sitting, he can see the road. He has not sat with his back to the road once since they left Thorngate. She notes this as a professional habit carried by instinct, not by current threat. The question she does not ask: what he did. The question she does ask, out loud, is whether the deserters were likely to come back. He says no. One word, conclusive. She believes him.

5. **The discharge comes up anyway.** Not because anyone asks. Evren, from across the fire, says: *That man knew you.* Not an accusation, not even quite a question — more like the verbal equivalent of noting that it rained. Corvin looks at him. Long pause. Corvin says: *Seven years in the Aldenmere infantry.* Another pause. *It ended.* That is the entire explanation. Evren opens his mouth. Agda, without looking up from the cloth she's waxing, says: *Leave it.* Evren closes his mouth. Agda does not look at Corvin. Corvin does not look at Agda. Sela logs the exchange with the particular sharpness of something that will be important later.

6. **Sela and Corvin, briefly.** After the others have turned in, Sela is still awake by the fire, re-annotating the map. Corvin is on first watch — sitting a little outside the firelight. He says, without preamble: *You held a spell during the fight.* She says yes. He says: *I noticed you decided not to use it.* She says the fight didn't need it. He's quiet for a moment. Then he says: *Good decision.* This is, from Corvin, approximately a paragraph of emotional content. Sela marks it in her internal catalog under *significant* and does not show this on her face. She says thank you and goes to sleep.

7. **Night sounds.** Lying awake for a few minutes, Sela hears the forest: it sounds different from the scrubland. The trees are older. Distance is harder to judge by ear. She has a brief, clear thought about the fact that she is three days from Thorngate with three people she's known for three days, walking into a forest that travelers who know what they're doing avoid. She notes this as a fact. It does not stop her from sleeping.

---

## Outcome / Turn
The party is functioning. Corvin's past has been poked and not opened; Agda foreclosed the conversation in a way that revealed she has opinions about it. Sela has been seen — her non-cast was noticed by the two most observant people in the group. The camp is quiet. The morning will bring the first real edge of the Hollowback.

## New Question / Pressure
Agda said "leave it" to Evren. Not "he'll tell us eventually" or "it's not our business." Just *leave it* — with the specific weight of someone who already knows more than she's saying. Does Agda know what happened?

## Continuity Notes
- Sela's magic cost: holding a spell prepared costs something even if it isn't released. This chapter makes explicit what the brief requires: the cost is real, physical, and never suspended.
- The herbs Agda gives Sela are part of Duhr-adjacent healing — practical, efficacious, no drama. The magic cost is reduced but not eliminated. Sela's headache was real.
- Agda's intervention ("Leave it") is a planted fact: she has guessed something about Corvin's discharge that she is keeping to herself. This pays off in Ch 11.
- The crossbow Evren keeps is a minor continuity item — he should have it available in later chapters if needed.
- Corvin's watch-placement: always able to see the road or door. Consistent behavior, established here explicitly.

## Romance Threads in This Chapter
- **Sela/Corvin:** He noticed her non-cast. He said *good decision.* This is the most he has said to her directly that wasn't purely functional. She catalogs it under *significant.* Nobody makes a move; the significance is in the registration. First real beat of the Sela/Corvin thread.
