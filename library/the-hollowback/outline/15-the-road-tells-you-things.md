# Chapter 15 — The Road Tells You Things

**One-line summary:** The long road home — the party fond of each other, everything still unsaid.

**POV character:** Corvin Ash
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** The Hollowback and the east road back toward Thorngate. Days 8–11. The landscape is running in reverse: old forest, then scrubland, then farmland, then the city's fringe. Ren is with them.
**Target words:** ~3,500

---

## Chapter Shape
**Time-skip / montage.** Four days of road compressed into a series of vivid moments, each one a relationship beat, a piece of processing, or a piece of the decision about what to do. Corvin's POV is the right instrument here: spare, observational, organized around what he sees rather than what he feels, with the feelings present in the exact facts he chooses to observe. His interiority is at its most characteristic — the narration does not announce that he is fond of these people. It shows what he notices about them.

## Ending Mode
**Unresolved.** They are close to Thorngate. They have made the preliminary decision about the knowledge. The romance threads are unresolved — specifically, a near-moment between Corvin and Sela that neither of them completes. The chapter ends with the city lights visible in the distance, and the party on the road, and everything still open.

---

## Goal
Get home. Protect the party through the road back. Decide what to do with what they know.

## Obstacle
The road is not dangerous now — no ambush on the return trip. The obstacle is interior. Corvin has been running a parallel calculation since the chamber: something is different about the four of them. He cannot articulate this in the vocabulary he normally uses for things. He keeps checking sightlines and finding them clear and not knowing what to do with the surplus attention.

## Action Beats

1. **Day 8: Leaving the waystation behind.** They leave the waystation exterior at first light, Sela walking unassisted (she is not fine but she is fine enough). Ren carries his documentation case and a pack and a badly cleaned sword he will probably never use. He fits into the party's walking order without ceremony — between Sela and Agda, which is where he ends up because those are the two people he has had the most real conversation with in the last two days. Corvin watches the new shape of the group and adjusts his coverage.

2. **The charter gate.** They reach Grenn Vott's checkpoint on Day 9. Vott is there. He looks at the five of them — looks at Agda specifically, at her bandaged hand, at the party's general depleted quality — and says nothing except to wave them through. He says, to Agda alone, as she passes: *Still here.* She says: *Still here.* This is, Corvin observes, Dwarven for something he cannot quite translate — satisfaction, maybe, or acknowledgment, or *I thought you'd make it.* He files it.

3. **Ren and Sela.** Two stretches of road where they walk together. Corvin observes this from a distance with no editorial comment. He notes: Ren talks and Sela listens, and then Sela asks something and Ren stops and thinks about it before answering, which apparently means she asked a good question. Sela doesn't do this with many people — she listens and observes and rarely invites reciprocal conversation. She is doing it with Ren. This is, Corvin notes without naming it, the fourth specific thing he has registered about her. He stops counting. He watches the treeline.

4. **The decision about the knowledge.** The conversation happens over Day 9's evening camp. Five people, a fire, the east road's open scrubland around them: What do they tell Maret? What do they tell anyone? The outcome: they tell Maret her brother is alive and returning. They tell her the waystation was not empty and the situation has been addressed. The detailed documentation — Ren's maps and notes — goes in a locked copy: Ren keeps one, Sela keeps one. They will not report to the Church or the crown. Not yet. Not until they understand who to trust with this information and what the consequences of trust might be. This is a decision made by five adults with incomplete information in a field, which is how most decisions this size actually get made. Corvin does not like it. He agrees with it. He says so.

5. **Evren, quietly.** On Day 10, Evren is quieter than usual. He keeps up with the group and makes jokes at the usual frequency but the frequency is slightly lower, which Corvin can calibrate exactly because he has been paying attention. Evren walks near Agda more often than he walks near anyone else. Agda does not comment on this. Corvin doesn't either. He watches Evren's face when the last of the Hollowback trees are behind them and the east road opens back up into scrubland: something releases in Evren's posture. He looks younger for about thirty seconds. Then he makes a joke about the smell of farmland and sounds like himself again.

6. **Corvin and Sela, evening of Day 10.** They are on watch together — his rotation and hers ended up adjacent. The road is clear. The fire is low. For twenty minutes they sit without speaking, which is not uncomfortable. Then Sela says: *You were between it and everyone else.* She does not say *the Hollowed One.* He says: *Yes.* She says: *You knew you couldn't stop it by force.* He says: *Yes.* She says: *So why?* He says: *Because it was the thing to do.* She is quiet for a moment. Then she says: *I know why you were discharged.* Pause. *I don't know the details, but I know the shape of it.* He looks at her. She says: *You refused an order you knew was wrong, and it cost you. And you did the same thing in the chamber.* He says nothing for a long time. Then he says: *You should get some sleep.* She says: *Yes.* Neither of them moves for a while.

7. **The near-moment.** The watch ends. She stands to go. He says — he has not planned to say this, it is not his register to say it, but he says: *Sela.* She stops. He says: *For what it's worth.* And then he stops, because he has no architecture for what comes after "for what it's worth." She waits. He says: *You paid a real cost in there.* She says: *It healed.* He says: *Yeah.* She stands there for one more second, and then she goes to sleep. He watches the dark road and thinks: *yes.* That is the entire interior. Then he watches the road.

---

## Outcome / Turn
The party is returning to Thorngate, intact, fond of each other in the way people become fond of other people when they have been through something together. The decision about the knowledge is made and uncomfortable. The near-moment between Corvin and Sela happened and did not resolve and they both know it and will not discuss it. Ren is going home. The book's last chapter awaits.

## New Question / Pressure
What does coming back to Thorngate mean? The contract ends when Ren walks into Maret's office. What are they after that?

## Continuity Notes
- Corvin names the shape of his discharge to Sela (indirectly): she says *you refused an order you knew was wrong.* He doesn't confirm it explicitly — he says nothing — but his saying nothing is a confirmation. This is as close to the full story as anyone gets in this book.
- The decision about the knowledge: five-person agreement, documented in Ren's maps (two copies). Not reported to the Church or the crown. The kingdom does not know at the end of this book, per the brief's requirement.
- Ren and Sela on the road: she invites reciprocal conversation, which she doesn't do with many people. Corvin observes this. The Sela/Ren thread continues.
- Evren's release when the Hollowback is behind him: thirty seconds of looking young. This is the quiet resolution of the hidden reason he never voiced — he came for Lira's story, and he found an answer that isn't complete but is more than he had. He does not say this. The posture says it.
- Grenn Vott waves them through and says *still here.* This is a coda on the charter scene; the charter holder recognized something in Agda at the start and recognizes its continuation.

## Romance Threads in This Chapter
- **Sela/Corvin:** Third and most significant beat. The near-moment during watch. She names what he did; he can't quite say what he means; neither of them resolves it. This is the Sela/Corvin thread at its peak before the final chapter. It does not complete.
- **Sela/Ren:** Ongoing texture — they walk together; she invites his conversation. Seen from Corvin's POV, so rendered through what he observes. Third notable scene.
- **Evren/Agda:** He walks near her more often. She doesn't comment. The thing between them is now mutual and neither of them will name it yet.
