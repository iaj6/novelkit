# Chapter 14 — Threshold

**One-line summary:** The party escapes the waystation; Duhr's territory, Agda's work, Evren standing too quietly outside.

**POV character:** Agda Stonebrook
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** The waystation interior (ascending), exterior, and the Hollowback just outside. Day 7, mid-morning into afternoon.
**Target words:** ~3,500

---

## Chapter Shape
**Action-heavy.** The escape from the waystation is physically demanding — Sela is incapacitated, Agda is exhausted from the invocation, and the party has to move through the antechamber (now going in reverse) with a non-functional team member. The chapter's action is the logistics of getting everyone out, not combat. The real content is in the aftermath: the party sitting in the forest outside the waystation, and Agda's processing of what just happened, and Evren standing three meters away from the group not quite all right.

## Ending Mode
**Resolved.** They are out. Everyone is alive. The containment is sealed. The chapter closes on the party outside in the forest light, depleted, intact, and in possession of something they'll have to decide what to do about. The resolution here is earned — the danger is over; the question of what comes next is allowed to wait for the next chapter.

---

## Goal
Get everyone out of the waystation. Keep Sela stable. Do not lose anyone to the antechamber traps going backward.

## Obstacle
Sela is down — she can be walked if supported, but she cannot keep her cantrip active and she cannot react fast enough to watch her own footwork on the pressure plates. The antechamber traps do not care about their circumstances. Ren knows the route but has not reversed it under these conditions. Agda is exhausted but she is also the one who heals, and she does not have the luxury of being exhausted yet.

## Action Beats

1. **Moving up from the containment chamber.** Agda inventories everyone in the first ten seconds: Sela — down, breathing, nosebleed stopped, needs rest; Corvin — carrying Sela, moving, not discussing it; Evren — moving, has taken a draw-effect, moving somewhat carefully, not admitting this; Ren — intact physically, carrying the documentation; herself — exhausted to the marrow, both hands and both knees working, keeping up. She invokes Duhr in the corridor: a small, practical invocation for clarity of mind when the body is depleted. The answer comes back: *continue.* Duhr is not generous with encouragement but this is the most it usually gets.

2. **The antechamber, reversed.** The pressure plate field going back: Ren leads, calling the path (reversed). Evren helps Corvin with Sela over the field — not carrying her, steering. The path is less legible reversed; Evren re-reads Ren's marks on the wall and corrects Ren twice. Agda goes last again. The ceiling block above the inner passage shifts as it did on entry. She walks through it the same way she walked into it: briskly, without running, because running is for emergencies and this is the aftermath of an emergency, which is different.

3. **Out.** The stairwell. The entry. Daylight — the particular flat overcast light of the Hollowback, grey and close and cooler than the waystation's preserved air. Agda steps out last and stands in the entry for a moment, breathing. She says the word Duhr uses for the space between an ending and what comes next. It is not a translation; there is no Common equivalent. But Duhr answers, briefly, with the feeling of a door closing properly: the threshold settled. This is Duhr's sign that something is less wrong than it was. It is not the same as everything being right.

4. **The party at rest.** They make camp fifteen meters from the waystation's exterior, in a clear area where Agda can see the entry. Corvin lays Sela down on her pack. She is awake — conscious, actually — and her first coherent statement is to ask if the sealing worked. Ren says yes. She says: *how long will it hold?* Ren says: *Years. A lot of them, I think.* She closes her eyes. Agda performs a proper healing pass over her — practical, Duhr-assisted, reducing the inflammation, clearing the post-cast fatigue faster than it would clear on its own. Sela's color improves. Agda tells her to eat something and sleep.

5. **Ren's documentation.** Ren is already re-examining his maps and making notes. He is nineteen and the adrenaline has not quite cleared his system and writing helps him process. Agda watches him for a moment: a young man who came here alone to deal with something too large for one person, who made the right decisions under conditions that would have made a lot of people make wrong ones. She will not say this to him. He would find it embarrassing. But she thinks it.

6. **Evren.** He is standing about three meters from the group, near the Hollowback tree line, not quite looking at the waystation and not quite looking at the forest. He has his arms crossed, which is not his usual posture. Agda goes over to him. She doesn't ask what he's thinking. She stands next to him. After a while he says: *You know what you said. That it's not dead and not alive and it's a wound that hasn't healed.* She says: *Yes.* He says: *Do you think she knew what she was walking into?* She does not know who he means and she does not ask. She says: *I don't know. But someone knew, once. And they wrote it down.* He nods. She stays next to him until he uncrosses his arms. Then she goes back to check Sela's color.

7. **The decision not made yet.** Ren, Corvin, and Sela (who is sitting up now, eating the dried fruit Ren has in his pack) have a brief exchange about what comes next. They will leave tomorrow. They will go back through the Hollowback, past the charter gate, and to Thorngate. What they tell Maret, what they tell anyone else — that is tomorrow's problem. Tonight: rest. Ren asks if they're satisfied with the documentation as he's assembled it. Corvin says: *For now.* Agda says nothing. She is checking the bandage on her hand from the invocation — she does not remember cutting it; Duhr-work sometimes leaves physical marks.

---

## Outcome / Turn
Everyone is out. The waystation is behind them. The sealing is done. They will leave tomorrow. The chapter ends on the party in the forest, depleted and intact, with the decision about the knowledge deferred to the road.

## New Question / Pressure
Evren asked about "her" — his unnamed person — and Agda did not push. She stood next to him. What does it mean to stand next to someone when they are carrying something they won't name? (This is not a narrative question requiring answer — it is the thing the chapter leaves in the reader.)

## Continuity Notes
- The antechamber reversed: Evren corrects Ren on the path twice. This is not a dramatic moment; it is competence, which is its own kind of character.
- Agda's hand: a physical mark from the invocation. This is consistent with how Duhr-work is rendered — it is real, it has cost, it is not magical power that arrives without price.
- Duhr's closing-door response: not a victory fanfare. A door closing properly. This is Duhr's register — thresholds settling, not celebrations.
- Ren's documentation: all his notes, maps, and the Vethic records he's copied. He has a complete account. The party does not yet know what they will do with it.
- Sela's magic cost: she is recovering, not recovered. She should not cast again today. She doesn't.

## Romance Threads in This Chapter
- **Evren/Agda:** The major beat of this thread. She stands next to him. He asks about "her." She answers with what little she has. She stays until he uncrosses his arms. She goes back to do her job. This is the Evren/Agda thread at its most real — not banter, not performance, just presence. The third notable scene of this pairing.
