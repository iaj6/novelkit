# Chapter 01 — The Worst Job on the Board

**One-line summary:** Four strangers take the only job nobody else will touch.

**POV character:** Sela Vane
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** Thorngate, trade district — the job board, the street, Maret Ashford's warehouse office. Morning through early afternoon.
**Target words:** ~3,000

---

## Chapter Shape
**Multi-scene.** Three locations, continuous day. Scene 1: the job board. Scene 2: the street negotiation. Scene 3: Maret's office. Pacing is brisk and observational — Sela's cataloging interiority establishes each new character through what she notices, not through exposition.

## Ending Mode
**Unresolved.** The contract is signed. The job already worries everyone who knows enough to be worried. The party is not a party yet; they are four people who happened to say yes to the same piece of paper.

---

## Goal
Sela needs paid work. Her master is in debt and she took the apprentice fee from a ledger she shouldn't have read. This job — badly paid as it is — solves the immediate problem.

## Obstacle
The contract has sat on the board for six days. Everyone in the trade district already knows what it means. Sela doesn't, fully, until she starts asking.

## Action Beats

1. **Job board.** Sela is scanning the board in the trade district's hiring hall — a narrow room smelling of chalk dust and old oilcloth. The Ashford contract is the only one without competing handwriting in the margin (no one has tried to underbid). She reads it carefully. Missing person, east road, *Hollowback* — a word she has to parse. She takes the paper.

2. **Bumping into Evren.** Outside the hiring hall, she collides with Evren Cael coming out of a side alley. He has also taken a copy of the Ashford contract. (He somehow obtained it before it was posted, which she clocks and does not mention.) The banter starts here: Evren proposes they split the fee if they work together; Sela counters that splitting an already-inadequate fee does not solve the problem of how inadequate the fee is. Evren concedes the logic but still tags along. His energy is relentless. She finds him funny, which annoys her.

3. **Corvin is already there.** When they reach the address on the contract, Corvin Ash is already standing at the door — earlier copy, longer stride. He has read the whole thing and has one question: is the job to find the brother alive or just to find him. Sela considers this the most important question anyone has asked today. She likes him immediately, which she will not say aloud and files away as a separate problem.

4. **Agda appears.** Agda Stonebrook is sitting on a crate beside the warehouse door eating an apple and reading what appears to be the same contract. She looked at the Hollowback clause first, then the pay rate, then took the job anyway. She says three words of explanation when asked: "Someone should go." The others absorb this. Nobody argues.

5. **Meeting Maret Ashford.** The four of them crowd into Maret's warehouse office — turpentine smell, stacked inventory ledgers, a map of the east road with a hand-drawn circle around the Hollowback. Maret is mid-thirties, visibly frightened, trying not to show it, and slightly embarrassed by them (they are not quite what she was hoping for). She explains: Ren, nineteen, cartographer's apprentice, went east three weeks ago. Left a note saying he'd be back. She gives them what she has — Ren's note, a description, a partial map. She does not know about the waystation specifically; she only knows the Hollowback is where people don't go. Sela watches the frightened sister more than she watches the map.

6. **The fee negotiation.** Evren attempts to negotiate the fee upward, deploying a confidence Sela finds alarming in its brazenness. Maret cannot afford more. Corvin cuts off Evren's second attempt with a look. Agda does not engage and has already pocketed her contract copy. They sign. The fee is bad. The job is real.

7. **Walking out.** The four of them on the street, contract in Sela's hand. First real exchange as a group: Corvin says they should leave by first light, light packs, three days' food. Evren says he can "procure" additional supplies this afternoon and not to ask questions. Agda says she needs to restock her kit at the apothecary and will meet them at the east gate. They disperse. Sela stands alone for a moment with the contract and the feeling that she has agreed to something she does not fully understand. She notes the angle of afternoon light. She goes to pack.

---

## Outcome / Turn
Contract signed. Party assembled. They're leaving for the Hollowback at dawn. No one has the full picture yet of what's inside the waystation, but Evren knows more than he said, and Sela has noticed that he does.

## New Question / Pressure
Why hasn't anyone else taken this job, really? Sela asked two people at the hiring hall and got answers that were technically answers and not actually answers.

---

## Continuity Notes
- Establishes Thorngate, the hiring hall, the trade district texture.
- Establishes Maret Ashford's fear and the nature of the contract: find Ren, bring him back.
- First introduction of all four party members; no backstory is dumped — each is rendered through action and dialogue.
- Sela's magic: she does not cast in this chapter. Her capability comes up only obliquely (she has the Church's provisional apprentice certification, which Maret notes with visible uncertainty — it is not the seal Maret hoped to see on a hire like this).
- Evren already has a copy of the contract before it was properly posted. This is a plant. No one comments on it except Sela internally.
- Agda has no Church seal on her healer's kit; Sela clocks this and files it.
- Corvin is wearing a coat that covers his old army insignia; Sela notices the shape of the fabric at the collar and the deliberateness of the covering. Files it.

## Romance Threads in This Chapter
- **Sela/Corvin:** First encounter. She finds his competence immediately arresting and doesn't understand why. She files it as a "separate problem." Nothing spoken.
- No other threads introduced yet — too early.
