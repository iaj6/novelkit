# Chapter 09 — Interior

**One-line summary:** The waystation's lower level: rooms, maps, Ren's notes, and something heard deeper in the dark.

**POV character:** Evren Cael
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** The Eastmark Waystation, lower level. Day 5, afternoon through dusk. Sela's light cantrip; the corridors are cold and dry and older than anyone alive.
**Target words:** ~3,000

---

## Chapter Shape
**Multi-scene.** Room-to-room exploration — each room is a beat. The chapter's function is dual: establish the waystation's interior world, and reveal Ren's presence through his traces before the party finds him. Evren's POV makes this chapter faster and more associative than Corvin's would be; his observations are sharper on the human evidence (what Ren left) than on the tactical geometry. His hidden reason presses through the interiority here — he has been in a room like this in his imagination for three years.

## Ending Mode
**Unresolved.** They find Ren's primary campsite and notes. They understand he is alive, he has been here for weeks, and he found something specific. The notes are alarming. They haven't found Ren yet. In the far corridor, the sound from Ch 8 has not gone away.

---

## Goal
Find Ren. Understand what he found. Get everyone out intact.

## Obstacle
The waystation is larger than the exterior suggested — the lower level runs back further than the original entry footprint, following the old Imperial road junction underground. Some rooms are collapsed; some are intact and full of three-century-old debris. Ren's trail is readable but not obvious. And the farther they go, the more Evren has to manage the fact that he is standing in the place he's been trying to get to for three years, and some of what he finds here is confirming a theory he had and badly wanted to be wrong about.

## Action Beats

1. **The garrison rooms.** First corridor opens into two parallel rooms: former barracks. Bunks corroded to their bolt-lines, a few items preserved by the dryness — a buckle, a boot heel, something that might have been a canteen. Evren's read of the rooms: this was a real garrison, probably a dozen soldiers at capacity. He has been in old barracks before (he won't say when or where). There is nothing threatening here. There is also no sign of how the garrison ended — no bodies, no scorching, no indication of violent departure. They just stopped being here.

2. **The storage rooms.** A series of vaulted storage chambers. Most empty; three contain Imperial supply goods in varying states of preservation — salt (still good; the Empire sealed their salt well), lamp oil (not good; rancid), a locked chest that Evren opens without asking for permission. Inside: Vethic survey equipment. Good quality instruments, probably still functional. He holds one up — a sighting tool — and recognizes the design from Lira's description. He sets it down carefully. He does not comment. Sela watches him set it down.

3. **Evren's interior.** Moving deeper, the rooms becoming less garrison and more something-else — lower ceilings, different stonework, a section that smells faintly different (not rot; more like ozone, like the air before a storm). He tracks his own reaction and files it: the smell is wrong and has been wrong since the second corridor turn. The garrison ended. Something else in here did not end. He has been telling himself for three years that Lira might have gotten out some other way, or that whatever took her wasn't in a waystation like this one. He is in the waystation. The smell is wrong. He stops in front of a closed door and stands there for five seconds before Corvin comes up behind him and says, quietly: *Problem?* He says no and opens the door.

4. **Ren's workspace.** A former officer's room, slightly larger than the barracks cells — and full of Ren. A bedroll in decent shape. A camp lantern. Maps. Not just Maret's maps or even Aldenmere's maps: hand-drawn sheets, some on paper and some on the back of salvaged Vethic vellum that has survived three centuries with nothing wrong with it. The maps are detailed, annotated, cross-referenced to each other by a system of symbols. Corvin goes through the door first, which means he sees the maps first, and his expression does a specific thing that Evren reads as *this is more than we expected.* Agda looks at the bedroll. She names what's obvious: he ate well, he slept here, he has been here for weeks, not in distress. Evren stands at the table of maps.

5. **Reading the maps.** He can read the Vethic notation on the hand-drawn maps — fluently. He has done this twice now in front of the party and it is too late to pretend otherwise. Sela is definitely watching him not-pretend-otherwise. The maps show: the waystation's full layout, including rooms below this level that aren't on any surface survey. A containment chamber, three levels down. And next to the containment chamber, in Ren's own handwriting: a notation in Common that reads, simply, *it is still here.* Evren reads this aloud. Then he re-reads the Vethic notation on the older map next to it. Then he puts the map down. He says: *We should find him now.*

6. **The sound.** As they prepare to move deeper, the sound from the bottom of the stairwell — the regular, low sound that has been there since they entered — is closer. Not louder. Closer. As though something has moved, without sound, from wherever it was to nearer where they are. The hallway ahead is dark past Sela's light range. Corvin is ahead of everyone else, stopped, looking at the dark. He says nothing. The sound continues. Regular. Low. Not breathing — too slow for breathing.

---

## Outcome / Turn
Ren's workspace found. He is alive, he is not in distress, and he has been here documenting something in the containment chamber three levels below. His notes say *it is still here.* The sound from the lower levels is something that has moved toward them without them hearing it move.

## New Question / Pressure
What is "it"? Ren's maps show the containment chamber. The something that's been sounding from the lower levels just moved.

---

## Continuity Notes
- Evren reads the maps fluently: this is the third time he's done this in front of others. It is no longer ambiguous — the party knows he can read Vethic notation. The explanation he gave ("old cartographer's trick, you pick it up") was not challenged but was not believed.
- The ozone smell: this is the earliest environmental sign of the Hollowed One's presence. It is not explained here — just noted as wrong.
- The garrison rooms: no bodies, no violence. The Imperial garrison left, or was called back, or dispersed — long before whatever is currently in the containment chamber became a problem. The waystation was maintained and then abandoned, separately from whatever the containment chamber holds.
- Ren's notation "it is still here" is in Common, not Vethic — he wrote it for himself as a reminder. This is characterization: he writes reminders to himself in his own language, which means he processes things by writing them down.
- Corvin's tactical read of the garrison rooms: he notes that the garrison left in order, not under duress. This is important for the eventual understanding of what the Hollowed One is: the garrison was withdrawn when the Empire fell, not driven out by it. The containment was holding; it is only now, three centuries later, degrading.

## Romance Threads in This Chapter
- **Evren/Agda:** Minor beat. She looks at him when he stands at the table of maps and reads them without hesitation. She doesn't say anything. The look is the thing.
