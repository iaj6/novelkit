# Chapter 06 — What the Empire Paved

**One-line summary:** The party finds the old Imperial road in the forest floor and stops breathing for a moment.

**POV character:** Sela Vane
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** Deep in the Hollowback, Day 4 afternoon. Old-growth forest, the light changing quality. The track they're following is becoming something that looks less like a road.
**Target words:** ~3,000

---

## Chapter Shape
**Interiority-heavy.** This is a breathing chapter between the charter fight and the waystation. The encounter is not combat or confrontation; it is the forest doing what the Hollowback does: showing its age, making distance hard to judge, and then producing the moment of awe. Sela's cataloging interiority is the right instrument here — she notices things precisely, and the thing she notices is worth noticing precisely.

## Ending Mode
**Unresolved.** The awe landed. They camped. Something sounds wrong in the night. Not an immediate threat — just the forest saying: *you are not the first things to have been here, and we are not done yet.*

---

## Goal
Move deeper into the Hollowback. Stay oriented. Don't let the forest pull the party apart — literally and otherwise.

## Obstacle
The Hollowback disorients. The quality of the light makes distances hard to judge. The old road has been swallowed. The track they're following doubles back twice, which is its own kind of comment on navigation. And something in the afternoon forest feels like being watched in a way that cannot be attributed to specific fauna.

## Action Beats

1. **The light goes strange.** Two hours past the charter gate, the canopy changes: older trees, closer together, the light arriving in angled shafts that make the undergrowth look further away than it is. Sela watches Corvin adjust his pace — a slight shortening of stride, the way you walk when ground might be uneven. She does the same without being told. Evren is navigating by some instinct she cannot read (he says he once followed a cartographer's notes in a forest like this; he offers no other detail). Agda is watching the upper canopy.

2. **The road reasserts itself.** The track they're on dips slightly, and then — under the mud and the root-work and three centuries of leaf-fall — the track becomes something else: flat. Geometrically flat. In a way that forest floors are not. Sela stops. Under her boot, through the mud: stone. Not local limestone. Something harder. She scrapes with her heel and finds the edge of a paving stone, perfectly fitted to its neighbor, the join so close she can barely get her fingernail between them. The Vethic road. Still there. The forest grew over it, not through it.

3. **The moment of awe.** They follow the road's reassertion for about a quarter mile. The mud gives way to surface stone in places; in others the root-work has heaved the surface but the geometry holds underneath. Then — at the crest of a low rise where the old road must have commanded a sightline — the canopy opens slightly, and in the late-afternoon light coming through that gap, the moss on the Vethic paving stones is lit from below by something: not glow, exactly, not bioluminescence, but the particular quality of old stone releasing three centuries of stored heat into a cool forest air, making the air above it shimmer just slightly. Like the road is still warm. Like something that wide and that old takes a very long time to cool. Sela stands on it and her chest does something she will not describe aloud. The road was built by people who expected it to last. It did. They didn't.

4. **The party, briefly.** Nobody makes a joke. That is how she knows it matters. Evren is crouched, running his fingers over the join between two stones, and his face has the specific expression she has started to recognize as him being actually present rather than performing something. Agda stands at the crest of the rise and looks back down the road the way it must have run, into the dark of the old canopy. Corvin says, quietly: *Three hundred years.* And then nothing else, but the nothing else sounds like awe in Corvin's register, which is a fact she will keep.

5. **Pressing on.** They can't follow the Vethic road toward the waystation directly — it descends into a flooded section where the root-work has collapsed the old drainage. They go back to the track. The track rejoins the Vethic line later, shallower, less spectacular. The afternoon progresses. Sela keeps one eye on the map and one eye on the forest. Evren, beside her for a stretch, says: *The cartographer I mentioned — she said the road was still there.* She. He stops there. She notices the pronoun, notices him stopping, and does not ask. He doesn't volunteer more.

6. **Making camp; the night sounds.** Good campsite — flat ground, no standing water. Agda builds a small fire with the particular efficiency of someone who has camped alone before. Sela eats and re-reads Ren's notes (the fragments Maret gave her). She thinks about a nineteen-year-old who could read Vethic survey notation, walking this road alone, not afraid of the Hollowback — or not afraid enough to stop. She finds this interesting in a way that has nothing to do with the contract. She goes to sleep. At some point in the dark, she wakes to a sound: not animal. Not quite. It is the sound of something moving carefully, the way a person moves carefully, not the way a large animal blunders. Then it is gone. She tells herself it is an animal and almost believes it.

---

## Outcome / Turn
The awe moment has been earned — the party stopped, breathed, felt it together, and didn't perform it at each other. The road is closer now. Tomorrow they will find what's left of the waystation's exterior. Tonight, something moved in the forest with intentionality.

## New Question / Pressure
Evren said the cartographer was *she*. He stopped. Who was she? (This is not a question Sela asks; it's a question the reader now carries.) And what was moving through the camp's edges in the dark?

## Continuity Notes
- **THE MOMENT OF AWE** is here. The Vethic road, still flat and exact under three centuries of forest floor, moss-lit in late afternoon — geometrically impossible, quietly real. The party stops. Nobody is glib. This is the required moment from the brief; it is earned by what surrounds it (the exertion of the charter fight, the disorientation of the Hollowback, the approaching dread of the waystation).
- Sela does not cast in this chapter. She is conserving. Her headache from Ch 4 is entirely gone.
- The sound in the night is ambiguous. It could be something associated with the Hollowed One's influence at range; it could be the forest. The Drafter should leave it ambiguous.
- Evren says "the cartographer I mentioned — she said the road was still there." The "she" is the person who went into a ruin like this and didn't come back. He gives exactly one additional piece of information (female) and stops. This is his hidden reason cracking slightly, in public, just far enough that it landed.
