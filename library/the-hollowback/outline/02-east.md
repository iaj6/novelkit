# Chapter 02 — East

**One-line summary:** The party travels east and starts learning each other's shapes.

**POV character:** Evren Cael
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** The east road out of Thorngate, Day 1 and into Day 2. Moving from city fringe to farmland to the first reaches of wilder country.
**Target words:** ~2,500

---

## Chapter Shape
**Time-skip / montage.** Two days compressed into a series of vivid moments: road talk, a bad inn, second-morning impressions. The chapter's job is relationship texture and the quiet establishment of who these four people are when they're not in immediate danger. Fast pacing; Evren's associative interiority means the transitions are quick and funny. His observations of each party member are sharp, slightly unfair, and mostly accurate.

## Ending Mode
**Unresolved.** They're still on the road. The landscape is changing — farmland thinning, the quality of the light getting older. Something ahead. Evren knows more about where they're going than he's said, and by the end of the chapter he has a moment alone in which the joke stops and the real reason surfaces briefly in his interior — and then he shuts it down.

---

## Goal
Cover ground. Start to read these three people Evren has signed on with.

## Obstacle
Corvin does not answer questions about himself. Agda doesn't laugh. Sela sees more than she says. Evren has three reliable social tactics and none of them are quite working.

## Action Beats

1. **First mile out of Thorngate.** Evren's opening inventory of the group as they walk: Corvin moves like someone who is automatically tracking the road ahead and both tree lines. Agda has a pack that rattles with small equipment and should be heavier than she makes it look. Sela is reading the map Maret gave them and has already annotated it in the margin. Evren is being charming. Nobody is responding to being charmed. He commits anyway.

2. **Road banter, first morning.** The east road is well-traveled for the first four hours — grain carts, a few other parties, a woman on a mule with chickens who gives them all very specific directions. Evren and Sela fall into an exchange about whether the waystation is marked on any current map; Sela's copy has only a vague annotation. Evren says he once heard a cartographer's description of it. (He stops there. He did not hear this from just any cartographer.) He does not explain. Sela notices he stopped. She says nothing.

3. **Agda doesn't laugh.** First Evren/Agda beat. He tries three jokes over the course of the morning. The third one would have worked on almost anyone. Agda carries it with the same flat expression she carries everything. He finds this fascinating. Not discouraging — fascinating. He adjusts his strategy: if the jokes don't work, he'll try truth-adjacent material and see what happens. He does not actually try truth-adjacent material. He tries a fourth joke.

4. **Bad inn, end of Day 1.** A village inn twelve miles out — one room with four pallets, a fire, and a soup that Agda eats anyway because she can tell it won't kill them. Evening: Corvin sharpens his knife, which is its own kind of conversation. Agda patches a blister on her heel and invokes a quiet three-word phrase over the cloth wrap that isn't quite prayer and isn't quite instruction — Evren has heard that register before. He files it. Sela asks about the road conditions tomorrow and Corvin answers in eight words. The fire is good.

5. **Night, Evren's watch.** He sits at the door while the others sleep. The road is quiet. This is the moment the joke stops: Evren thinks about the person who went into the ruin before — briefly, specifically, with the particular grief of someone who has rehearsed the thought into near-blankness. He'd found a record of the Eastmark Waystation in old survey notes three years ago and had been looking for an excuse to go ever since. Here is the excuse. He looks at Sela's annotated map on the table and then back at the road. He shuts the thought down. By morning he is already planning his next attempt to make Agda laugh.

6. **Second morning.** Day 2: the farmland ends, the road narrows, the trees begin to feel older. Evren notes the moment the quality of the light changes — the canopy is thinning but the light through it is somehow less legible, distances harder to call. Sela is also watching. She hasn't said anything. Corvin has already adjusted his pace and sightline. Agda glances up at the trees and nods slightly, which is possibly agreement with the forest and possibly the most unsettling thing Evren has seen today.

7. **Closing miles.** They make camp as the road becomes genuinely difficult for wagons — the first sign they're approaching the Hollowback proper. Sela reads the distance on the map. Two more days to the waystation, maybe three. Evren is quiet for almost ten minutes, which is long enough for all three of the others to notice, though only Agda glances over to check. He makes a joke about the length of the road. Agda does not laugh. But she looked up, which is something.

---

## Outcome / Turn
Two days of travel. The party has begun to cohere: functional, funny, not yet trusting. The Hollowback is beginning to show its character in the landscape. Evren is the only one who knows what they might actually find inside the waystation, and the weight of that is visible in his night-watch interior even if he refuses to show it by day.

## New Question / Pressure
Evren said he heard a "cartographer's description" of the waystation and didn't explain the source. Sela noticed. The question — whose description? and why won't he say? — sits unanswered as the chapter closes.

---

## Continuity Notes
- The road east is a real road with real travelers for the first day; the second day it thins. This texture matters for the isolation to come.
- Agda's invocation over the cloth wrap is a micro-instance of Duhr-practice: brief, practical, not performed. First rendering of how her religion looks from outside.
- Evren's internal moment at night (thinking of the person who went into the ruin) is the first crack in his performed surface. It is not named or explained in interior monologue; it appears as a specific memory and a specific act of shutting that memory down. The reader gets information; the character does not deliver a backstory monologue.
- Corvin's behavioral portrait: he tracks sightlines, adjusts pace before others notice why, says less than anyone and means more.
- Sela annotates maps in margins. This is character.

## Romance Threads in This Chapter
- **Evren/Agda:** She doesn't laugh. He finds this fascinating. Three attempts at wit; she carries them flat. He starts adjusting. Very early stage — not romantic in its surface, but the specific attention is already there.
