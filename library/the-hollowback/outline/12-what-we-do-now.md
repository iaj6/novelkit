# Chapter 12 — What We Do Now

**One-line summary:** The argument about the knowledge — Ren vs. the party vs. the contract vs. the right thing.

**POV character:** Sela Vane
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** Ren's workspace in the waystation's lower level. Day 6, morning. Light comes from Sela's cantrip; no daylight reaches here.
**Target words:** ~4,000

---

## Chapter Shape
**Bottle.** One room, near real-time. The chapter is almost entirely character: five people arguing about what to do with information they would have been more comfortable not having. No new physical threats; the threat is the decision itself. This is the required action set piece #4 (confrontation resolved through character rather than combat). The resolution is not a fight; it is five people reaching a working agreement through the friction of their actual values.

## Ending Mode
**Turn.** The argument produces a plan: they will attempt to reseal the containment mechanisms before they leave, using Ren's documentation of the original design and the skills the party has between them. Ren agrees to come home after. The contract is technically fulfilled. The plan is not guaranteed to work, but it is the plan. Something shifts.

---

## Goal
Reach a decision about what to do with what they know about the Hollowed One, the degrading containment, and the waystation.

## Obstacle
Everyone has a different position:
- **Ren:** Document everything first. Report to someone qualified to make a decision. Do not attempt to interfere without understanding the mechanism fully.
- **Corvin:** The mission is to bring Ren home. Report what was found to someone with authority. Leave the waystation. This is above the party's pay grade.
- **Evren:** (does not state a clear position for most of the argument; is processing privately) Does not want to leave it unresolved. Does not say why.
- **Agda:** The containment is failing. Duhr's read on it is clear: this wound needs to close. Leaving it for someone with authority means the filters fail while the chain of authority debates it.
- **Sela:** The party has the skills to attempt a re-seal. They have Ren's documentation, Evren's mechanical knowledge, the unraveling. But she is also aware she is not fully objective about this, because a part of her wants to try it for reasons she won't examine.

## Action Beats

1. **The morning argument opens.** Corvin states his position first, which is its own social choice — he is the combat lead and the oldest and the one who has been in situations where someone's job was to follow orders, and he frames the situation as: this is not their decision to make. Ren argues immediately. Sela watches Corvin and Ren talk at each other for four minutes and notes that they are both right in incompatible ways.

2. **Ren's real position.** Under the argument about authority and documentation, Ren's actual position surfaces: he doesn't want to leave it to someone with authority because someone with authority will probably use it wrong. The Hollowed One contains the proof that vital energy transfer is possible. That proof, in the hands of the wrong people, is a weapon. He is not being dramatic; he is being a nineteen-year-old who has spent three weeks alone with this thought and has worked through its implications. Sela thinks: he is not wrong. He is also not in a position to make this decision alone, and he knows it, which is why he is still arguing.

3. **Agda's intervention.** She says, in two sentences, what the chapter has been building toward: *The containment fails in weeks. There is no time for a chain of authority.* She does not editorialize. She says it the way she says everything: flatly, with her hands doing something practical (she's repacking her kit). The room gets quiet.

4. **Evren breaks.** He has been quiet for most of the argument — which is so abnormal that everyone has been peripherally aware of it. He says: *There was a cartographer who went into a station like this one, three years ago, and didn't come back.* He says it the way you say a fact you have been carrying for a long time in an enclosed space and can no longer keep in your pocket. He does not say Lira's name. He says: *I want to know what happened to her.* He says: *And I don't think sealing it again answers that question.* He looks at Ren. *Is there documentation of what happened to the original subject?* Ren says: *Yes. A volunteer. It was consensual, within the terms they were told. The terms didn't include what actually happened.* Evren nods once and stops talking. The room absorbs this. Sela files it under: *the real reason. He finally said it.*

5. **The turn toward a plan.** Sela, who has been running the structural problem of the mechanism in parallel to the argument: asks Ren to walk her through the original sealing documentation. The containment has a designed state — the filters at full integrity, the mechanisms engaged. The station's designers built it to be resettable; they anticipated degradation and maintenance. The question is whether the materials are still here to do it. Ren has an answer: mostly yes. The mechanism runs on the station's own Vethic stonework, which is still integral. It requires a specific sequence of mechanical resets — Evren's domain — and one step that requires dissolving a corroded lock component that has partially fused with the mechanism. That is Sela's unraveling. The cost: she will be casting under pressure, on a target that is not a standard mechanical lock, in proximity to something that wants to consume vital energy. The cost is unknown and possibly significant.

6. **Sela's decision.** She says yes before she has fully thought through the cost calculation, and then she thinks through the cost calculation, and she says yes again. Corvin, quietly: *You know what you're agreeing to.* She says: *Yes.* He says: *All right.* That is Corvin's version of *I'll be there.* She notes this in the catalog under *significant* and does not make it complicated out loud.

7. **Ren agrees.** After the plan is established, Ren agrees to come home with them after — not before. The task is first. He says this with the particular stubbornness of someone who has been right about everything he committed to so far and intends to stay right. Sela points out that the contract has him going home, not going home *after finishing a complicated and possibly dangerous mechanical restoration project.* He says: *The contract is a piece of paper. This is real.* She thinks: that is, unfortunately, the right call, and he's correct about it.

8. **Preparation.** The afternoon is spent on logistics: Ren goes over the mechanism with Evren in detail. Agda prepares what she can — her kit, a pre-cast of Duhr's minor threshold-strengthening invocation that she performs quietly in a corner, arguing with her god in the specific register of someone who expects to be heard and intends to be heard. Sela reads the documentation Ren has assembled, organizing it into a sequence she can act on. Corvin goes through the route from here to the containment chamber and from the containment chamber to the exit and does not say aloud that he is planning the retreat path. He doesn't need to say it aloud.

---

## Outcome / Turn
The plan is made. They will attempt to reseal the containment tomorrow. Ren will come home after. The party has reached this agreement not because anyone's position was defeated, but because the actual constraints of the situation — Agda's timeline, the available skills, the decision Sela made — forced the shape of it. Nobody got exactly what they wanted. The plan is the right shape.

## New Question / Pressure
Sela is going to have to cast the unraveling in proximity to the Hollowed One, which wants vital energy, which is the substance magic is made of. Will the cost be manageable, or will it compound?

## Continuity Notes
- **Evren discloses his real reason, partially:** He says there was a cartographer who went into a station like this and didn't come back. He does not say Lira's name. He asks if there's documentation of the original subject. He does not say *she was mine to lose.* But the party now knows there is a specific person. This is as far as the disclosure goes in the book.
- Ren's position on vital energy transfer as a weapon: this is why he's been keeping it quiet. Not because he's paranoid — because he's right. The information is dangerous and he knows it.
- Sela agrees to cast the unraveling in a high-cost situation. Corvin's response (*all right*) is his version of *I will be there.* Sela knows this.
- Agda's Duhr invocation: she argues with her god the way she argues with family. The invocation here is a strengthening — threshold reinforcement — that will be part of the sealing attempt. It's not magical power in the action-system sense; it is a genuine practitioner-deity collaboration.
- The sealing mechanism requires: mechanical resets (Evren), the unraveling (Sela), and Agda's threshold invocation as an additional stabilizer. Ren provides knowledge. Corvin provides security. This is a party solution: no chosen one; everyone contributes their specific skill.

## Romance Threads in This Chapter
- **Sela/Ren:** Second major beat. She asks him to walk her through the documentation; he does, with precision, and they end up across the table from each other working through the mechanism together. It is entirely practical. It is not entirely practical. She makes the decision to try the unraveling before the argument is formally resolved, and she doesn't examine why.
- **Sela/Corvin:** He says *you know what you're agreeing to.* She says yes. He says *all right.* She catalogs it. This is the closest they have come to saying something real. Neither of them has moved.
