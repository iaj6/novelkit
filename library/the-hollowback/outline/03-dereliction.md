# Chapter 03 — Dereliction

**One-line summary:** Deserters ambush the party on the east road; Corvin's past gets named out loud.

**POV character:** Corvin Ash
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** The east road, late afternoon, Day 3. Dense scrubland on both sides; the track has narrowed to single file. The light is going.
**Target words:** ~4,000

---

## Chapter Shape
**Action-heavy.** The chapter opens in pre-ambush tension (Corvin has already noticed the signs), moves through the ambush itself, and closes on the aftermath. Corvin's tactical interiority is the register: short declarative sentences, physical inventory, decisions stated before feelings. The emotional weight comes after the fight, not during it.

## Ending Mode
**Turn.** The ambush is defeated but not cleanly. One deserter, before being released, says something about Corvin's past that the whole party hears. Corvin doesn't explain. The road ahead is quiet and that quiet is different from the earlier quiet.

---

## Goal
Get through the stretch of road without losing supplies, blood, or days of travel.

## Obstacle
Four deserters from Aldenmere's army, using the standard approach — send a limping man into the road, wait for the party to slow. One of them recognizes Corvin. That recognition is a weapon aimed at something the deserter thinks will unbalance him, and he is not entirely wrong.

## Action Beats

1. **Pre-ambush read.** Corvin notices: broken-down cart just visible through the scrub to the right, too recently placed. Footprints in the mud — four sets, two diverging off the road at intervals. The limping man ahead in the road is holding himself carefully, not the way an injury makes you hold yourself. Corvin says quietly: *stop walking.* Nobody argues. That was the right kind of voice to use.

2. **They try it anyway.** The limping man calls for help. One of the deserters moves out of cover on the right, crossbow visible. Two more emerge from the left — swords. Corvin has already handed Evren a specific instruction: the crossbowman first, not the swords, because a bolt doesn't require distance. He's watched Evren long enough in two days to know he can be quiet and fast. Evren goes. Sela has a hand up, holding her breath — he notices she's gauging whether to cast. He does not tell her to. Not yet.

3. **The fight.** Crossbowman neutralized (Evren, fast and dirty — not fatal, but the bow is broken and the man is on the ground). Two swords on Corvin. He is good at this and thinks about it in the vocabulary of geometry: angles of advance, the way the left-hand man telegraphs his swing in his shoulder before his arm moves, the decision tree that collapses in real time. He injures one man significantly and disarms the other without killing either. The limping man from the road has drawn a knife and is approaching Sela. Agda steps in front of her and hits him with her pack, which is heavier than it looks. He sits down hard.

4. **The fourth man.** A fourth deserter has been hanging back — older, scar along his jaw, watching. He hasn't moved. He's looking at Corvin. Corvin recognizes the posture before he recognizes the face, and then he recognizes the face. Former unit. He was infantry when Corvin was sergeant. His name is a fact that costs nothing to know.

5. **The recognition.** The fourth man says: *Ash. I heard you got derelicted.* A beat. *Heard the whole story, too.* He says this with a specific weight — not the kind that threatens blackmail, but the kind that says: I know what you did and I know what it cost you and I am watching to see what that does to you right now. The rest of the party hears this. Sela hears this. Evren hears this. Agda already has her hand inside her kit.

6. **Corvin's response.** He says nothing about the charge. He says: *The road's clear. Walk off it.* The fourth man looks at the rest of the party, looks at Corvin, weighs something. Then he gestures for his companions to get up and they go, heading back into the scrub. The crossbowman limps. The fourth man does not look back. Corvin watches the scrubline for sixty seconds after they disappear. Then he picks up his pack and starts walking.

7. **Aftermath, first two hundred meters.** The silence behind him has the specific texture of people who want to ask and are deciding whether to. Sela is breathing carefully — she had a hand up during the fight and he's fairly sure she was preparing to cast and did not, which means she made a judgment call about cost. He doesn't ask. Nobody asks him anything either. The late-afternoon light is going fast.

---

## Outcome / Turn
The party wins the ambush — no one is seriously hurt, the deserters are gone, their supplies are intact. But *dereliction* is now in the air between all four of them, named, uncontested, unexplained. Corvin's past has been announced in the worst possible way: by someone who made it sound like a verdict, to people Corvin has known for three days.

## New Question / Pressure
What did Corvin actually do to be discharged? The fourth man said he'd heard the whole story — which means there is a story, which means Corvin has been sitting on it this whole time. Nobody has pressed yet. They will want to, eventually.

## Stakes in This Fight
- Physical: injury or death would change the whole mission
- Informational: the deserters could have taken Maret's contract copy, learned where the party is going, sold that to someone with worse intentions than themselves
- Relational: Corvin's past is now a known unknown for the whole party, and the party is not yet stable enough to absorb this cleanly

- Sela's magic: she prepares to cast (force-push) but doesn't, because she makes a tactical judgment about cost vs. necessity. This is important — it shows her discipline. She does not cast in this chapter.
- Agda's pack is heavier than it looks; she hits someone with it. This is practical and slightly funny and reveals something about her.
- The deserter's face: not named at this stage in the outline; the Drafter may name him or not. He is a minor character whose job is to plant the discharge question. He is not a villain; he is someone who knows a fact.
- No one is killed in this chapter. The deserters are injured, disarmed, and released. This is the party's default calibration: stop the threat, don't escalate.
