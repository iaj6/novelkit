# Chapter 08 — The Antechamber

**One-line summary:** The puzzle-trapped antechamber — Evren and Sela crack it together; Sela pays for every spell.

**POV character:** Sela Vane
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** The Eastmark Waystation exterior and antechamber. Day 5, mid-morning to mid-afternoon. Overcast; the light in the forest is flat and grey.
**Target words:** ~4,500

---

## Chapter Shape
**Action-heavy.** This is the dungeon-puzzle set piece (required action set piece #2). The chapter divides into two sections: arrival at the waystation exterior (shorter, atmospheric) and the antechamber itself (the bulk of the chapter, focused, physical, real). Sela's interiority here is her at her most precise — she is the right POV for a puzzle because she thinks in structures, and the Vethic antechamber is a structure she has to learn and break in real time.

## Ending Mode
**Mid-action.** They get through the antechamber. Corvin is in the lead, stepping into the corridor beyond. Sela's head is pounding from two casts. In the dark ahead, something changes — a shift in air pressure, a sound too regular to be water dripping. They are inside, and whatever is further in knows that too.

---

## Goal
Get through the antechamber traps and into the waystation's lower level.

## Obstacle
The Vethic antechamber is a real puzzle: pressure plates, a mechanical combination lock with Vethic notation, and at least one trap that fires on magical signatures (it is calibrated to Vethic-style magic, but close enough to modern casting to be a problem). Ren has been through here — he left passage marks — but the passage marks are subtle and some of the traps reset after a period of non-activation.

## Action Beats

1. **Finding the waystation.** The exterior: what's left is mostly foundation and the collapsed upper walls. The lower level entrance is half-buried in a century of root-work and soil, accessed through what was once a stairwell down. The stonework here is visibly Vethic — the same hard pale stone as the road, the same tight joins. Three centuries of forest have not shifted it. There is a smell coming up from below: cool, dry, metallic. Not rot. Preserved.

2. **Ren's marks.** On the lintel, just inside the entry: a chalk mark in Vethic notation, Ren's style (they know this now from the tree cairn). It reads, roughly, *second step, left edge, safe.* Below: *turn left immediately.* Evren reads it aloud and then stops, having done so, and Sela adds the notation fluency to the list of things she knows about him that he hasn't explained. She focuses on the marks. The second step is obviously a pressure plate — she can see it, slightly higher than its neighbors. Left edge: the trigger zone is the middle third.

3. **The descent.** Sela holds her light cantrip — low cost, she can hold it for an hour. The stairwell is narrow: one at a time. Corvin goes first (wrong — she should have argued for Evren, who handles traps, but Corvin is already moving). She follows. The air below is cooler by ten degrees. The walls are intact Vethic stone. The light off them is strange — the stone absorbs it oddly, more than limestone does, so her cantrip makes the corridor look slightly underwater. She notes this. She notes everything.

4. **The first trap: pressure plate field.** The corridor opens into a rectangular chamber, maybe five meters by ten. Floor is a grid of pressure plates, most inert, several not — the active ones have the faintest discoloration at the joins, years of dust disturbed differently. Ren's marks on the left wall indicate a path: nine specific squares, in order. Evren reads the marks and calls the path out loud. Agda follows it exactly. Corvin follows it exactly. Sela follows it exactly. At square seven, she puts her foot down wrong by two inches. The plate shifts. Evren grabs her sleeve and yanks her forward to square eight. The trap fires — not a blade, a concussive percussion that hits the space she just left and knocks a chip from the wall behind them. Her ears ring. Everyone is through.

5. **The combination lock.** Beyond the pressure plate field: a door. Iron, set in the Vethic stone, with a mechanism on the front that is — not quite a lock. A combination of rotating rings, each marked with Vethic notation, that must be aligned in a specific sequence. The notation is mathematical: a series of astronomical positions, a Vethic date cycle. Ren has scratched the correct sequence onto the door frame itself, which means he solved this. Evren reads the scratch marks. He and Sela work through the sequence: she reads, he turns the rings, the mechanism advances through position after position.

6. **The magic-sensing trap.** Halfway through the sequence, Sela's light cantrip triggers something: a pressure in the air, a vibration in the floor — the chamber has a secondary trap keyed to active magical signatures. She has four seconds to drop the cantrip before it escalates. She drops it. Total darkness. She can hear Evren's hands on the rings by sound — he keeps going, by touch and by memory. She counts his movements in the dark. The trap dissipates. After the sequence is complete and the door clicks open, she re-casts the light — immediate cost, second cast of the day. The headache starts behind her right eye.

7. **The inner pressure trap.** The door opens onto a short passage. One more trap: a weight-sensitive ceiling section. Ren's marks say *move fast, do not stop.* Simple. They move fast and do not stop. Agda goes last and a section of ceiling groans behind her. A block shifts but does not fall. Agda does not run — she steps up her pace to a brisk walk, which is, Sela observes, a very specific kind of courage.

8. **Through.** The passage opens into the first of the lower-level corridors. Vethic vaulted ceilings, intact. The smell is dry and cold and very old. Sela's head is pounding. She has two casts left at reliable cost (sleep induction, the unraveling) and she has already spent one minor cantrip twice. She does not say this aloud. Corvin is looking at the corridor ahead with his tactical read going at full speed. In the dark ahead — far enough that it shouldn't be audible — something makes a sound. Regular. Low. Not water.

---

## Outcome / Turn
The party is inside the Eastmark Waystation. Sela has paid for the entry in headache and a depleted light cantrip. The antechamber is behind them. They have Ren's marks to follow. And there is something in the deeper dark that is not random.

## New Question / Pressure
The sound from deeper in the waystation — too regular to be environmental. Something is active in there.

## Stakes in This Chapter
- Physical: the traps were real. The ceiling block shifted. If Evren hadn't grabbed Sela's sleeve at plate seven, she would have been hit by a concussive charge.
- Magical: Sela's cost is tracked. Two light-cantrip casts. Reliable spells remaining: force-push (medium cost), sleep induction (high cost, unreliable under stress), the unraveling (headache either way). She is not depleted but she is not fresh.
- Informational: Ren was here and solved the puzzle. His marks are recent enough to be deliberate — he left them for people following him, which means he anticipated being followed, which means he made a choice about whether to let people in.

## Continuity Notes
- Sela's light cantrip was dropped mid-sequence and re-cast: two uses today. Headache is present.
- The force-push, sleep induction, and the unraveling are all still available.
- The magic-sensing trap calibrated to Vethic magic: this is a hint about the waystation's original design — the Empire built their stations to resist magic from outside a certain signature. Relevant to Ch 13 when Sela has to push past her reliable range.
- Ren's marks confirm he solved the puzzle himself and left guidance — this is characterization before we meet him. He is the kind of person who expects others to follow and leaves the door open.
- Evren reads Vethic notation in this chapter, openly (he reads Ren's scratch marks aloud). He has now done this twice. The party knows.
