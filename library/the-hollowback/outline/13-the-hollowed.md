# Chapter 13 — The Hollowed

**One-line summary:** The Hollowed One gets out; Corvin holds the line while the others work; Sela pushes past threshold.

**POV character:** Corvin Ash
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** The containment chamber, three levels below the waystation's entrance. Day 7, morning. Confined space, poor light, the smell of ozone and cold stone.
**Target words:** ~4,500

---

## Chapter Shape
**Action-heavy.** This is the required action set piece #3: combat against something the party should not be able to beat. Corvin's POV is the right instrument for this chapter — tactical, declarative, physical. The fight is a holding action while the others complete the sealing; Corvin's job is to not let the Hollowed One reach anyone who is occupied with the mechanism. He cannot kill it. He knows this. The question is whether he can keep it from killing anyone for long enough.

## Ending Mode
**Mid-action.** The containment is resealed. Everyone is alive. Corvin is spent. Sela is on the floor. They need to go now, immediately, because the air in this room is wrong and getting worse, and the sealing is new and untested and they should not be here to find out if it holds. The chapter ends not with triumph but with *out, now, move.*

---

## Goal
Complete the sealing mechanism while keeping the Hollowed One from reaching any member of the party.

## Obstacle
The Hollowed One's containment filter has deteriorated enough that it can now direct its draw intentionally — within the chamber, it can reach toward a living thing and pull. Not devastating at first. Deeply destabilizing. And if the mechanism sequence is disturbed — if one of the three people working it flinches, retreats, is incapacitated — the sealing fails. Corvin is the difference between the plan working and the plan failing.

## Action Beats

1. **Descent to the chamber.** The five of them go down. Ren leads; he's the one who knows the route. Corvin goes last, watching the corridor behind them. The temperature drops as they descend. The ozone smell is stronger. Corvin counts the steps because he will need to know them in the dark if Sela's cantrip goes down. He notes: the corridor is wide enough for two abreast but not for swinging a sword with full extension. He plans around this.

2. **The filters are further degraded.** When Ren unlocks the chamber door, the air that comes through the grille is colder than yesterday — the draw has increased since last night. Not catastrophic, but the rate is accelerating. Evren notes it first, rubbing his arm. Agda invokes her threshold invocation, quietly, standing at the door. Ren opens it. The Hollowed One is in the same position in its cage — mostly. But there is a section of the cage's outer filter lattice that has corroded through since yesterday. A gap, approximately the width of a hand.

3. **Work begins.** Evren goes to the mechanical reset panels, which are in alcoves on three walls of the chamber. He has to move close to the cage to reach the panel on the far wall. Ren is directing, reading from his documentation, calling sequence steps. Agda positions herself near the cage to perform the threshold invocation at the right moment in the sequence — Ren's documentation specified that a threshold invocation from a practitioner of the right tradition was required at step seven; no one was sure if this was literal or metaphorical until Agda read it and said it was literal. Sela is at the fused lock mechanism on the cage's anchor post. She is not yet casting. She is conserving.

4. **The Hollowed One becomes aware.** The gap in the filter lattice is wide enough that the draw through it can orient — it can sense, through the gap, the concentration of living things in the room. Corvin sees the thing's posture change: a slight inclination of the human-shaped form toward the group, the way a plant orients toward light. He says, very quietly: *It knows we're here.* Ren says: *It's always known. The filter used to make that irrelevant.* Corvin says: *And now?* Ren says, without stopping his direction of Evren: *Try to stay out of its direct line.*

5. **The draw begins.** The Hollowed One extends through the gap — not physically, not immediately, but the draw intensifies and it has direction. Evren, at the far wall panel, is closest to the cage and takes the first effect: he braces against the wall, looks gray, keeps working. Corvin positions himself between the gap and Evren and does not feel the draw diminish — he is not blocking it, but he is closer, and it takes the closer source. He is a larger target. He feels it: a cold pulling sensation at the center of his chest, not painful exactly, more like blood pressure dropping too fast. He plants his feet.

6. **The gap opens further.** The physical filter lattice, stressed by the draw's increase, breaks: the gap widens. The cage is still structurally intact but the filter is gone on that section. The Hollowed One does not move — it is still bound by the cage's frame. But the draw through the full gap is immediate and significant. Agda is in its line and goes to her knees. She does not stop the invocation. She keeps going, on her knees, eyes shut, arguing with Duhr in three-word bursts. Sela shouts: *now?* Ren says: *Step seven — Agda first, then Sela on my count.*

7. **Agda at the threshold.** Agda's invocation — the threshold-strengthening — is the most sustained piece of Duhr-practice in the book. She is performing an invocation that is usually brief, under active draw from an entity that wants vital energy. The invocation is not what Duhr draws on; it is what she draws on. She is the medium. She keeps going. She is going to finish or she is going to fall over. She finishes. Step seven complete.

8. **Sela casts the unraveling.** This is the highest-cost moment in Sela's arc so far. The unraveling on a corroded, partially-fused Vethic lock component — not a standard mechanical lock — in a room where something is actively drawing vital energy, including from her. The spell cost is additive to the draw. She knows this. She cast it anyway. The sequence: she builds the unraveling, feeds it to the lock component, the component resists (Vethic material, centuries of rust-welding), she pushes. Her nose bleeds. She pushes harder. The lock component dissolves — slowly, not the clean 1.5-second version she's used for inn doors — and the anchor post releases. The final mechanical seal engages with a sound like the building clearing its throat. Sela's vision goes black at the edges and she puts a hand on the floor.

9. **Corvin catches her.** She was standing. She is not standing. He catches her before she hits the stone. He does not say anything. He picks her up — she is seventeen and not heavy — and says: *Out. Everyone out. Now.*

10. **The sealed chamber.** The Hollowed One is in the cage, the cage is in the chamber, the chamber is sealed. The draw is still present — faint, through three layers of intact filter and sealed stone — but it is contained. Ren is the last one out. He looks at the chamber door for a long second, and then follows. He does not say anything about it, and nobody asks him to.

---

## Outcome / Turn
The Hollowed One is resealed. Sela is incapacitated. They need to get out of the waystation before anything else happens. The plan worked. The cost was real: Agda is exhausted, Evren took early draw-effects, Corvin held the line in the draw's path. Sela pushed past her reliable threshold. Everyone is alive.

## New Question / Pressure
Is the sealing permanent? Ren says: probably not. It will hold for much longer than the degraded version — years, not weeks. Long enough for them to go to someone with the authority to deal with it properly. But not permanent. The Hollowed One will be someone else's problem again, eventually.

## Stakes in This Chapter
- **Sela's magic:** She pushed past her reliable threshold. The cost is real — nosebleed, incapacitation. She is physically depleted for the rest of the day.
- **Corvin's holding action:** He cannot kill the Hollowed One. He stands between it and the party and absorbs its draw to keep the others working. This is not heroic in a speech-making way; it is tactical, competent, and costs him something.
- **Agda's invocation:** Performing under active draw on her vital reserves. She stays on her knees and finishes. This is the most sustained Duhr scene in the book.
- **Evren:** Takes the first draw-effect, braces, keeps working. Does not retreat. This is the moment that is adjacent to his hidden reason — he did not stop.

## Continuity Notes
- Sela's unraveling: cast under pressure, on a non-standard target, in a vital-energy-drain environment. The result: beyond her reliable cost threshold. Nosebleed. Brief incapacitation. She is not in serious danger — she did not go as far past threshold as she could have — but she has been genuinely hurt by the magic's cost. This is the required "magic costs the caster, always" moment at its most serious.
- The sealing will hold for years. This matters for the ending: the party's decision not to report it immediately is less catastrophic than it would be if the filters were days from failing.
- Corvin catches Sela: the chapter ends in his arms because it was the nearest functional option. He picked her up because she was down. He does not perform this. He does it.
- Ren's last look at the chamber door: he is thinking about the volunteer who is in there. Three hundred years. He made a decision to reseal it. He lives with that.

## Romance Threads in This Chapter
- **Sela/Corvin:** He catches her. He picks her up. This is not a romance scene — it is a practical response to a party member going down. But it is also, unambiguously, him catching her, and the chapter ends there. His interiority does not editorialize. He just did it.
