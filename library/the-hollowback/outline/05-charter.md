# Chapter 05 — Charter

**One-line summary:** A hill-dwarf charter holder blocks the border; the debate turns into a fight and then into something else.

**POV character:** Agda Stonebrook
**Tense:** Past
**Setting / time:** The eastern edge of open country, where the Hollowback begins. A timber gate and a low stone booth across the road — the charter holder's checkpoint. Mid-morning, Day 4.
**Target words:** ~3,500

---

## Chapter Shape
**Bottle.** One location, near real-time. The party arrives at the checkpoint, the argument unfolds, violence interrupts, Agda resolves it. This is a tightly contained scene with a single sustained arc. Agda's POV is crisp and grounded — she processes the situation clinically before she acts, and what she does is often warmer than what she says.

## Ending Mode
**Turn.** They get through the checkpoint, but at cost (minor injuries, depleted confidence). The charter holder — who had every legal right to stop them — tells them something about the waystation in parting that changes the texture of what they're walking into.

---

## Goal
The party needs passage through the charter-held stretch of the Hollowback's border. They have a contract, but the contract doesn't mention charter rights.

## Obstacle
**Grenn Vott**, a hill-dwarf who holds crown charter to this specific border stretch, is the obstacle. He is not corrupt, not foolish, and not wrong about the law. He cannot stop them from entering the Hollowback — the charter doesn't cover the whole forest — but this road crosses his holding and he can demand a toll, a full accounting, and compliance with charter restrictions. One of his restrictions: no parties with unlicensed magic users or unregistered healers. Agda has no Church certification. Sela's apprentice certification covers the road to this point; beyond this point it's a gray zone the Church hasn't enforced recently. Vott knows this.

## Action Beats

1. **Arrival at the checkpoint.** The booth is tidier than expected — someone maintains this gate. Vott steps out when he sees them coming: stocky even for a dwarf, mid-fifties, wears his charter papers in a copper case at his belt. Two employees behind him, both armed. He looks at the party for exactly long enough to read what they are, and then says: *Papers.* Agda observes him first — the way he stands, the case, the fact that the two employees are positioned for cover not aggression. He's used to people trying to bluff through. He has done this many times.

2. **The paperwork problem.** Corvin produces the Ashford contract and a travel permit. Evren produces travel papers that are technically real and slightly less technically accurate. Sela produces her apprentice certification; Vott looks at it, looks at her, and says nothing, but he folds a corner of the paper that he probably should not fold. Then he looks at Agda. Agda produces her healer's kit registration from her enclave — not a Church document. Vott hands it back without looking at it. *No Church certification. Party has an unlicensed healer.* He will require them to post a surety bond.

3. **The argument.** Corvin doesn't argue; he is calculating costs. Sela doesn't argue; she is watching Vott's face. Evren argues, at length and with considerable flair, that the road through this charter is a public right-of-way that predates the charter itself, and he has read the statute in question. (He has actually read the statute. This is unsettling to everyone, including Vott.) Vott acknowledges the argument is technically partially correct and the bond requirement stands anyway under a different statute. Agda listens to all of this and says nothing.

4. **Vott's employee moves first.** One of the employees — a younger man, edgier than the other — decides the half-elf is being disrespectful to the charter holder and shoves Evren. This was not Vott's instruction. Evren doesn't go down, but he steps back. The situation tips. Corvin's hand moves to his sword and then stops — they cannot afford a fight with a charter holder's authorized personnel, not legally. The other employee's crossbow comes up.

5. **Agda ends it before it escalates further.** She steps in front of Evren, turns to face Vott directly, and speaks in Dwarven. Not a plea — something else. She names herself by clan and by practitioner tradition. She names Duhr. She invokes an old dwarven customary law predating any Aldenmere charter: the right of a practitioner of threshold passage to walk the border roads in service of the living. It's a real legal tradition. It has no standing in Aldenmere's crown law. It has standing with Vott in a way that crown law does not always reach. There is a long pause.

6. **The fight, brief and bad.** Vott doesn't move to stop his employee fast enough — the edgy one swings at Agda, who takes a hit to the shoulder (not serious; she has been hit before). Corvin pulls the younger man off her. The crossbow fires and goes wide. Then Vott says — sharply, finally — *enough.* The employees stand down. Vott looks at Agda. He says, in Dwarven, a formal acknowledgment of her invocation. Then, in common: *Pass.*

7. **The parting word.** As the party moves through the gate, Vott speaks to Agda quietly — just Agda, not the others. He says: there's something in that waystation that's older than the current ownership argument and worse than the old maps indicate. He's had people try to go in over the years and they come back different or they don't come back at all. He does not tell her what it is because he does not know. He tells her to be careful because she is a practitioner of thresholds and what is in there does not respect thresholds properly. He says this in Dwarven. Agda says thank you.

8. **Aftermath, moving into the Hollowback.** Agda tends her own shoulder — she invokes Duhr over the bruise and the swelling goes down by half, which is not miraculous, it is the usual thing — while the others ask what Vott said. She translates it plainly: there's something wrong in the waystation. She does not tell them the full weight of how he said it. She is processing what it means that a hill-dwarf charter holder at this road junction — someone who has probably seen a hundred people go in — warned her specifically. She keeps walking.

---

## Outcome / Turn
Passage gained. Agda's authority (her tradition, her god, her identity) resolved what no amount of argument could. The charter holder's parting warning changes what the party is walking toward: not just a missing person in an empty ruin, but a ruin that is not empty in the way they'd assumed, and something in it that does not respect the line between living and dead.

## New Question / Pressure
What is in the waystation that sends people back "different"? What does it mean that a threshold-keeper's warning was delivered specifically to Agda, and not to anyone else in the party?

---

## Continuity Notes
- Agda's dwarven customary-law invocation is real and works — not because Aldenmere law recognizes it, but because Vott does. This is how worldbuilding via use works: we learn what dwarven threshold tradition is by watching it function.
- Duhr's minor intervention on Agda's shoulder: the bruise improves but does not disappear. Duhr heals at the rate of actual healing, slightly accelerated. This is not miraculous power; it is the threshold practice.
- Grenn Vott is a minor character but should feel like a person who has lived in this place for decades and knows more about the Hollowback than any of the party. He is not a villain. He had the right to stop them.
- Sela's apprentice certification: it covers the road to the checkpoint under Church rules, but the charter holder's unlicensed-healer restriction is the actual problem. This is worldbuilding via bureaucracy.
- The party has now officially entered the Hollowback.

## Romance Threads in This Chapter
- **Evren/Agda:** She steps in front of Evren. He didn't ask her to. He notices this. He doesn't say anything for a while after. When she's tending her shoulder, he is the one who holds the light — without being asked. They don't discuss it.
