## 04-aftertaste.md

Two word/clause-level fixes applied directly to draft. No unfixable issues requiring writer judgment.

**Fix 1 — Corvin dialogue voice slip (campfire scene, final section)**
Original: `"I noticed you decided not to use it," he said.`
Fixed: `"You held it back," he said.`

Reason: Corvin's documented voice is dry, short, and declarative — he states physical facts without announcing his own awareness. "I noticed you decided to X" foregrounds the noticing itself, which is a socially attentive framing that reads more Sela-register than Corvin-register. It is, implicitly, the revealing thing (I was watching you specifically) that his character does not say. Rephrased as a plain declarative about her action, which is how Corvin would process and report it.

**Fix 2 — Strained collocation in forest reflection (final scene)**
Original: `the forest had existed before it and was running, at whatever pace old forests ran things, the process of becoming what it would be after.`
Fixed: `the forest had existed before it and was, at whatever pace old forests ran things, becoming what it would be after.`

Reason: "Running the process of becoming" is an odd collocation — "run" implies deliberate system operation and lands slightly off applied to geological-timescale forest succession. Sela's precision-oriented voice benefits from the simpler, more direct construction. The parenthetical hedge is preserved; only the awkward noun phrase is removed.

## 11-the-thing-the-empire-kept.md

**Possible register creep — Ren psychological portrait (mid-chapter)**

The following clause reads closer to Sela's cataloging register than to Agda's concrete-and-gruff interiority:

> "Ren on the other side of his own notes, with the specific quality of someone who had been alone with an argument for three weeks and was finding the experience of having the other side articulated by other people both useful and disorienting."

Agda reads people through physical signals and names what she sees in short, direct terms. This clause is long and explicitly psychological — it diagrams Ren's internal experience across two subordinate clauses rather than naming a behavior or a face. Agda might land on something shorter: "with the look of a man who had been alone with a problem too long and was only now hearing the other side." But the fix requires a judgment call about how much analytical depth Agda's voice can sustain in a dense chapter. Left for the writer.

## 12-what-we-do-now.md

**Floating second quoted string — Ren's dialogue after Evren's revelation (needs author judgment)**

In the scene where Evren discloses the cartographer, Ren's response is formatted as two separate quoted strings in the same paragraph without re-attribution between them:

> Ren said: "Yes." "A volunteer. There is a signed account of the procedure, with the terms the subject was informed of in advance." He paused. [...]

After `"Yes."` the period closes the quote and the paragraph continues with a new opening quote — `"A volunteer."` — that has no tag, action beat, or attribution anchor. The intended reading is clearly Ren elaborating after a brief pause, but the formatting is ambiguous: a reader could parse it as a second speaker. Options:

1. Fold into one speech: `Ren said: "Yes — a volunteer. There is a signed account..."`
2. Add a beat between: `Ren said: "Yes." He paused. "A volunteer. There is a signed account..."`
3. Keep as-is if the double-quote gap is intentional stutter/beat, but add a minimal action anchor (e.g., `He looked at the floor.`) before `"A volunteer."` so the attribution stays with Ren.

Not a voice register issue; flagged because it cannot be resolved at the word/clause level without knowing which dramatic shape the author intends for the beat.

**Applied fixes (word/clause level):**
- Removed two `She thought:` labels that prefixed direct thought, per style guide: "Delete the label. If she saw it, she saw it; the narration doesn't need to announce the seeing." The thoughts (`Unknown. Possibly significant.` and `That is the calculation. The outcome of the calculation has not changed since the first pass.`) carry themselves as free indirect discourse without the label.

## 14-threshold.md

**Voice drift — Agda's interior thought structured in Sela's taxonomizing register**

In the scene where Evren asks Agda "Do you think she knew what she was walking into?", the interiority that follows uses a triple "Whether" construction that is Sela's voice, not Agda's:

> "Whether a person who came to a place like the Eastmark Waystation — knowing what old Vethic sites sometimes held, knowing what the reports described, knowing the specific details — whether knowing and going in anyway was different from going in without knowing. Whether the person inside the housing had known what would happen after. Whether that would have made it better or whether it would have made it worse."

Agda's documented register: names a feeling once and stops. Concrete. Does not build taxonomies or lay out nested sub-questions. This passage does what Sela's interiority does — organizes an unresolved problem into three labeled component questions. For Agda, the same interior beat would be shorter and more complete: she either has a working answer or she sits with the absence of one. The elaboration is not fixable at word/clause level without restructuring the paragraph; needs writer judgment about how Agda's version of this particular not-knowing would actually land.
