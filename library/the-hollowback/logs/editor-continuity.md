# Editor — Continuity Pass

Notes from per-chapter review. Chapters with no entry below had no continuity issues.

## 12-what-we-do-now

- **Issue:** Evren explicitly discloses his personal connection to the missing cartographer — the hidden reason he took the job — to the assembled party.
- **Conflict:** Canon fact #10 (canon/continuity.md) and the matching entry in logs/continuity.md both state: "He does not disclose this during the book." Chapter 12 has Evren say aloud: *"There was a cartographer who went into a station like this one. Three years ago. She didn't come back. I want to know what happened to her."* This is a complete, deliberate disclosure, not an aborted hint. The scene is structurally built on it: the room's reaction, Ren's careful response about the archive documentation, and Sela's full observational paragraph framing the moment as "a fact you had been keeping in a sealed space for a very long time and had just decided to unlock."
- **Suggested resolution:** Replace Evren's disclosed personal motive with a position-statement that is equally character-consistent but does not name the cartographer or his connection. For example, he could argue against resealing on the grounds of the archived knowledge itself — that closing the door without understanding what the documentation contains is destroying the only record of what happened to the subject — which expresses the same emotional investment without making the personal reason audible. The Ren-to-Evren beat (Ren describing the subject's archive record, Evren's quiet nod) could be preserved as a private, unspoken resonance rather than a disclosed answer to an explicit question.

## 13-the-hollowed

- **Issue:** The operational sequence for resealing the containment mechanism is reversed from the plan established in Chapter 12 — with no in-story explanation for the change.
- **Conflict:** Chapter 12 states the plan explicitly and gives a technical rationale for why the sequence is fixed. Sela says: *"First node, third node, second node. The fused one is first."* Ren explains: *"The design logic requires the third node to establish the configuration before the channels can run correctly. If you engage the second node first, you lock an incomplete configuration. The sequence is non-negotiable."* Sela's summary confirms: *"Evren and Ren work the two operable nodes — first the configuration node, then the second node in sequence **after I've addressed the fused one**. I take the third node with the unraveling. Agda's invocation as a stabilizer on the channel configuration **once it's established**."* In Chapter 13, the execution is the inverse: Evren works both operable nodes first (steps 3–6), Agda invokes at step 7, and Sela addresses the fused node last — after step 7. Ren even says mid-scene *"It changes the exposure window. Not the sequence"* when the filter gap is found larger, which makes the actual sequence inversion more glaring. Additionally, Chapter 12 assigns **both Evren and Ren** to the two operable nodes; in Chapter 13, Evren works both nodes alone while Ren calls the count — the "right" panel of the three-panel array disappears from the execution entirely.
- **Suggested resolution:** Two options. (A) Revise the plan section of Chapter 12 so the stated sequence matches Chapter 13's execution — Evren on both operable nodes first, Agda's invocation second, Sela on the fused node last — and adjust Ren's technical rationale accordingly (the fused node must seal last, as the lock that holds the newly established configuration). Remove Ren's role at a node and reassign him to count-calling. (B) Revise Chapter 13's action sequence to match Chapter 12's stated plan: Sela on the fused node first while Evren and Ren hold their positions, then Evren and Ren engaging the operable nodes in the documented sequence, then Agda's stabilizing invocation — with Corvin holding the draw throughout. Option A requires fewer structural changes and better fits the arc summary for Chapter 13 (*"Agda finishes the invocation on her knees; Sela pushes past threshold and goes down"*), which implies Sela's expenditure follows Agda's invocation.

## 16-thorngate

- **Issue:** Chapter 16 opens as Sela's first return through the east gate in daylight (Day 12, mid-morning), but Chapter 15 already shows the whole party entering through that same gate at night on Day 11.
- **Conflict:** Ch15 ends unambiguously inside Thorngate: *"The east gate. The night watch waved them through … The trade district. Founder's Street."* Corvin's interior confirms they are spending the night in the city: *"Tonight the contract was still open … tomorrow morning they would walk to [Maret's office]."* Ch16 then opens with Sela apparently arriving through the east gate for the first time — *"Day 12. Mid-morning. The east gate smelled like rope and bread … over eleven days of not being here … It arrived correctly"* — using the sensory and emotional register of a fresh return passage, which contradicts the prior-night entry.
- **Suggested resolution:** Revise the Ch16 opening so the setting is clearly inside the city, not at the gate threshold. The "calibration returned to familiar conditions" beat could be anchored to a different landmark visible from inside (the trade-district square, Founder's Street, the bakery smell, the fish market — all of which are already in the chapter). Alternatively, collapse the Ch15 night entry into a briefer note and give the full arrival beat to Ch16 morning — but that requires altering Ch15, which is the previous chapter and outside this invocation's scope.

- **Issue:** Sela is described as having *"Thirty-seven marks in her left coat pocket"* at the chapter's close, implying she holds the full contract value.
- **Conflict:** Canon (canon/continuity.md, fact 2; logs/continuity.md) states the Ashford contract pays 22 marks base + 15-mark bonus = 37 marks as the total contract fee. The contract is a single group agreement covering all four party members. If 37 marks is the total, Sela's share would be ~9 marks, not 37. The chapter offers no scene in which the payment is split or in which Sela is designated the group's treasurer.
- **Suggested resolution:** Either (a) add a brief beat between the payment scene and the Copper Lane dispersal showing the four-way split, so Sela's pocketed share is explicitly a quarter of the total; or (b) revise the closing line to reflect her actual share ("nine marks" or "her share of the thirty-seven"); or (c) confirm in canon that the contract pays 37 marks *per person*, which would require updating the canon note and verifying Maret Ashford's finances are plausible at 148 marks total.
