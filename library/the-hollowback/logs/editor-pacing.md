# Editor — Pacing Pass
## Macro arc assessment

### Overall shape

The book's five-phase structure (Setup → Descent → Heart → Crisis → Coda) is clean and well-sequenced. The first third (Ch 1–5) earns its ground: the contract, the party's awkward formation, and the ambush all raise external pressure in legible increments, with Ch 3 providing a proper action spike and Ch 4 a functional cool-down. The final third (Ch 11–16) is the book's strongest zone: revelation → argument → boss fight → escape is a crisp escalation with no slack, and Thread 4 (Sela's magic limit) resolves at exactly the right moment inside Ch 13. The arc's bones are sound.

---

### Midbook sag: Ch 6–7 is the specific problem

The Descent phase (Ch 5–9) should function as a gathering-dread corridor — the party moves deeper, the waystation mystery tightens, the reader's footing becomes less certain. Instead, Ch 6 and Ch 7 form two consecutive low-pressure chapters back to back:

- **Ch 6 (What the Empire Paved)** — interiority-heavy, 3,000 words, ends unresolved. Earns its place as the book's required awe moment, but its pressure contribution is atmospheric, not escalating.
- **Ch 7 (Watch)** — bottle scene, 2,500 words, ends on a "turn" that is internal rather than external. The Evren/Agda beat and Lira's name are genuinely valuable, but the chapter operates at the same temperature as Ch 6.

Two consecutive unresolved, contemplative chapters in the middle of an active descent is the book's clearest pacing debt. The charter fight's energy (Ch 5) dissipates across both chapters before Ch 8 restores momentum with the antechamber. A reader who trusts the book's banter-and-stakes contract may feel the ground go soft underfoot during the Ch 6–7 stretch. The fix is not structural restructuring — it is ensuring that one of the two chapters carries a sharper external pressure hook (something observed, heard, or discovered that makes the next chapter feel *necessary* rather than *welcome*).

---

### Ch 9 potentially underweight

The story-arc one-liner for Ch 9 ("Evren leads the party through garrison and storage rooms, finds Ren's workspace, reads maps fluently for the third time, learns something is in the containment chamber") describes a connective-tissue chapter more than an encounter chapter. The thread tracker confirms it: Ch 9 advances only T2 and T3 — and both advances are incremental rather than a step-change. The Vethic notation fluency also appears in Ch 7 and Ch 8 before this third instance, risking a "yes, we know, get on with it" quality by the time it's "no longer deniable." This chapter warrants scrutiny in the per-chapter pass to confirm it has its own pressure spine, not just corridor-clearing.

---

### Thread 1 (Corvin's discharge) goes dormant for seven chapters

T1 enters in Ch 3, takes one follow-up beat in Ch 4, then disappears until Ch 11 — a gap of seven chapters, nearly half the book's length. This is the most charged character secret and the party's most loaded silence, and its dormancy from Ch 4 through Ch 10 is notable. This may be intentional (Corvin's method is silence; the thread's absence is mimetic), but in a 16-chapter book, seven chapters without a single T1 touch risks readers losing the thread's tension. The per-chapter pass on Ch 8 or Ch 9 should confirm whether a subterranean T1 beat exists that the thread tracker hasn't logged, or whether a small pressure reminder could be inserted without forcing Corvin's hand.

---

### Repeated beats: Ch 4 and Ch 15 share a register

Both Ch 4 (Aftertaste) and Ch 15 (The Road Tells You Things) are quiet aftermath chapters in which relationships are processed and things go unsaid, both ending unresolved. They serve different functions — Ch 4 is post-ambush; Ch 15 is post-climax homecoming — and eleven chapters separate them. But they are the book's two "processing in transit" chapters and land in the same emotional key: introspective, low external pressure, relationship-forward. This is unlikely to read as a problem given their placement, but the per-chapter pass on Ch 15 should confirm it contains forward movement (decisions made, knowledge landed, something changed) rather than simply recapitulating the post-Ch 13 warmth that Ch 14 has already delivered.

---

### Coda balance: slightly long

Ch 15 + Ch 16 total ~6,500 words — about 11.5% of the target book length sitting after the Crisis is closed. The brief's structural requirements (the party must be fond of each other; none of the romance subplots resolve; the loose thread is confirmed) legitimately require two chapters. The concern is that both chapters end with the "unresolved" or "coda" mode, which means the book's final impression is of sustained deferral. This is the intent, and the brief supports it. The pacing risk is drift rather than landing. Ch 16 must feel like an ending even as it declines all resolutions — it needs at least one concrete, forward-pointing gesture (the knowledge held in two copies; Corvin waiting outside Maret's office; Sela walking home) that gives the reader a settled resting place even without closure.

## 10-found.md

**Overall:** Clean. The chapter does its two jobs — completing the contract's nominal arc (Ren found alive) and delivering the T3 reveal (the Hollowed One first seen) — without fat. The Ren introduction is precise and economical. The Corvin/Ren contract argument is crisp. The "What something?" pivot is well-timed. The Sela/Ren T5 beat lands without forcing. The chamber reveal is properly built and properly paid off. The final pull-back paragraph (archive → garrison → forest → Thorngate → Maret's office) is the right close for a chapter that ends the contract's simplicity.

**One low-severity pacing note: the ozone-in-stone paragraph in the descent sequence.**

The descent to the containment chamber lands three sequential interiority beats, all confirming the same underlying fact — the magic here has been running continuously for a very long time:

1. The gradient pull ("A gradient. The way a current ran in still water: there, directional, and not ceasing.")
2. The headache plateau ("It did not worsen. It simply plateaued, as if it had found the correct elevation and decided to stay.")
3. The ozone-in-stone paragraph ("Not spreading through the air from a distance. Present in the stone… As if it had been here for a very long time and was not going anywhere.")

Beats 1 and 2 are both necessary — the gradient establishes the draw, the headache makes it physical and personal. Beat 3 is attempting to carry a different data point (the magic has saturated the architecture, not just the air), but this specific detail is then superseded almost immediately by the through-the-door beat, which delivers architectural-saturation-plus-duration with far greater impact: *"the kind of smell that meant sustained and unceasing and — three hundred years."* That dash is the chapter's best moment; the pre-door ozone paragraph lands first and partially spends it.

**Suggested action (polish pass):** Either cut the pre-door ozone paragraph entirely (the through-the-door beat is sufficient and stronger), or rewrite it to make its unique claim — that the magic is *in the stone*, not just the air — feel categorically distinct from the door-reveal, so the two beats are doing different work rather than staging the same discovery twice. The paragraph as written softens the impact of the chapter's strongest sentence.

No structural changes required. No rewrite warranted. This is a single-paragraph trim or sharpening.

**Small fix applied:** The second cost-calculation pass (three paragraphs immediately following "He said: 'All right.'") was cut. The original text ran the calculation again in full detail — enumerating compounding costs — then concluded "The answer was the same as it had been," which was self-confirming and added no new information beyond what the first pass had already established. The cut brings "All right" into direct contact with the node-sequence work — which is both tighter pacing and characterologically accurate for Sela: she committed; she's already on to the problem. Nothing was rewritten or restructured; three paragraphs were removed.

**Remaining note (polish pass):** In the closing section, "She had run this calculation every way she knew how, and the answer was the same every time" is the more mechanical of the two cost-calculation sentences in that paragraph. The sentence that follows it — "She had said yes at the table before she had fully thought through the cost, and then she had fully thought through the cost, and the answer was the same" — is doing the actual emotional work (tracking her psychological arc: impulsive yes → full accounting → still yes) and is the stronger of the two. The first sentence could be cut without loss, leaving the second to carry the weight alongside the secondary-records beat. This is a one-sentence trim, flagged rather than applied because it is the third instance of this calculation in the chapter (now reduced from the original four) and a judgment call about whether the exhaustiveness claim adds anything the second sentence doesn't already deliver. Either version is defensible; the trimmed version is marginally tighter.

## 14-threshold.md

**Overall:** Clean. The chapter earns its place in the book's strongest escalation zone. The pressure-plate re-traverse is tight and thread-active (Evren correcting Ren's exit-direction map-reading is the most live demonstration of T2's Vethic-literacy strand in the book). The *halveth* moment at the doorway is precisely placed and delivers T3's spiritual close without ceremony. The Evren/Agda scene — built to slowly via Agda's deliberate postponement of it through the care-work — lands as the chapter's right emotional center, and her exit move ("she went back to check Sela's color") is the correct register and weight for the pairing.

**One small-fix note: the bandaged hand paragraph is out of position.**

The paragraph beginning "She looked at her left hand" arrives after the chapter's emotional resolution (Evren's arms uncrossed, Tomorrow logistics settled) and before the closing Hollowback image. It opens new interiority — Agda's grandmother's teaching on Duhr-marks, the uncanny quality of not knowing when or where she bled — that is not picked up within the chapter or in the arc. Agda's cost is already communicated three times before this point: "depleted to the marrow" in the opening inventory, Duhr's dry one-word *Continue*, and the sustained twelve-minute healing work on Sela. The bandaged hand is a fourth cost signal, but it also introduces a mildly mystical register (the boundary between practitioner and work made imprecise, marks left in places she can't account for) that the rest of the chapter's grounded pragmatism hasn't prepared for.

As placed, the paragraph creates a tonal restart just as the chapter is finding its landing note — a small dispersal of the reader's arrival at "She would check Sela's color one more time before dark. Then she would sleep." The closing image is exactly right; the bandaged hand paragraph delays the reader's access to it while opening an interiority thread that doesn't close.

**Suggested action (polish pass):** Either cut the bandaged hand paragraph entirely (Agda's cost is already established; the closing image can follow directly from the dried fruit beat), or move it into the opening garrison-stair inventory section, where it reads as part of the status-check Agda runs on herself after the emergency — a discovered-in-passing detail rather than a post-resolution meditation. Either version tightens the chapter's close. Cutting is marginally cleaner given that the grandmother's teaching opens a note the arc doesn't develop.

No structural changes required. No rewrite warranted.
